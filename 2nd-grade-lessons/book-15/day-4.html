📚 A Second Deception

The villagers were understandably annoyed and disappointed by Marcus's thoughtless prank, but they chose to respond with patience and understanding, recognizing that he was still young and might not fully comprehend the seriousness of his actions. They gave him a stern but gentle warning about the importance of honesty and the dangers of crying false alarms, hoping that this single incident would serve as a valuable learning experience for the immature shepherd.

However, instead of feeling remorseful about his deceptive behavior, Marcus found himself energized and excited by the dramatic response his false alarm had generated. The memory of seeing the entire village mobilize so quickly and efficiently filled him with a sense of power and importance that he had never experienced before during his solitary hours on the mountainside with only sheep for company.

Several days later, when his boredom returned and the temporary excitement had faded from memory, Marcus decided to repeat his successful experiment with an even louder and more convincing performance. "Wolf! Wolf!" he screamed with theatrical desperation, "A huge wolf is attacking the sheep! Please help me immediately! I cannot fight him alone!" His cries echoed across the valley with remarkable authenticity and urgency.

Once again, the dedicated villagers responded to his call with the same swift efficiency and selfless concern they had demonstrated before, though some noticed that his previous false alarm had planted seeds of doubt in their minds. Despite their growing skepticism, they still felt obligated to respond to what might be a genuine emergency, understanding that the consequences of ignoring a real wolf attack would be catastrophic for their entire community.

📝 Language Lesson

Rhyming Words

Goal: Students will identify and create rhyming words.

Rhyming words sound the SAME at the end. They make poems and stories fun to read aloud!

  • Rhyming Words:
  • cat, hat, mat, sat (all end with -at)
  • play, day, say, way (all end with -ay)
  • book, look, cook, took (all end with -ook)

From today's story: Listen for words that rhyme as you read!

Practice - Find the rhyme: 1. Which word rhymes with "dog"? (log, big, cat) 2. Which word rhymes with "jump"? (run, bump, sit) 3. Which word rhymes with "bed"? (chair, red, sleep)

Remember: Listen to the END of words to find rhymes!
