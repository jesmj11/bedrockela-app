📚 Moving Day

The children were so excited about their new home! They had never seen anything like a shoe house before. They packed up all their toys, clothes, and belongings as fast as they could.

When they arrived at the giant shoe, the children could not believe their eyes. "We're going to live in a shoe?" they laughed. "This is the best house ever!" they cheered.

They ran inside to explore their new home. There was so much space! The children could run around without bumping into each other. Each child could have their own special corner.

The old woman smiled as she watched her children explore. For the first time in a long while, she felt happy and peaceful. Her family finally had room to grow and play.

Ocean

The ocean covers more than 70% of Earth's surface and contains about 97% of all the water on our planet. Ocean water is salty because it has dissolved minerals that have washed into it from rocks and soil over millions of years. The ocean is divided into five main parts: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Arctic, and Southern oceans, but they are all connected as one giant body of water. The ocean is home to millions of different plants and animals, from tiny plankton to massive blue whales. It provides food for billions of people around the world and helps control Earth's weather and temperature. Ocean currents act like rivers in the sea, carrying warm and cold water around the planet. The ocean also gives us oxygen to breathe – tiny ocean plants called phytoplankton produce much of the oxygen in our atmosphere.
