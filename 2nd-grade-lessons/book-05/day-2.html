📚 The Giant Shoe

The old woman walked closer to the giant shoe. It was made of brown leather and had long laces just like a regular shoe. But it was enormous! It had windows and a door cut into it.

She knocked on the door. No one answered. She peeked through the windows. The shoe was empty inside! It had been turned into a house, but no one was living in it.

"This is perfect!" thought the old woman. "There's plenty of room for all of us here!" The shoe had a big main room, a kitchen, and even an upstairs area.

The old woman hurried home to tell her children the good news. "Pack up your things!" she called out. "We're moving to a new home! And wait until you see where we're going to live!"

Rocks

Rocks are solid materials made up of minerals that form naturally in the Earth. There are three main types of rocks: igneous rocks form when melted rock cools down, sedimentary rocks form from layers of sand and other materials pressed together over time, and metamorphic rocks form when existing rocks are changed by heat and pressure deep underground. Rocks are useful to people in many ways. We use granite and marble for countertops and buildings, limestone to make cement, and coal for energy. Some rocks contain valuable metals like gold and silver. Geologists are scientists who study rocks to learn about Earth's history and find useful materials. By examining different rock layers, they can tell what the Earth was like millions of years ago.
