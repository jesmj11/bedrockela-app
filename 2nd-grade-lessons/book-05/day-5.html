📚 Visitors and Friends

Word spread quickly through the town about the family who lived in a shoe. People were curious and came to see this unusual house. At first, some visitors just stared and pointed.

But when people met the old woman and her children, they saw how kind and friendly the family was. The children were polite and well-behaved. They welcomed visitors with warm smiles.

Soon the neighbors began bringing gifts. Someone brought fresh bread. Another person brought vegetables from their garden. A kind baker brought cookies for all the children.

The old woman was touched by everyone's kindness. "Thank you all," she said. "We may live in a shoe, but we have the best neighbors in the world!" Everyone laughed and agreed.

📰 Learn Something New!

Imagination

Bicycles

Bicycles are vehicles with two wheels that people power by pedaling with their feet. The pedals turn a chain that makes the back wheel spin, moving the bicycle forward. Bicycles have handlebars for steering and brakes to slow down or stop. They are an efficient way to travel because they don't need gasoline or electricity – just human power! Riding bicycles is good exercise that helps keep people healthy and strong. Bicycles are also good for the environment because they don't produce pollution like cars do. Many cities have bike lanes and paths to make cycling safer and more enjoyable. Some people use bicycles to get to work or school, while others ride them for fun or sport. There are many different types of bicycles designed for different activities, like mountain biking or racing.
