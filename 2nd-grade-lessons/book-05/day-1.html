📚 A Very Big Family

Once upon a time, there lived an old woman who had so many children she didn't know what to do! She had twelve little ones, and they were always busy and loud.

The problem was that their house was much too small. It was so tiny that the children could barely fit inside. They were always bumping into each other and getting in the way.

The old woman loved her children very much, but she was tired and worried. How could she find a bigger place for her big family? Where could they all live together?

One day, while walking through the town, the old woman saw something very strange. There, in an empty field, sat the biggest shoe she had ever seen! It was as big as a house!

🤔 Reading Comprehension

1. How many children did the old woman have?

Plants

Plants are living things that make their own food using sunlight, water, and air. This process is called photosynthesis, and it happens in the green parts of plants, especially the leaves. Plants are very important because they produce the oxygen that we breathe and provide food for animals and humans. Without plants, there would be no life on Earth! There are many different types of plants, from tiny grass to giant redwood trees. Some plants, like roses and daisies, grow beautiful flowers to attract insects that help them make seeds. Other plants, like pine trees, make cones instead of flowers. Plants can grow almost anywhere – in deserts, underwater, on mountains, and even in the cracks of sidewalks. They are amazing at adapting to different environments.
