📚 Little Engine Volunteers

The toys were feeling very sad. Two big, strong engines had said no. How would they ever get to the children on the other side of the mountain?

Then they heard a little voice. "Maybe I can help you," said a small blue engine. She was much smaller than the other engines. She had never pulled a train over a mountain before.

"You are very small," said the toy clown. "Do you think you are strong enough?" The Little Blue Engine looked at the mountain. It was very high and steep.

"I have never done it before," said the Little Blue Engine. "But I will try! The children need their toys and food. I think I can do it!"

Rain

Rain is water that falls from clouds in the sky. It starts when the sun heats up water in oceans, rivers, and lakes, turning it into invisible water vapor that rises into the air. High up in the sky, the water vapor cools down and forms tiny water droplets that stick to dust particles in the air. These tiny droplets join together to make bigger droplets inside clouds. When the droplets get heavy enough, they fall down as rain. Rain is very important because it gives plants the water they need to grow. It also fills up rivers, lakes, and underground water sources that people and animals need for drinking.
