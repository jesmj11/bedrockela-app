📚 Adding to the Soup

The traveler dropped his stone into the pot of boiling water. He stirred it slowly and tasted it with a spoon. "Mmm," he said. "This stone soup is good, but it would taste even better with just a few carrots."

"I have some carrots!" said one villager. She ran to her house and came back with an armful of orange carrots. The traveler thanked her and added them to the pot.

After a few more minutes, the traveler tasted the soup again. "This is getting better," he said. "But you know what would make it perfect? Some potatoes and onions."

"I have potatoes!" called out another villager. "And I have onions!" said his neighbor. Soon the pot was bubbling with carrots, potatoes, and onions. The soup smelled wonderful!

Snow

Snow forms when water vapor in clouds freezes into tiny ice crystals instead of turning into raindrops. This happens when the temperature in the clouds is below freezing. The ice crystals stick together as they fall through the cold air, creating snowflakes. Each snowflake has a unique shape with six sides, and no two snowflakes are exactly alike! Snow is important for many reasons. It provides water for plants and animals when it melts in the spring. Many places depend on melted snow for their drinking water and to water crops. Snow also helps keep the ground warm in winter by acting like a blanket over plants and small animals. Some animals, like polar bears and penguins, need snow and ice to survive in their natural habitats.
