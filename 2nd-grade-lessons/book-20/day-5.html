📚 Tea Time and Stories

Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle invited Lucie to sit down for a cup of tea and some delicious homemade shortbread cookies. As they sat together in the cozy laundry kitchen, Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle told Lucie fascinating stories about all the different woodland creatures whose clothes she washed regularly throughout each busy week.

"The animals are always getting into such adventures!" chuckled Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle as she poured steaming tea into delicate china cups. "Poor Tom Kitten split his jacket completely in half last week, and Benjamin Bunny brought me a whole basketful of clothes covered in onion juice from Mr. McGregor's garden!"

Lucie listened with growing wonder to these enchanting tales of the secret lives of woodland animals. She had never imagined that the creatures living around her farm had such exciting adventures, or that they wore proper clothes that needed regular washing and mending just like her own garments.

As the afternoon grew later, Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle carefully folded Lucie's clean laundry and placed everything in a neat bundle. "You must take these home to your dear mother," she said kindly. "And remember, whenever you lose your handkerchiefs again, you know exactly where to find me for help!" Lucie thanked her warmly for the wonderful hospitality.

📰 Learn Something New!

Kindness

Kindness

Kindness means being friendly, caring, and helpful to others. When you show kindness, you make other people feel happy and valued. Simple acts of kindness include sharing your toys, helping someone who fell down, or saying nice things to make someone smile. Being kind doesn't cost anything, but it can make a big difference in someone's day.

Practicing kindness helps create a better world for everyone. When you are kind to others, they often want to be kind too, creating a chain reaction of good deeds. Teachers, parents, and friends appreciate kindness. You can show kindness to people, animals, and even yourself. Remember: treat others the way you want to be treated.
