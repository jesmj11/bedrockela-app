📚 Unwelcome Visitors

One morning as the farmer was inspecting his nearly ready grain fields, he made a disturbing discovery that filled him with anger and frustration. A large flock of cranes had descended upon his crops during the night and were systematically eating the grain he had worked so hard to grow. These greedy birds were consuming his valuable harvest as if it were their own personal feast.

The cranes showed no respect for the farmer's property or labor. They moved through his fields in large groups, pecking at the ripened grain heads and scattering seeds carelessly on the ground. What they didn't eat, they often knocked loose from the stalks, wasting precious grain that represented months of careful cultivation and significant financial investment.

Day after day, the farmer watched helplessly as these destructive birds returned to continue their assault on his crops. They seemed to have no fear of him and appeared to consider his fields their own private feeding ground. Their daily raids were rapidly diminishing his expected harvest and threatening to turn his profitable season into a financial disaster.

The farmer realized that if he didn't take decisive action soon, these persistent thieves would destroy everything he had worked for throughout the growing season. He had tried shouting and waving his arms to scare them away, but the cranes only flew to other parts of his field and continued their destructive feeding. Clearly, more serious measures would be necessary to protect his livelihood.
