📚 Setting the Trap

After careful consideration of his options, the farmer decided that he would have to use more drastic measures to protect his remaining crops from the persistent cranes. He spent an entire evening preparing a large net that could be spread across a section of his field, creating a trap that would capture the greedy birds when they came to feed on his grain the following morning.

The farmer worked by lamplight, carefully checking every knot and connection in his net to ensure that it would function properly when the time came to use it. He planned to hide in the nearby woods and wait for the cranes to settle into their usual feeding pattern, then spring the trap when they were too busy eating to notice the danger until it was too late to escape.

Early the next morning, just as the sun was beginning to rise above the eastern hills, the farmer positioned his trap in the area where the cranes usually began their daily feast. He scattered some extra grain around the net to make the spot even more attractive to the hungry birds, then retreated to his hiding place to wait for his unwelcome visitors to arrive.

The farmer didn't have to wait long before he heard the familiar sound of wings beating overhead and the harsh cries of the cranes as they approached his field. Right on schedule, the flock descended toward the grain-rich area where he had set his carefully prepared trap, completely unaware that their routine morning feeding was about to be dramatically interrupted by the consequences of their destructive behavior.
