📚 Justice Without Mercy

When the farmer saw that his trap had successfully captured the grain-stealing cranes, he felt a surge of satisfaction at finally being able to protect his hard-earned crops from further destruction. However, as he approached the struggling birds caught in his net, he discovered that his trap had also snared the innocent stork who had simply been in the wrong place at the wrong time.

The stork looked up at the farmer with frightened eyes and tried to explain her situation. "Please, kind farmer," she said in her gentle voice, "I am not like these greedy cranes who have been stealing your grain. I was only searching for insects and small creatures among your crops. I have never taken so much as a single seed from your field, and I would never dream of causing you any harm or loss."

The farmer listened to the stork's plea, but his anger over the damage to his crops had made him hard and unforgiving. All he could see was that this bird had been found among the thieves who had been destroying his livelihood, and that was enough to condemn her in his mind. He had suffered significant losses because of grain-eating birds, and he was in no mood to make careful distinctions between the guilty and the innocent.

"I don't care what your intentions were," the farmer replied coldly. "You were found among the thieves, and that is enough for me. A bird is known by the company it keeps, and if you choose to associate with grain-stealers, then you must be prepared to share their fate. I cannot and will not make exceptions, no matter how convincing your story might sound."
