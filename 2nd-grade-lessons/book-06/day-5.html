📚 Jack Jumps the Candlestick

With all his speed, Jack reached the candlestick and leaped high into the air! Up, up, up he went, flying over the bright flame. For a moment, he seemed to hang in the air like a bird.

The crowd watched in amazement as Jack soared over the candlestick. His jump was perfect! He cleared the flame completely and landed safely on the other side with a soft thud.

"He did it! He did it!" everyone cheered. Jack had jumped over the candlestick! All his practice and hard work had paid off. He had achieved his biggest dream.

Jack stood up and brushed off his clothes. He was grinning from ear to ear. The villagers clapped and cheered for their nimble, quick friend who had done the impossible.

Recycling

Recycling is the process of collecting used materials and turning them into new products instead of throwing them away as trash. Common materials that can be recycled include paper, plastic bottles, aluminum cans, and glass jars. When we recycle these materials, they are cleaned, broken down, and made into new items, which helps reduce the amount of waste in landfills and conserves natural resources. Recycling is important for protecting our environment because it reduces pollution and saves energy. Making new products from recycled materials uses less energy than making them from brand new materials. For example, making paper from recycled paper uses much less water and energy than making paper from trees. Everyone can help by separating recyclable materials from regular trash and putting them in special recycling bins.
