📚 The Big Moment

As the sun began to set, the whole village gathered around the town square. Everyone wanted to see if Jack could really jump over the tall candlestick. Even the grown-ups came to watch.

The candlestick was lit, and its bright flame flickered in the evening breeze. Jack took a deep breath and stepped back to get a running start. His heart was beating fast with excitement.

"Jack be nimble, Jack be quick!" called out his friends. Jack smiled and nodded. He had been nimble and quick all his life. Now it was time to prove it in the biggest way possible.

Jack began to run toward the candlestick. Faster and faster he went, building up speed with each step. The crowd held their breath as Jack got closer and closer to the tall flame.

📝 Language Lesson

Rhyming Words

Goal: Students will identify and create rhyming words.

Rhyming words sound the SAME at the end. They make poems and stories fun to read aloud!

  • Rhyming Words:
  • cat, hat, mat, sat (all end with -at)
  • play, day, say, way (all end with -ay)
  • book, look, cook, took (all end with -ook)

From today's story: Listen for words that rhyme as you read!

Practice - Find the rhyme: 1. Which word rhymes with "dog"? (log, big, cat) 2. Which word rhymes with "jump"? (run, bump, sit) 3. Which word rhymes with "bed"? (chair, red, sleep)

Remember: Listen to the END of words to find rhymes!

Being quick

Music

Music is organized sounds that create rhythms, melodies, and harmonies that people enjoy listening to and creating. Music can be made with instruments like pianos, guitars, drums, and violins, or with the human voice through singing. Different cultures around the world have their own unique styles of music, and people have been making and enjoying music for thousands of years. Learning music and playing instruments has many benefits for children. It helps develop listening skills, improves memory and concentration, and teaches patience and discipline through practice. Music also helps people express their emotions and creativity. Many schools have music programs where students can learn to play instruments, sing in choirs, or compose their own songs. Playing music with others teaches teamwork and cooperation.
