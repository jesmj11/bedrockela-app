📚 The Big Candlestick

In the center of the village stood a very tall candlestick. It was made of shiny brass and was as tall as a grown-up person. Every evening, the villagers would light its big candle.

The candlestick was much taller than anything Jack had ever jumped over before. When he looked up at it, it seemed to reach toward the sky. The flame on top danced in the wind.

Jack's friends shook their heads. "That's too high, Jack," they said. "You'll never be able to jump over that!" But Jack was determined. He believed he could do it if he practiced enough.

Jack studied the candlestick carefully. He walked around it many times, looking at it from every angle. He needed to figure out the best way to approach his biggest jump yet.

Sleep

Sleep is a natural state of rest that our bodies and brains need every day to stay healthy and function properly. During sleep, our bodies repair themselves, grow new cells, and strengthen our immune system to fight off illnesses. Our brains use sleep time to organize memories from the day and prepare for learning new things tomorrow. Children need more sleep than adults because their bodies and brains are still growing and developing. Most school-age children need about 9-11 hours of sleep each night to feel rested and alert during the day. Good sleep helps us concentrate better in school, have more energy for playing, and feel happier overall. Creating a bedtime routine and keeping a regular sleep schedule helps ensure we get the rest we need.
