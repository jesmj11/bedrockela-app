📚 The Perfect Nesting Place

The charming gentleman led Jemima deeper into the forest along winding paths that seemed to grow narrower and more secluded with each step, until they arrived at what appeared to be an ideally situated woodland shed tucked away in a private clearing surrounded by tall trees and dense undergrowth. "Here, my dear lady, is the perfect sanctuary for a sophisticated mother such as yourself," he announced with theatrical flourish, gesturing grandly toward the rustic building that seemed to offer exactly the kind of privacy and security that Jemima had been seeking.

The shed's interior had been prepared with considerable attention to comfort and convenience, featuring soft hay arranged in an inviting nesting area, fresh water available in a clean basin, and various amenities that suggested someone had invested significant time and effort in creating an attractive environment for visiting waterfowl. Jemima was impressed by the thoughtful arrangements and touched by what she interpreted as evidence of the gentleman's genuine concern for her welfare and maternal success.

"This is absolutely perfect!" exclaimed Jemima with undisguised delight and gratitude as she explored the cozy accommodations that seemed designed specifically to meet her needs and preferences. "You are truly a gentleman of exceptional kindness and understanding to provide such wonderful facilities for a stranger. I cannot adequately express my appreciation for your generosity in offering me this marvelous opportunity to achieve my maternal dreams in such comfortable and secure surroundings."

The mysterious benefactor accepted her effusive praise with modest dignity, waving away her thanks with elegant gestures while assuring her that he was simply delighted to assist a lady of her obvious quality and refinement. "Think nothing of it, dear madam," he replied with courtly grace. "It brings me great pleasure to help someone who possesses such admirable determination and intelligence. Please make yourself completely at home, and feel free to begin your nesting activities whenever you feel ready to proceed with your important maternal project."

📝 Language Lesson

Rhyming Words

Goal: Students will identify and create rhyming words.

Rhyming words sound the SAME at the end. They make poems and stories fun to read aloud!

  • Rhyming Words:
  • cat, hat, mat, sat (all end with -at)
  • play, day, say, way (all end with -ay)
  • book, look, cook, took (all end with -ook)

From today's story: Listen for words that rhyme as you read!

Practice - Find the rhyme: 1. Which word rhymes with "dog"? (log, big, cat) 2. Which word rhymes with "jump"? (run, bump, sit) 3. Which word rhymes with "bed"? (chair, red, sleep)

Remember: Listen to the END of words to find rhymes!
