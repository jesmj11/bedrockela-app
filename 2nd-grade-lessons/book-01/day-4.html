📚 Good News for Mother

The three little kittens were so excited! They had found their lost mittens. Now Mother Cat would not be angry anymore. And maybe she would give them some food!

They ran as fast as they could back to the house. "Mother! Mother!" they called as they burst through the door. Mother Cat looked up from her knitting.

The kittens held up their mittens proudly. "Look! We found our mittens!" said Mittsy. "All of them!" added Bitsy. "They were by the garden gate!" explained Ditsy.

Mother Cat smiled for the first time that day. "What! You found your mittens? You good little kittens! Now you shall have some pie!" The kittens cheered with joy.

📝 Language Lesson

Rhyming Words

Goal: Students will identify and create rhyming words.

Rhyming words sound the SAME at the end. They make poems and stories fun to read aloud!

  • Rhyming Words:
  • cat, hat, mat, sat (all end with -at)
  • play, day, say, way (all end with -ay)
  • book, look, cook, took (all end with -ook)

From today's story: Listen for words that rhyme as you read!

Practice - Find the rhyme: 1. Which word rhymes with "dog"? (log, big, cat) 2. Which word rhymes with "jump"? (run, bump, sit) 3. Which word rhymes with "bed"? (chair, red, sleep)

Remember: Listen to the END of words to find rhymes!

Penguins

Penguins are black and white birds that cannot fly, but they are amazing swimmers! Most penguins live in cold places near the ocean, especially around Antarctica. They have thick feathers and fat under their skin to keep them warm in the icy water. Penguins waddle when they walk on land, but they can slide on their bellies across the ice. Penguins eat fish, squid, and tiny sea animals called krill. They can dive deep underwater and hold their breath for a long time while they hunt for food. Baby penguins are called chicks, and their parents take turns keeping the eggs warm and bringing food. Penguin families stay together and help take care of each other.
