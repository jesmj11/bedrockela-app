📚 A Messy Mistake

The three little kittens hung their wet mittens on hooks by the warm fire. Steam rose from the wool as they began to dry. Then the kittens sat down to eat their pie.

It was the best apple pie they had ever tasted! The pie was so good that the kittens ate very quickly. They were so hungry from searching for their mittens all day.

As they ate, pie filling dripped on their paws. Apple pieces fell on their fur. Soon the kittens were quite messy. When they put on their mittens, something terrible happened again.

Mother Cat looked at them and frowned. "What! You soiled your mittens? You naughty kittens!" The mittens were covered with pie filling and crumbs. "Oh no!" cried the kittens.

The Sun

The Sun is a star that gives Earth light and heat. Even though the Sun looks small in the sky, it is actually huge – much, much bigger than Earth! The Sun is so hot that nothing can live on it. The temperature on the Sun is thousands of degrees, hot enough to melt any metal instantly. Without the Sun, there would be no life on Earth. Plants need sunlight to grow, and animals need plants for food. The Sun also makes the water cycle work by heating up water and turning it into clouds. During the day, the Sun lights up our part of Earth, and at night, our part of Earth faces away from the Sun so it gets dark.
