📜 THE SHOOTING MATCH (Part 1)

The Sheriff of Nottingham was furious. Robin Hood was making him look like a fool. The outlaw robbed travelers on the King's roads, gave the money to the poor, and vanished into the forest before the Sheriff's men could catch him. Worse, the common people loved Robin and helped hide him. The Sheriff devised a plan. "Robin Hood is vain about his archery," the Sheriff told his advisors. "We'll hold a grand shooting match in Nottingham with a prize so fine he won't be able to resist. A golden arrow. When he shows up, we'll surround him and take him." Word of the contest spread across Nottinghamshire. The golden arrow would be awarded to the finest archer in England. Every bowman for fifty miles would compete. Robin's men warned him. "It's a trap," said Will Stutely, one of his most loyal followers. "The Sheriff wants your head." "Of course it's a trap," Robin said cheerfully. "That's what makes it fun." On the day of the contest, the streets of Nottingham were packed. The Sheriff sat on a raised platform, surrounded by armed guards, scanning the crowd for Robin Hood. He was looking for a young man in Lincoln green with a feather in his cap. What he saw was a ragged old man with a patched cloak, a bent back, and a scraggly beard — shuffling to the shooting line with a battered bow that looked like it might fall apart. The contest went on for hours. Archer after archer stepped forward. The targets were set at increasing distances. One by one, the competitors were eliminated until only three remained: a yeoman in brown, a tall stranger in scarlet, and the ragged old beggar. The yeoman shot first. His arrow struck the inner ring — an excellent shot. The crowd cheered. The stranger in scarlet shot next. His arrow struck the exact center. The crowd roared. The old beggar stepped forward. He drew his battered bow with surprising strength, aimed for a long moment, and released. His arrow split the stranger's arrow down the middle, lodging in the exact same spot. The crowd went wild. The Sheriff, puzzled but not suspicious, awarded the golden arrow to the ragged beggar, who shuffled away into the crowd and disappeared. That evening, an arrow thudded into the wooden post outside the Sheriff's window. Attached to it was a note: "Thank you for the golden arrow, my lord Sheriff. It was a lovely prize. — Robin Hood." The Sheriff's scream could be heard across Nottingham.

🤔 Reading Comprehension

Answer these questions about today's chapter:

1. How does Robin avoid being caught at the Sheriff's archery contest?

2. What is the Sheriff's reaction when he discovers Robin won the golden arrow?

3. Robin splits the other archer's arrow down the middle — a nearly impossible shot. Why does Robin take such extreme risks to show off his skill? What does this pattern of behavior suggest about a possible weakness in his character?

🗣️ Language: Word Roots: Greek and Latin Foundations

Learn:

Most academic vocabulary comes from Greek and Latin roots. Common roots: 'aud' (hear), 'dict' (speak), 'spec' (see), 'port' (carry), 'graph' (write), 'bio' (life), 'chrono' (time), 'geo' (earth). Knowing roots unlocks hundreds of words.

Example:

"'Spectator' (one who sees), 'inspect' (look into), 'retrospect' (look back). Same root, different prefixes change meaning. 'Biography' (writing about life), 'biology' (study of life)—same root, different suffixes."
