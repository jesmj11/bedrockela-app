📜 THE PRIORESS (Part 1)

Robin was not well. The wound on his arm had never healed properly, and now a fever had taken hold — burning and relentless, sapping his strength day by day. Friar Tuck's medicines helped, but they couldn't cure what ailed him. "You need proper care," Marian said. "A physician. Medicine. A real bed." Robin resisted, but eventually his body overruled his will. He could barely stand. Marian knew of a place: Kirklees Priory, a convent run by Robin's cousin, the Prioress. In those days, convents and monasteries were often the only places with medical knowledge. The nuns practiced bloodletting — the common treatment for fever — and the Prioress had a reputation as a healer. Robin rode to Kirklees with Little John at his side. They arrived at the priory as the sun was setting. The building was old and cold, built of gray stone, surrounded by a high wall. The Prioress met them at the gate. She was a thin woman with sharp eyes and a smile that didn't reach them. She welcomed Robin warmly — too warmly, Little John thought. Something felt wrong, but Robin was too ill to notice. "Let me bleed you," the Prioress said, leading Robin to a private chamber. "The fever must be drawn out." Little John wanted to stay, but Robin waved him off. "Wait outside. I'll be fine." Little John waited. An hour passed. Two hours. Three. The sun set. The moon rose. No word came from inside. Something was very wrong. Little John pounded on the door. No answer. He broke it down with one massive kick. Robin lay on the bed, pale as death. His arm was bandaged, but the bandage was soaked through. The Prioress had opened a vein and left it bleeding — not to heal, but to kill. She had been paid by the Sheriff. Robin's own cousin had betrayed him. "John," Robin whispered. His voice was barely audible. "My bow. Bring me my bow."

📝 Language: Latin and Greek Roots

What is it?

Many English words come from Latin and Greek roots. Learning common roots helps you understand new words. Example: 'port' means carry (transport, portable, import). 'Graph' means write (autograph, biography).

Example:

"The root 'bene' means good (benefit, benevolent). The root 'dict' means say or speak (predict, dictionary, verdict)."

📰 Informational Text

Medieval Medicine: Bloodletting

Medieval medicine was based on the theory of "humors"—four bodily fluids (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, black bile) that needed to be balanced. Bloodletting was a common treatment: cutting a vein to release "excess" blood, which supposedly cured fevers, infections, and other ailments.

In reality, bloodletting often did more harm than good, especially when patients were already weak. The Prioress's use of bloodletting to murder Robin while claiming to heal him shows how medical procedures could be weapons. Patients trusted healers completely, making them vulnerable to betrayal.
