📜 THE SHERIFF'S LAST STAND (Part 1)

The new Sheriff was a different kind of enemy — methodical, patient, and cruel. He didn't set traps or hold archery contests. Instead, he burned the villages that supported Robin. He arrested families. He offered rewards so large that even loyal friends were tempted to betray the band. One by one, the safe houses disappeared. The network of farmers and servants that Marian had built was dismantled through fear and force. Supplies grew scarce. The band was forced deeper into the forest, into territory that was harder to defend and harder to live in. Then the Sheriff gathered an army — not a patrol, not an escort, but a real army of two hundred soldiers — and marched into Sherwood Forest. The battle that followed was the largest Robin had ever fought. His men were outnumbered three to one, but they knew the forest. They used every trick: false trails, hidden pits, rope traps, ambushes from the trees. Arrows rained down from invisible positions. Soldiers who chased shadows found themselves lost in the green maze of Sherwood. Little John held a narrow bridge alone for thirty minutes, his quarterstaff cracking against swords and shields until the soldiers retreated rather than face him. Marian led a flanking attack that drove an entire company into a bog. Will Scarlet fought in his red outfit like a flame moving through the trees. By nightfall, the Sheriff's army withdrew. They had lost dozens of men and accomplished nothing. Robin had won — but the cost was high. Several of his men were wounded. Their camp was destroyed. Their supplies were gone. Robin sat on a stump, exhausted, watching his people tend their wounds and rebuild their shelters. He was forty years old. His hair was beginning to gray. The wound on his arm from the silver arrow contest still ached in cold weather. "How long can we keep doing this?" he asked Marian. She didn't answer. She didn't have to.

🗣️ Language: Tone and Mood: Language Creates Atmosphere

Learn:

Tone is the author's attitude toward the subject (humorous, serious, ironic, respectful). Mood is the feeling created for the reader (suspenseful, hopeful, melancholy). Authors create tone and mood through word choice, sentence structure, imagery, and pacing.

Example:

"Serious tone: 'The king's decree devastated the kingdom.' Ironic tone: 'The king's "justice" brought only misery.' The quotation marks create irony. Suspenseful mood: 'The dawn approached. Would the king demand she continue or order her death?'"
