📜 THE GREENWOOD (Part 1)

For weeks, Robin hid in Sherwood Forest, sleeping under trees, eating what he could hunt and forage. He was alone and afraid, but he was also free — freer than he had ever been in his life. And he was not the only outlaw in the greenwood. Sherwood Forest was home to dozens of men who had been driven outside the law by the cruelty of the times. Some had poached deer to feed hungry families. Others had fallen into debt and been thrown off their land by greedy lords. A few were former soldiers, abandoned by the crown after years of service. They lived in the forest like ghosts, hunted by the Sheriff's men, surviving on whatever they could find. Robin found them — or they found him. Either way, within a month he had gathered around him a band of loyal followers. They recognized something in the young outlaw: courage, fairness, and a gift for leadership that went beyond his years. He gave them rules. "We rob from those who have too much," Robin declared, standing on a fallen oak in a forest clearing that would become their meeting place. "We give to those who have too little. We never harm a woman or a child. We never rob a farmer, a craftsman, or any honest person who works for their bread. But lords, bishops, and tax collectors who grow fat on the suffering of common folk — they are fair game." The men cheered. "And one more thing," Robin added, his eyes serious. "We answer to each other. Every man here has a voice. No one is above the law of the greenwood." They made their camp in a hidden glen deep in the forest, beneath ancient oaks so massive that six men linking hands could not circle their trunks. They built shelters of branches and moss, dug a great cooking pit, and set lookouts on every trail. They wore green — Lincoln green, the color of the forest — so they could move through the trees like shadows. The people of Nottinghamshire began to whisper the name Robin Hood. And the Sheriff of Nottingham began to grind his teeth.

📝 Language: Context Clues

What is it?

When you encounter an unfamiliar word, use context clues from surrounding sentences to figure out its meaning. Look for definitions, examples, synonyms, or antonyms in nearby text.

Example:

"In 'The outlaw was a fugitive, fleeing from the law,' the phrase 'fleeing from the law' helps you understand that fugitive means someone running from authorities."

📰 Informational Text

Life in Sherwood Forest

Sherwood Forest in the 12th-13th centuries was an enormous woodland covering over 100,000 acres in Nottinghamshire. The forest was dense with oak, birch, and ash trees, providing perfect cover for those hiding from the law. Wild game was plentiful: deer, boar, rabbits, and birds. For outlaws, the forest offered both sanctuary and survival.

Living as an outlaw meant constant danger. You couldn't seek help from the law, visit family, or trade in towns. Outlaws formed bands for protection and survival. They built hidden camps, developed coded signals, and relied on a network of sympathetic villagers for information. The greenwood became a kingdom unto itself—outside royal law but governed by its own code of honor.
