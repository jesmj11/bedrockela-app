📚 Reading Comprehension

Passage from "Home Roads"

Home Roads

Anne came home to Green Gables in June, two years after she'd left, and stood at the gate and looked at the white farmhouse with the green trim and the apple orchards she had loved since the first morning she'd woken to them.

1. Anne says she is 'exactly who she had been becoming all along.' What does she mean — and how does this connect to the book's central theme?

2. The final line says Anne 'never had been' afraid, really — she just hadn't always known it. What does this final realization say about self-knowledge?
