📜 The Pacific (Part 1)

The Pacific

We entered the Pacific through the Straits and the ocean opened before us like a held breath releasing.

The Pacific is not like the Atlantic. The Atlantic has weather and history and the presence of continents on either side giving it edges. The Pacific is simply enormous. It covers more of the earth than all the land combined. Standing at the bow and looking out at it, you understood things about the scale of the world that no map can convey.

Ahab became more himself in the Pacific. He was at the rail more hours of the day. He consulted his chart. He spoke to the crew with a directness that had a quality of imminence, as if the thing he had been sailing toward was now close enough to smell.

He climbed the masthead himself some mornings, which captains normally did not do. He stood in the crow's nest with his spyglass and looked at the ocean.

📜 The Pacific (Part 2)

He was looking for white.

The rest of us looked too — not with his intensity, but with the natural vigilance of men at sea who know that spotting a whale means a share of profit. Every splash examined. Every disturbance in the water considered.

I was at the masthead one afternoon when I saw — or thought I saw — something large and pale beneath the surface. I held my breath. I looked again. It was a trick of the light, a shadow, a wave.

But for one moment it had been there: something vast and white in the green water, moving with the purposeful ease of something that owned the ocean.

I said nothing. I had not seen it clearly enough to report.

📜 The Pacific (Part 3)

But I thought about it for the rest of my watch, and I thought: I understand now. Not the obsession — that I still could not justify — but the whale itself. The white shape in the water that was real and enormous and belonged to a world that existed entirely without reference to us.

There was something about that which was both terrible and magnificent. A thing that large, that indifferent, that genuinely other.

I thought: if I were less sane than I am, I might need to pursue that to its end too.

I went down from the masthead and said nothing to anyone.

📝 Grammar: Subjunctive Mood for Hypotheticals and Wishes

Learn:

The subjunctive mood expresses wishes, suggestions, or hypothetical situations. It often uses 'were' instead of 'was': 'If I were the king...' Common in formal writing after verbs like wish, suggest, recommend: 'I wish she were here.' 'I suggest that he study harder' (not 'studies').

Example:

"Subjunctive: 'If Scheherazade were to fail, she would die.' 'The king wished that his wife were faithful.' Indicative (not subjunctive): 'Scheherazade was clever.'"
