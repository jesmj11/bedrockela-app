Hint: Authors don't always state things directly—they imply them through word choice, actions, and context.

2. Create TWO examples of three moments related to this week's chapter where the author implies something without stating it directly. For each, cite the text, explain what's implied, and identify the textual evidence that supports your inference. Discuss why the author chose implication over direct statement.

Example: Text: 'The king listened without speaking.' Inference: He's processing something deeply, perhaps beginning to change.
