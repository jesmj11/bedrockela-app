Definition: Stopped or caught something or someone before they reached their destination. (revisited again)

"Aramis's network intercepted messages revealing Milady's location and direction of travel."

📖 The Warning - Part 2

They rode hard for Paris, gathering information at every stop. Aramis's network of contacts proved invaluable — at each coaching inn, there was someone who owed him a favor, someone who had heard something, someone who could point them in the right direction. The picture that emerged was terrifying. Milady had returned to France. She had been spotted in the north, traveling fast, with purpose. She was heading for a convent near the town of Béthune, where — according to Aramis's sources — Constance had been hidden for her own safety. "She knows where Constance is," D'Artagnan said, his face white. "How does she know?" "The Cardinal's spies," Athos said. "He knows everything. And Milady has his full resources behind her." They pushed their horses harder. D'Artagnan rode at the front, leaning forward in the saddle, willing the horse to go faster. Every minute felt like an hour. Every delay — a lame horse, a blocked road, a bridge under repair — was agony. On the third day, they met a man on the road who recognized Aramis. He was a monk from a monastery near Béthune, and he carried terrible news. "A woman arrived at the convent two days ago," the monk said. "Beautiful, with golden hair. She told the Mother Superior she was a friend of the young woman staying there — Constance Bonacieux. The Mother Superior believed her." "No," D'Artagnan whispered. "We must ride faster," Athos said. They abandoned the road and cut across open country, jumping fences and splashing through streams. The horses were foaming and wild-eyed. D'Artagnan's hands were numb on the reins, and his heart was pounding so hard he could hear it over the hoofbeats. As the sun set on the third day, they saw the convent in the distance — a quiet stone building surrounded by gardens, looking peaceful and safe. D'Artagnan leaped from his horse before it had fully stopped and ran for the door.

📚 Informational Text

Fashion in the Age of Musketeers

Fashion in the 1600s was elaborate and expensive. Men wore puffy breeches (short pants), long stockings, and fancy jackets with many buttons. They also wore large white collars, feathered hats, and high leather boots with folded tops. Rich men's clothes were made of silk and velvet in bright colors. Swords were not just weapons but fashion accessories. Women wore long dresses with tight bodices and full skirts. They used lots of makeup and wore their hair in fancy styles with ribbons and jewels. Looking fashionable showed wealth and social status.
