📖 Vocabulary

These words appear in today's chapter. Look for them as you read!

1. gloomy

Definition: Dark, dim, and depressing. A place or mood that feels heavy and sad.

In the story: "The forest was gloomy — the trees blocked out the sunlight."

2. undergrowth

Definition: The thick bushes, plants, and small trees that grow beneath the tall trees in a forest.

In the story: "They pushed through the undergrowth and found a man made entirely of tin."

📝 Language: Prefixes: un-, re-, dis-

Learn:

A prefix comes at the beginning of a word and changes its meaning. Un- means not (unhappy = not happy). Re- means again (reread = read again). Dis- means not or opposite (disagree = not agree).

Example:

"If kind means nice, then unkind means not nice. If do means to perform an action, then redo means to do it again."
