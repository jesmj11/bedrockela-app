Today's Words

1. ultimatum

Definition: A final demand or statement of terms — do this, or face consequences. A threat with a deadline.

"Frollo delivered an ultimatum: Esmeralda must leave the cathedral, or he would find a way to take her by force."

2. emancipation

Definition: The process of being set free from control or oppression. Gaining independence.

"Quasimodo's refusal to hand over Esmeralda was his emancipation — the moment he stopped being Frollo's puppet and became his own person."

Frollo's Visit

📖 Frollo's Visit - Part 2

Frollo came to the cathedral on the seventh day. He climbed the tower stairs with slow, deliberate steps — the steps of a man who owned the building and everything in it. He wore his black robes and his cold expression, and when he appeared in the doorway of Quasimodo's room, the temperature seemed to drop. Esmeralda was sitting by the window, braiding Djali's collar with ribbon she had found in the sacristy. She saw Frollo and went very still. "Good morning, Archdeacon," she said. Her voice was calm, but her hands had stopped moving. Frollo did not look at her. He looked at Quasimodo, who was standing by the wall, his fists clenched at his sides. "You defied me," Frollo said. "I saved her," Quasimodo said. "You broke into a prison. You assaulted guards. You carried a convicted criminal into the house of God." "She is not a criminal," Quasimodo said. "She is innocent. You know she is innocent." Something flickered in Frollo's eyes — a flash of surprise, quickly extinguished. In sixteen years, Quasimodo had never contradicted him. Never questioned him. Never raised his voice above a whisper in Frollo's presence. "I know what is best for this city," Frollo said. "I know what is best for the church. And I know what is best for you. This girl is poisoning your mind, filling your head with ideas that do not belong there. She must leave the cathedral." "No," Quasimodo said. The word hung in the air like the last note of a bell. "No?" Frollo repeated softly. The softness was worse than shouting. When Frollo spoke softly, it meant he was truly dangerous. "She has claimed sanctuary," Quasimodo said. "The law protects her. Even you cannot violate sanctuary." Frollo stared at Quasimodo for a long, terrible moment. Then he turned to Esmeralda. "You have bewitched him," Frollo said. "You have turned my ward against me with your tricks and your smiles and your false kindness. But sanctuaries do not last forever. Sooner or later, you will have to leave this cathedral. And when you do, I will be waiting." He turned and walked down the stairs without another word. Esmeralda was shaking. "He will not stop," she said. "Men like Frollo never stop." "Then I will not stop either," Quasimodo said. He stood in the doorway of his tower room, his hunched body filling the frame, his one eye burning with a determination that had not existed a week ago. He had spent his life obeying Frollo. He had spent his life believing he was worthless. That was over. "No one will take you from this cathedral," Quasimodo said. "I promise." It was the first promise he had ever made to anyone other than Frollo. And he meant it with every fiber of his being.

📚 Informational Text

Redemption: Can People Change?

Redemption means being saved from evil or error. It's about transformation — becoming a better person than you were before. Many stories explore whether people can truly change. Can someone who has done terrible things become good? In Christian theology (important in medieval times), redemption was central: anyone could be forgiven if they truly repented. But Hugo's novel asks whether redemption is possible for everyone. Quasimodo redeems himself through his protection of Esmeralda — he becomes more than the frightened, obedient creature Frollo raised. But can Frollo be redeemed? Or has he gone too far? The story suggests that redemption requires recognizing you were wrong — and some people are too proud or too far gone to do that.

Check Your Understanding:

2. How does Quasimodo redeem himself in the story?

3. Why might some people be unable to achieve redemption?
