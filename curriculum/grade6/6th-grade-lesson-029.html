📜 TOM SAWYER'S GANG (Part 1)

The Widow Douglas's parlor was full of people—the Thatchers, the Harpers, Aunt Polly, Sid, Mary, the minister, and half the town. The boys were scrubbed and stuffed into new suits. Huck kept eyeing the window, calculating the drop. "Tom, we can escape if we—" "Relax. It's just a party." The Welshman stood up and made a speech, thanking the widow for her hospitality and revealing that it was Huckleberry Finn who had saved her from Injun Joe that night on Cardiff Hill. Huck turned crimson. The widow said she meant to give Huck a permanent home, educate him, and eventually set him up in business. Tom couldn't wait another second. He stood up and said: "Huck doesn't need charity. Huck's rich." Nobody laughed—they assumed it was a joke. Tom ran outside, came back dragging a small wagon, and dumped two bags of gold coins onto the table. "Half is Huck's and half is mine!" The room went dead silent. Then everyone started talking at once. Tom told the whole story—the haunted house, Injun Joe, Number Two under the cross, the treasure box in the cave. When he was finished, the money was counted. Twelve thousand dollars. More than anyone in the room had ever seen. The Widow Douglas invested Huck's share. Judge Thatcher did the same with Tom's, at Aunt Polly's request. Each boy had an income of about a dollar a day—a fortune for the time. Judge Thatcher said Tom was the bravest boy he'd ever met. When Becky told her father how Tom had taken her whipping at school, the Judge said it was "a noble and generous act" and that Tom would make a great man someday. Huck's new life, however, was not going smoothly. The Widow Douglas was kind, but her world had rules. He had to eat with a fork. He had to wear shoes. He had to sleep in sheets. He had to go to church. He couldn't smoke, couldn't swear, couldn't spit. Everything was scheduled—up by a bell, meals by a bell, bed by a bell. After three miserable weeks, Huck disappeared. The whole town searched for him. Tom Sawyer found him on the second day—hiding in an empty barrel behind the old slaughter-house, happily smoking a pipe in his old rags. "Don't talk about it, Tom. I've tried it and it doesn't work. The widow means well, but I can't stand the rules. I've got to get up at the same time every morning. I've got to wash and comb my hair. I can't fish unless I ask, can't swim unless I ask. I've got to wear shoes every Sunday. Being rich isn't what it's cracked up to be—it's just worry and work and wishing you were dead. Take my share of the money and leave me alone." "I can't do that, Huck." "Why not?" "Because if you're not respectable, you can't be in the gang." "What gang?" "Tom Sawyer's Gang. I'm starting a gang of robbers, and we need a proper hideout, and rules, and an oath signed in blood at midnight. But we can't let you in if you're not respectable." Huck thought about this for a long time. "All right," he finally said. "I'll go back to the widow for a month and try to stand it—if you let me in the gang." "Done!" "When do we start?" "Right away. We'll get the boys together tonight for the initiation. We'll swear the oath in the cave at midnight." "That sounds perfect, Tom." "It will be. And Huck—I'll talk to the widow. I'll get her to ease up a little." "Would you? That'd help. If she'd just let me smoke and swear in private, I think I could make it." Tom grinned. "Come on, old friend. Let's go home." And so they did. THE END

🗣️ Language: Syntax and Sentence Structure for Effect

Learn:

Syntax is the arrangement of words and phrases. Authors manipulate syntax for emphasis, rhythm, and meaning. Short sentences create tension. Long, flowing sentences create contemplation. Inverted syntax ('Never had he felt such anger') creates emphasis. Sentence fragments can be powerful.

Example:

"Standard: 'She told the story carefully.' Inverted for emphasis: 'Carefully, she told the story.' Fragment for drama: 'One thousand nights. And one.' Varied syntax: 'The king listened. He said nothing. He simply listened, hour after hour, as if hearing for the first time.'"
