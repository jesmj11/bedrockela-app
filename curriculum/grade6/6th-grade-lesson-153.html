📜 THE GRANDMOTHER'S THREAD

Irene found her way to the grandmother's room again.

The old woman was waiting, as if she had known Irene would come. The spinning wheel turned. The thread gleamed.

"The time has come," the grandmother said.

She placed the gold ring on Irene's finger. It was warm — warmer than metal should be — and it fit perfectly, as if it had been made for her.

"The thread is tied to this ring," the grandmother said. "You cannot see it. You cannot feel it with your fingers. But it is there, and it will guide you."

📜 THE GRANDMOTHER'S THREAD (Part 2)

"Where will it lead me?"

"Wherever you need to go. Into the dark, if necessary. Through places that frighten you. But always — always — toward safety. The thread will never lead you wrong."

"How do I follow something I can't see?"

The grandmother smiled. "You follow it with your heart. You trust it. Even when everything around you says it's not there — even when people you love tell you you're foolish — you trust the thread."

"What if I'm scared?"

📜 THE GRANDMOTHER'S THREAD (Part 3)

"You will be scared. Courage doesn't mean you're not afraid. Courage means you follow the thread anyway."

Irene looked at the ring on her finger. She could see nothing attached to it — no thread, no line, no gleam of silver. But when she closed her eyes and reached out with her heart instead of her hands, she felt it. A gentle tug. A direction. A quiet voice that said: this way.

"I'll follow it," Irene said.

"I know you will," the grandmother said. "That's why I chose you."

📝 Grammar: Restrictive vs. Non-Restrictive Clauses and Commas

Restrictive clauses are essential to the meaning and need no commas: 'The girl who told the stories survived.' Non-restrictive clauses add extra information and use commas: 'Scheherazade, who had read everything, told stories.' The difference changes meaning: 'Students who studied passed' vs. 'Students, who studied, passed.'

Example:

Restrictive: 'The tales that changed the king came from many cultures.' Non-restrictive: 'Arabian Nights, which contains hundreds of tales, influenced world literature.'
