📜 CAPTAIN NEMO

They locked us in a small, pitch-black room. For hours we sat in silence, unable to see each other, unable to tell if it was day or night. Ned pounded on the walls and shouted. No one answered. Eventually the door opened and two men led us down a corridor lit by soft electric light—an extraordinary thing in 1866, when most of the world still used gas lamps and candles. The walls were polished metal. The air was clean and cool. They brought us to a large, elegant room furnished with velvet sofas, paintings, a marble table, and shelves lined with thousands of books. A pipe organ stood in one corner. The floor was covered with thick carpet. A man was waiting for us. He was tall and broad-shouldered, with dark skin, a high forehead, and eyes that seemed to look straight through you. His hair was thick and black, swept back from his face. He had the bearing of someone who was used to giving orders and having them obeyed without question. His age was hard to guess—somewhere between thirty-five and fifty. He studied us for a long moment in silence. Then he spoke—in perfect French. "I am Captain Nemo," he said. "You are aboard my vessel, the Nautilus." "Nemo?" I said. The word means "no one" in Latin. A faint smile crossed his face. "I am no one. I have severed all ties with the world above. I have no country. I answer to no government. The sea is my only home." Ned stepped forward aggressively. "You attacked our ship and nearly killed us. We demand to be released." Nemo's eyes hardened. "You hunted me. Your ship fired on me. You tried to destroy me with cannons and harpoons. I owe you nothing." He paused. "However, I do not wish to be cruel. You have two choices. You may remain aboard the Nautilus as my guests. You will be fed, housed, and allowed to witness things no human being has ever seen. In exchange, you will never leave. You will live and die aboard this vessel." "And the second choice?" I asked. "There is no second choice. You know the Nautilus exists. I cannot allow that knowledge to reach the surface world." Ned looked ready to fight, but I put a hand on his arm. We were unarmed, outnumbered, and trapped inside an iron machine at the bottom of the ocean. Fighting would accomplish nothing. "We accept your hospitality, Captain," I said carefully. "For now." "For now," Nemo repeated, with that same faint smile. "Come. Let me show you my ship."

📝 Grammar: Misplaced and Dangling Modifiers

Modifiers should be placed next to what they modify. Misplaced: 'She told a story to the king with great skill' (the king doesn't have the skill—she does). Dangling: 'Walking through the palace, the stories came to mind' (stories can't walk). Correct: 'Walking through the palace, she thought of stories.'

Example:

Misplaced: 'The merchant found treasure in the cave that was priceless' (the cave or the treasure?). Better: 'The merchant found priceless treasure in the cave.' Dangling: 'After telling the story, the king was impressed.' Better: 'After she told the story, the king was impressed.'
