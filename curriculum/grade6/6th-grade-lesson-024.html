📜 PRISONERS OF THE SEA

I sank into the dark ocean, choking, tumbling. My heavy clothes dragged me down. I kicked and clawed my way back to the surface, gasping for air. The Abraham Lincoln was already a quarter mile away, her lights growing smaller. She hadn't seen me go over. "Sir! Professor!" A hand grabbed my collar. It was Conseil. He had jumped in after me—without hesitation, without being asked. "Conseil! Are you insane?" "I am in your service, sir. Where you go, I go." We treaded water in the black ocean, watching the ship's lights shrink to pinpricks and vanish. We were alone. For hours we floated, taking turns resting against each other. The cold crept into our bones. My legs went numb. Conseil never complained, but I could feel him shivering. We were going to die out here—drowned in the middle of the Pacific. Then my foot struck something solid. Not the ocean floor—we were miles from land. Something hard, something flat, something that rang under my boot like iron. "What on earth—" "Up here!" a voice called. It was Ned Land. He was sitting on top of the very creature we had been hunting, and he was grinning. "It's not an animal," Ned said, pulling us up onto the metal surface. "It's a machine. A submarine." He was right. Beneath us was a long, smooth hull made of riveted iron plates. No scales, no skin—just metal, bolted together by human hands. The "eye" that had glowed was a powerful electric light. The jets of water were exhaust vents. We lay on the hull, catching our breath, trying to understand what we had found. A man-made vessel, shaped like a giant cigar, that could outrun the fastest warship in the world. It was impossible—and it was right beneath us. A hatch opened with a clang. Eight men in dark uniforms emerged, grabbed us without a word, and dragged us inside. The hatch sealed shut behind us. We were in absolute darkness. We were prisoners.

📝 Language: Tone and Mood: Language Creates Atmosphere

Tone is the author's attitude toward the subject (humorous, serious, ironic, respectful). Mood is the feeling created for the reader (suspenseful, hopeful, melancholy). Authors create tone and mood through word choice, sentence structure, imagery, and pacing.

Example:

Serious tone: 'The king's decree devastated the kingdom.' Ironic tone: 'The king's "justice" brought only misery.' The quotation marks create irony. Suspenseful mood: 'The dawn approached. Would the king demand she continue or order her death?'
