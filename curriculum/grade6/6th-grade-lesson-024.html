📜 HUCK SAVES THE WIDOW (Part 1)

While Tom and Becky wandered deeper into the cave, Huck Finn was keeping watch outside the tavern. The night was cloudy and still. By ten o'clock, the town had gone to sleep. By eleven, the tavern lights went dark. Huck waited in the shadows, patient and alert. Midnight came and went. He was about to give up when— The alley door clicked open. Two figures slipped out. One carried something bulky under his arm. The treasure box! Huck's pulse hammered. He couldn't go get Tom now—they'd lose the men forever. He had to follow them alone. He crept after them through the dark streets, bare feet silent on the dirt. The men turned up the cross-street, climbed the path toward Cardiff Hill, passed the old Welshman's house, and kept climbing. Huck stayed close, hidden in the shadows. They stopped at the top of the hill, near the path that led to the Widow Douglas's house. Huck crouched behind a boulder, straining to hear. "There are lights on," said the stranger. "She's got company." Then Injun Joe spoke, and what he said turned Huck's blood to ice. "Her husband was the justice who had me horsewhipped in front of the whole town, like a dog. He's dead now, so I can't get him. But I'll take it out on her." "Don't kill her, Joe." "I'm not going to kill her. I'm going to hurt her. Cut her face so she'll remember." Huck had heard enough. He turned, crept backward with agonizing care—one step, then another, then another—and then he ran. He ran down the hill like his life depended on it, because it did, and he didn't stop until he was pounding on the Welshman's door. "Who's there?" "Huckleberry Finn! Let me in—quick!" "Huckleberry Finn? That's not a name that opens many doors. But let him in, boys." Huck tumbled inside, gasping. "Please don't ever tell I was the one who told. He'd kill me. But the Widow Douglas—Injun Joe—he's up there right now—he's going to—" The old Welshman didn't need to hear more. He grabbed his gun, woke his two grown sons, and within three minutes they were charging up the hill. Huck hid behind a boulder and listened. For a terrible stretch of silence, nothing happened. Then—BANG! BANG! Gunshots split the night, followed by shouts and the sound of men crashing through the bushes. Huck didn't wait to find out what happened. He ran back down the hill and didn't stop until he was three miles away.

🤔 Reading Comprehension

Answer these questions about today's chapter:

1. Why does Huck follow the two men instead of going to get Tom?

2. What does Huck do when he hears Injun Joe's plan to hurt the Widow Douglas?

3. Huck is terrified of Injun Joe, yet he risks his life to save the Widow Douglas. What motivates Huck to act despite his fear? What does this moment reveal about who Huck really is underneath his rough exterior?

🗣️ Language: Tone and Mood: Language Creates Atmosphere

Learn:

Tone is the author's attitude toward the subject (humorous, serious, ironic, respectful). Mood is the feeling created for the reader (suspenseful, hopeful, melancholy). Authors create tone and mood through word choice, sentence structure, imagery, and pacing.

Example:

"Serious tone: 'The king's decree devastated the kingdom.' Ironic tone: 'The king's "justice" brought only misery.' The quotation marks create irony. Suspenseful mood: 'The dawn approached. Would the king demand she continue or order her death?'"
