📜 THE GOBLIN ATTACK

The goblins attacked on a night without moon.

They had been tunneling for months, and they finally broke through — not into the castle, as Curdie had feared, but into the cellars beneath it. The stone floor cracked, then caved in, and goblin warriors poured up through the hole like water through a broken dam.

The castle was thrown into chaos. Servants screamed. Guards grabbed swords and rushed to the cellar stairs. The goblin creatures — the eyeless dogs, the enormous rats — swarmed through the lower floors, knocking over furniture, tearing at doors.

📜 THE GOBLIN ATTACK (Part 2)

Irene was in her room when the floor trembled. She knew instantly what was happening. She grabbed her cloak, woke Lootie, and led her to the upper floors, away from the goblins.

Curdie was already in the castle — he had been sleeping in the servants' quarters, keeping watch as he had for weeks. When the floor collapsed, he grabbed his pick and ran toward the breach.

The battle was desperate. The goblins were strong in numbers, and they fought with a savage fury born of centuries of resentment. The castle guards were braver but unprepared. They had never fought goblins — most of them had never even seen one.

📜 THE GOBLIN ATTACK (Part 3)

Curdie rallied the guards. "Stamp on their feet!" he shouted. "Sing! They can't stand singing!"

The guards thought he was insane. Then they tried it — stomping, shouting, singing any song they could think of — and the goblins flinched. They fell back. The singing didn't kill them, but it confused them, disrupted their coordination, and gave the humans an advantage they desperately needed.

But the goblins kept coming. There were hundreds of them, and more were pouring through the breach every minute.

📝 Grammar: Parallel Structure in Lists and Series

Parallel structure uses the same grammatical form for items in a series, creating rhythm and clarity. All items should be the same part of speech or phrase type: 'She was intelligent, determined, and resourceful' (all adjectives). Not parallel: 'She was intelligent, determined, and had resourcefulness.'

Example:

Parallel: 'The king lost his faith, abandoned his principles, and destroyed his compassion.' Not parallel: 'The king lost his faith, his principles were abandoned, and destroying his compassion.'
