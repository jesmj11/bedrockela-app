📜 THE LOTUS EATERS

The storm raged for nine days. When it died, Odysseus found himself off an island with a sweet smell drifting from the interior. He sent three men ashore. They didn't come back. He found them eating the flowers of the lotus plant, surrounded by people with dreamy eyes. The lotus erased memory — all desire to go home, all ambition, all purpose. The men who ate them forgot who they were. Odysseus didn't eat. He dragged his men back to the ships by force and ordered the fleet to leave immediately. The lotus was the temptation to give up — a death not of the body but of the spirit.

📝 Grammar: Semicolons, Colons, and Dashes for Emphasis

Semicolons join closely related independent clauses: 'She was not afraid; she was determined.' Colons introduce lists, explanations, or quotes: 'She needed one thing: time.' Dashes create emphasis or mark interruptions: 'Her plan—dangerous but necessary—might work.'

Example:

Semicolon: 'The night ended; the story continued.' Colon: 'Scheherazade understood one truth: stories change minds.' Dash: 'The king—once kind, now cruel—listened carefully.'
