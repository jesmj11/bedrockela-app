📜 SCYLLA AND CHARYBDIS

On one side of a narrow strait was Charybdis — a whirlpool that could swallow a ship. On the other was Scylla — a six-headed monster who would snatch six men from any ship that passed. "Choose," Circe had said. "Lose six men, or lose everyone." Odysseus chose Scylla. He told no one. Six heads shot down from the cave. Six men were seized and pulled screaming into the darkness. Odysseus stood at the stern, looking back. He had made the right choice. But "the right choice" meant nothing when you could still hear your friends screaming. This, he thought, is what leadership costs.

📝 Grammar: Noun Clauses and Their Functions

A noun clause acts as a noun in a sentence, often starting with that, what, who, whoever, whether, if. They can be subjects, objects, or complements: 'What she said convinced him' (subject). 'He knew that she was clever' (object). 'The question is whether he will change' (complement).

Example:

Subject: 'Whoever tells the best story wins.' Object: 'The king realized that he had been wrong.' Complement: 'The problem was that he couldn't trust anyone.'
