📜 GOBLIN CREATURES

The goblins weren't the only danger on the mountain. They kept creatures — animals as twisted and strange as the goblins themselves.

These goblin creatures had once been ordinary animals — dogs, cats, rats — that had been taken underground generations ago. Over the years, living in darkness with the goblins, they had changed. They were larger, fiercer, and stranger than their surface cousins. Dogs with no eyes but an uncanny sense of smell. Cats with claws like fishhooks. Rats the size of small dogs.

The creatures began appearing on the mountainside at night, ranging further from the goblin tunnels than they ever had before. Livestock disappeared. A miner's dog was found dead. The people of the town grew nervous.

Curdie tracked the creatures through the forest and back to their source — a new tunnel entrance the goblins had dug, closer to the surface than any Curdie had seen. The goblins were expanding their territory, pushing upward, preparing for something big.

📜 GOBLIN CREATURES (Part 2)

He brought this news to Irene. They met in secret, at the castle gate, speaking through the bars like prisoners.

"They're getting closer," Curdie said. "The tunnel I found is only a few hundred yards from the castle wall."

"What should we do?"

"I'll keep watching. You stay inside after dark. And if anything strange happens — any sound from below the floors, any cracking or shaking — run. Get out of the castle and go uphill. The goblins won't follow into open ground above the tree line."

📜 GOBLIN CREATURES (Part 3)

Irene nodded. She was eight years old, and she was being asked to prepare for an invasion. She didn't cry. She didn't panic. She filed the information away, the same way Curdie did.

They were alike, these two — the princess and the miner. Different worlds, different lives, but the same practical courage.

📝 Language: Context Clues: Multiple Strategies Combined

Expert readers use multiple context clue types simultaneously: definition/restatement (author defines it), synonym/antonym (similar/opposite word nearby), example (specific instances given), inference (reasoning from situation). Look for signal words: 'or,' 'such as,' 'unlike,' 'however.'

Example:

From Arabian Nights: 'The king was consumed by a rage that was also grief—unlike simple anger, this sorrow-fury combination destroyed his judgment.' Multiple clues: definition ('sorrow-fury'), contrast ('unlike simple anger'), and effect (destroyed judgment).
