📜 THE RED SEA

The Nautilus turned west and entered the Red Sea, that long, narrow body of water separating Africa from Arabia. Nemo was in unusually good spirits. He spent time in the salon with me, discussing marine biology, ocean currents, and the history of navigation. For a man who claimed to have abandoned humanity, he was remarkably well-informed about human affairs. "Professor," he said one afternoon, "how do you suppose the ancient Egyptians traveled between the Red Sea and the Mediterranean?" "The Suez Canal is nearly complete," I said. "But the ancients had no such channel." "No. But nature provided one." His eyes gleamed. "There is an underground passage—a tunnel beneath the Isthmus of Suez—that connects the two seas. I discovered it myself. I call it the Arabian Tunnel." I stared at him. "That's impossible." "Many things I do are considered impossible. Come to the salon window in one hour." I did. And I watched, speechless, as the Nautilus entered a dark, narrow passage in the rock, submerged completely, and traveled through an underwater tunnel for twenty minutes. When we emerged, the water had changed color. The temperature had shifted. The fish were different species entirely. We were in the Mediterranean Sea. We had crossed from the Indian Ocean to the Mediterranean in less than half an hour, through a passage that no one on the surface world knew existed. Ned Land, when told, simply shook his head. "This ship gets stranger every day." But his eyes were calculating. The Mediterranean meant Europe. Europe meant home. I could see the escape plans forming behind his eyes.

📝 Grammar: Verbals: Participles, Gerunds, and Infinitives

Verbals are verb forms used as other parts of speech. Participles (-ing or -ed forms) act as adjectives: 'The sleeping king.' Gerunds (-ing forms) act as nouns: 'Storytelling saved her life.' Infinitives (to + verb) act as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs: 'To survive was her goal.'

Example:

Participle: 'Scheherazade, speaking carefully, chose her words.' Gerund: 'Listening became the king's obsession.' Infinitive: 'To trust again would require courage.'
