📜 LIFE AT COURT (Part 1)

Robin tried. He really did. He went to London. He wore fine clothes. He attended banquets and councils and ceremonies. He smiled at lords who had grown rich by cheating peasants. He bowed to bishops who owned more land than the farmers they were supposed to care for. He stood in stone halls and breathed stale air and pretended that this was where he belonged. It was a disaster. Robin Hood was built for the greenwood — for open sky and fresh air, for the crack of a bowstring and the laughter of friends, for the freedom of a man who answered to no one but his own conscience. Court life was the opposite of everything he loved. The other nobles despised him. To them, he was a common criminal who had been elevated above his station by a sentimental king. They whispered behind his back, blocked his proposals, and excluded him from every decision that mattered. Robin's men fared no better. Little John nearly started a brawl with a duke who insulted the common people. Friar Tuck got into a loud theological argument with the Archbishop of Canterbury. Will Scarlet's scarlet outfit was mocked by every courtier in London. Only Marian adapted. She had grown up in the world of minor nobility and understood its rules — even if she despised them. She navigated the court with skill, gathering information, making quiet alliances, and keeping Robin from punching anyone. But even Marian could see the truth: Robin was dying inside. The light in his eyes — that restless, joyful spark — was fading. He spent more and more time staring out of windows, looking north toward the forests he had left behind. "Go," Marian finally said, one evening as they stood on the castle walls. "The King—" "The King gave you a pardon, not a leash. You've served him faithfully for over a year. Your heart is in Sherwood, Robin. And so is mine."

🗣️ Language: Semantic Fields and Word Families

Learn:

Semantic fields are groups of words related by meaning: words for 'saying' (whisper, shout, murmur, declare, announce), 'intelligence' (clever, wise, shrewd, cunning, astute). Authors vary words within semantic fields to create precise meanings and avoid repetition.

Example:

"Instead of repeating 'said,' the author might use: 'whispered,' 'announced,' 'murmured,' 'declared'—each adds different meaning (volume, formality, emotion). Each word in the semantic field of 'speaking' creates a different effect."

📰 Medieval Court Life

Royal courts were centers of power, intrigue, and complex social rules. Courtiers competed for the king's favor through flattery, gifts, and political maneuvering. Success at court required education, refined manners, fashionable clothes, and the ability to navigate complex social hierarchies.

For someone like Robin Hood—used to the freedom and honesty of the greenwood—court life was suffocating. Every word was calculated. Enemies wore smiles. Decisions were made through whispered conversations rather than open debate. The text shows that physical chains aren't the only kind of prison.
