📜 BREAKING THE CODE

I kept the secret for approximately four hours. My uncle, who had not eaten all day, announced that no one in the house would eat until the code was broken. Martha looked at me with desperate eyes. My stomach growled. Gräuben was coming for dinner, and there would be no dinner. "Uncle," I said miserably, "I may have figured it out." I showed him. He read the decoded message once, twice, three times. His face went through a series of expressions — confusion, understanding, wonder, and finally, a blazing, terrifying joy. "Saknussemm!" he roared. "The great Saknussemm! He went to the center of the earth, and he left us directions!" "Uncle, surely you don't believe—" "Believe? Science doesn't believe or disbelieve. Science investigates! And we are going to investigate. We leave for Iceland immediately." "Iceland?!" "Sneffels Yökull is a volcano in Iceland. The message says to descend into its crater when the shadow of a specific peak — Scartaris — falls across the correct opening. Before the first of July. That gives us less than a month." "But Uncle — the center of the earth is thousands of miles below the surface. The heat alone would be impossible. The pressure would crush us. This is insane." He turned on me with the full force of his personality — all six feet of furious German professor. "Science has never proven that the earth's interior is hot," he said. "That is a theory. Saknussemm went and returned. He left this message as proof. Are you a scientist, Axel, or are you a coward?" That was unfair. But it was effective. I went to Gräuben, hoping she would talk sense into me — or at least give me permission to stay home. Instead, she took my hands, looked into my eyes, and said: "Go, Axel. Go with your uncle, and come back to me." And so I went.

📝 Grammar: Noun Clauses and Their Functions

A noun clause acts as a noun in a sentence, often starting with that, what, who, whoever, whether, if. They can be subjects, objects, or complements: 'What she said convinced him' (subject). 'He knew that she was clever' (object). 'The question is whether he will change' (complement).

Example:

Subject: 'Whoever tells the best story wins.' Object: 'The king realized that he had been wrong.' Complement: 'The problem was that he couldn't trust anyone.'
