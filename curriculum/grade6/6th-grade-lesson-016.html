📜 TREASURE HUNTING (Part 1)

Tom was the golden boy of St. Petersburg once more. His name appeared in the local paper. People said he might be president someday. But at night, the dreams came. Injun Joe was in every one of them—always lurking, always watching, always with murder in his eyes. Huck was just as frightened. Tom had told the lawyer everything, which meant Huck's share in the secret might eventually come out. A detective came from St. Louis, poked around town, shook his head wisely, and went home without finding anything useful. Injun Joe had vanished. Slowly, day by day, Tom's fear faded. He would never feel completely safe until Injun Joe was caught or confirmed dead. But boys' minds don't stay in one place for long, and before he knew it, Tom was consumed by a new obsession: buried treasure. He found Huck and presented the idea. "Where do we dig?" Huck asked. "That's the thing—treasure is always buried in specific places. Under dead trees, in haunted houses, on islands." "Who buries it?" "Robbers, obviously." "If I had treasure, I'd spend it." "So would I. But robbers always hide it and then forget where they put it. Or they die. Then somebody finds an old yellow map with mysterious markings, and after about a week of working out the clues, they find the treasure." Huck was sold. They grabbed a pick and a shovel and hiked three miles to a dead tree on a hillside. They dug for hours. Nothing. They tried a different spot. Nothing. They tried yet another. "Do they always bury it this deep?" Huck asked, wiping sweat from his face. "Not always. I think we've got the wrong place." They tried two more spots before Tom slapped his forehead. "Wait—you have to find where the shadow of the dead branch falls at midnight, and that's where you dig!" "Then we've wasted the whole day! We have to come back at night." They hid their tools and came back that night by moonlight. It was eerie work—the wind moaning through the trees, the darkness pressing in. They dug where the shadow fell. Still nothing. "I know what we should do," Tom said at last. "We should try the haunted house." Huck's face went pale. "Tom, I don't like haunted houses. They're worse than graveyards. Dead people might talk to you, but ghosts sneak up behind you and breathe on your neck." "Ghosts only come out at night. We'd go in the daytime." Huck thought it over. "All right. But I don't like it."

🤔 Reading Comprehension

Answer these questions about today's chapter. Use evidence from the text to support your answers.

1. According to Tom, where is treasure usually buried?

2. Why do the boys decide they need to come back to the dead tree at midnight?

3. Tom and Huck are full of superstitions about treasure hunting — incantations, witches, midnight shadows. How do these superstitions affect the way they approach the treasure hunt? Do the superstitions help them or hold them back?
