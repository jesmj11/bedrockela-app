📜 THE LAND OF THE DEAD

Circe told Odysseus he must visit the underworld and speak with the prophet Tiresias. At the edge of the world, Odysseus dug a trench, filled it with sacrificial blood, and waited. The dead came — pale, flickering shades. Tiresias warned him: do not harm the sacred cattle of Helios on the island of Thrinacia. "If you touch those cattle, your ship will be destroyed and your men will die." But the spirit who shook Odysseus most was his mother, Anticlea. She had died of grief while he was gone. He tried to embrace her — his arms passed through her shadow like smoke. He understood the full cost of his journey: not just the men he had lost, but the years of his life, spent far from everyone he loved.

📝 Grammar: Subjunctive Mood for Hypotheticals and Wishes

The subjunctive mood expresses wishes, suggestions, or hypothetical situations. It often uses 'were' instead of 'was': 'If I were the king...' Common in formal writing after verbs like wish, suggest, recommend: 'I wish she were here.' 'I suggest that he study harder' (not 'studies').

Example:

Subjunctive: 'If Scheherazade were to fail, she would die.' 'The king wished that his wife were faithful.' Indicative (not subjunctive): 'Scheherazade was clever.'
