These words appear in today's chapter. As you read, notice how they're used in context!

1. desperate

Definition: feeling hopeless and willing to try anything; extremely urgent

2. sputtered

Definition: burned or flickered unsteadily before going out; also, spoke in a confused, choppy way

3. stupor

Definition: a state of near-unconsciousness; being dazed and unable to think clearly

📜 LOST IN THE CAVE (Part 1)

Deep under the earth, Tom and Becky had lost all sense of time. They had tried every passage they could find. They had shouted until their voices were raw. They had listened for sounds of rescue and heard nothing but the echo of their own desperate calls, bouncing off the stone walls like mocking laughter. Their candles were almost gone. Tom had started with one whole candle and three or four pieces. He blew out Becky's candle to save it. She understood what that meant, and the last bit of hope drained from her face. They found a small spring and sat down beside it. Both were exhausted—legs shaking, stomachs gnawing with hunger. "Tom, they'll come looking for us, won't they?" "Of course they will." "When will they notice we're gone?" "When everybody gets back to the boat, I expect." Becky's face changed. "Tom—it might be dark by then. Would they even notice?" Tom couldn't answer that. And then a worse thought struck both of them at the same time: Becky was supposed to be staying at Susy Harper's house. Her mother wouldn't miss her until morning. Maybe not even until Sunday afternoon. They stared at their dying candle. The flame sputtered, rose one last time, and went out. Total darkness. The blackest dark either of them had ever known. Becky cried. Tom held her. Time stopped having any meaning. They slept and woke and slept again. They had no idea if it was day or night, Sunday or Monday. Tom had one piece of cake in his pocket—their "wedding cake" from the day they got engaged. They split it. It only made them hungrier. Tom said they had to keep moving. He had a kite string in his pocket—he tied one end to a rock and played it out as they crept forward, so they could find their way back to the spring if they needed to. He explored one passage, then another. At the end of the third passage, just as the string was about to run out, he reached around a corner— And saw a hand holding a candle. Tom shouted with joy—and then the person stepped into view. It was Injun Joe. Tom's heart stopped. For one frozen moment, the two stared at each other. Then Joe turned and ran, disappearing into the darkness. Tom stood paralyzed. But something was strange—Joe had run away. The echoes must have disguised Tom's voice. Joe didn't know who had shouted. He was hiding in this cave too. Tom crept back to Becky but didn't tell her who he'd seen. He said he'd shouted "just for luck." They rested again, sleeping fitfully, tortured by hunger and thirst and the absolute, crushing darkness. When Becky woke, she told Tom to go on without her. She was too weak. She would wait here and die—it wouldn't take long. Tom kissed her forehead and promised he would come back for her. He took the kite string and crawled into the blackness, feeling his way along the stone walls with his hands and knees.

📝 Grammar: Colons and Dashes for Emphasis

What is it?

A colon introduces a list, explanation, or quotation: 'Tom had one goal: freedom.' A dash creates emphasis or interruption: 'Tom—always clever—found a solution.' Both add style to writing.

Example:

"Colon: 'Tom needed three things: courage, wit, and luck.' / Dash: 'The fence—all thirty yards of it—needed whitewashing.'"
