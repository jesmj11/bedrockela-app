📜 AEOLUS AND THE BAG OF WINDS

Aeolus, keeper of the winds, gave Odysseus a leather bag containing all the unfavorable winds. With the bad winds imprisoned, the gentle west wind carried them straight toward Ithaca. On the tenth day, Odysseus could see home — the rocky cliffs, the smoke from chimneys. He wept with joy. Then he fell asleep. His men, convinced the bag held gold, untied it. Every wind erupted at once. The storm hurled them backward across the ocean. Ithaca vanished. They returned to Aeolus and begged for help. "The gods must hate you," he said. "I won't help a man the gods have cursed."

📝 Language: Etymology: Word Histories and Evolution

Words have histories—they come from other languages, change meanings over time, and carry their origins with them. 'Sinbad' comes from Arabic. 'Scheherazade' is Persian. 'Genie' comes from Arabic 'jinn.' Knowing etymology deepens understanding and makes words memorable.

Example:

'Assassin' comes from Arabic 'hashashiyyin' (users of hashish), from a medieval group. 'Admiral' from Arabic 'amir al-' (commander of). Many English words have Arabic origins through trade and cultural exchange.
