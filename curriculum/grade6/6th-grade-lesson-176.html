📜 THE CATTLE OF THE SUN

Tiresias had warned him. Circe had warned him. Odysseus himself warned his men as the island of Thrinacia appeared on the horizon: "We must not stop on this island. The cattle that graze there belong to Helios, the sun god. If we harm even one, we will die." But the men were exhausted and hungry. Eurylochus spoke for the crew: "You ask too much. We need rest. One night on the beach. We won't touch the cattle." Odysseus relented. One night became a month. Adverse winds trapped them on the island. Their food ran out. Hunger gnawed at them. Odysseus went inland to pray, and while he was gone, the men broke. Eurylochus made the argument: "We are dying. The cattle are right there. If the gods want to kill us for eating, at least we'll die with full stomachs." They slaughtered the finest cattle of the sun god. When Odysseus returned and saw the fire, smelled the meat, he knew they were dead. Helios demanded justice from Zeus. They sailed from the island with full stomachs and doomed souls. Within hours, Zeus sent a thunderbolt that shattered the ship. Every man was thrown into the sea. Every man drowned. Except Odysseus. He clung to the wreckage — the keel and a piece of the mast — and floated alone on the open sea. Every ship, every man, every friend he had led from Troy was gone. The prophecy of Tiresias had come true. Odysseus was alone.

📝 Grammar: Misplaced and Dangling Modifiers

Modifiers should be placed next to what they modify. Misplaced: 'She told a story to the king with great skill' (the king doesn't have the skill—she does). Dangling: 'Walking through the palace, the stories came to mind' (stories can't walk). Correct: 'Walking through the palace, she thought of stories.'

Example:

Misplaced: 'The merchant found treasure in the cave that was priceless' (the cave or the treasure?). Better: 'The merchant found priceless treasure in the cave.' Dangling: 'After telling the story, the king was impressed.' Better: 'After she told the story, the king was impressed.'
