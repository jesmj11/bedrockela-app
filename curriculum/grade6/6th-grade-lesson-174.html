📜 THE CATTLE OF THE SUN

On the island of Thrinacia, Helios kept his sacred cattle. Odysseus wanted to sail past, but his starving men forced a landing. He made them swear not to touch the cattle. A month-long storm trapped them. Their food ran out. While Odysseus prayed alone, Eurylochus convinced the men to slaughter the cattle. "Better to die with full bellies." When Zeus hurled his thunderbolt, the ship split apart. Every man who had eaten the sacred cattle drowned. Odysseus alone survived, clinging to a piece of the mast for nine days, until the current carried him to Calypso's island — where he would be held prisoner for seven years.

📝 Language: Tone and Mood: Language Creates Atmosphere

Tone is the author's attitude toward the subject (humorous, serious, ironic, respectful). Mood is the feeling created for the reader (suspenseful, hopeful, melancholy). Authors create tone and mood through word choice, sentence structure, imagery, and pacing.

Example:

Serious tone: 'The king's decree devastated the kingdom.' Ironic tone: 'The king's "justice" brought only misery.' The quotation marks create irony. Suspenseful mood: 'The dawn approached. Would the king demand she continue or order her death?'
