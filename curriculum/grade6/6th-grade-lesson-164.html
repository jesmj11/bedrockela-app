📜 THE CYCLOPS

The next island seemed like a gift — wild and fertile, with fat goats and enormous caves. Odysseus took twelve men and a skin of fine wine to explore. They found a cave filled with cheeses and milk. The owner came home at dusk: Polyphemus, a one-eyed Cyclops, son of Poseidon. He rolled a boulder across the entrance, trapping them inside. Then he grabbed two men, smashed them against the floor, and ate them. Odysseus couldn't kill the sleeping giant — the boulder was too massive for the men to move. So he made a plan. He offered the Cyclops his powerful wine and told him his name was "Nobody." When Polyphemus passed out drunk, they drove a sharpened stake into his eye. Other Cyclopes came running. "Who is hurting you?" — "Nobody is hurting me!" They shrugged and left. In the morning, Odysseus's men escaped by clinging to the undersides of the giant's sheep.

📝 Language: Idioms, Colloquialisms, and Cultural Expressions

Idioms are expressions whose meanings aren't literal: 'Break the ice' doesn't mean breaking actual ice. Understanding cultural expressions helps comprehension and shows how language reflects culture. Some expressions translate across cultures; others don't.

Example:

'The king lost his head' (lost control, not literally lost his head). 'She kept him in the dark' (kept him uninformed). Cultural expression: 'A thousand and one nights' suggests something vast, uncountable—the title itself is an idiom.
