📜 THE SIRENS

The Sirens sang a song so beautiful that any man who heard it would steer toward the rocks and die. Odysseus ordered his men to seal their ears with beeswax and bind him to the mast. The song was not just music — it was knowledge. The Sirens sang of everything and called him by name. Odysseus screamed, thrashed, and begged to be released. His deaf men rowed harder and tied him tighter. He had heard the Sirens' song and survived. But he would hear it in his dreams for the rest of his life.

📝 Language: Semantic Fields and Word Families

Semantic fields are groups of words related by meaning: words for 'saying' (whisper, shout, murmur, declare, announce), 'intelligence' (clever, wise, shrewd, cunning, astute). Authors vary words within semantic fields to create precise meanings and avoid repetition.

Example:

Instead of repeating 'said,' the author might use: 'whispered,' 'announced,' 'murmured,' 'declared'—each adds different meaning (volume, formality, emotion). Each word in the semantic field of 'speaking' creates a different effect.
