📜 Getting Ready (Part 1)

Jack knew he needed to train harder than ever before. Every morning, he would wake up early to practice his jumping. He started with small objects and worked his way up to bigger ones.

He jumped over fence posts, then over hay bales, then over his neighbor's gate. Each day, Jack could jump a little higher than the day before. His legs were getting stronger.

📜 Getting Ready (Part 2)

Jack also practiced his running. He knew he would need to run very fast to get enough speed for such a high jump. He ran through the fields and around the village every day.

After weeks of training, Jack felt ready. His friends gathered to watch as he stood in front of the tall candlestick. Tonight would be the night he would try his greatest jump.

📰 Exercise

Exercise is physical activity that makes our bodies stronger, healthier, and more flexible. When we exercise, our hearts pump faster, our muscles work harder, and our breathing increases. Regular exercise helps build strong bones and muscles, improves our balance and coordination, and gives us more energy for daily activities. There are many fun ways to exercise, like playing sports, riding bikes, swimming, dancing, or even playing active games at recess. Exercise also helps our brains work better by increasing blood flow and oxygen to help us think more clearly and concentrate in school. Additionally, physical activity can help us feel happier and less stressed by releasing natural chemicals in our brains that improve our mood.
