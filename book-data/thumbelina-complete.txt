Chapter 1: A Tiny Beginning
Page 1
Once upon a time, there lived a kind woman who desperately wanted a child of her own to love and cherish. She visited a wise old fairy who gave her a special barleycorn seed. "Plant this carefully," instructed the fairy mysteriously, "and your deepest wish may come true in the most unexpected way."
Page 2
The woman planted the magical seed in a beautiful flowerpot and tended it with great care and patience. Soon, a magnificent flower grew from the earth, unlike any bloom she had ever seen before. The flower was enormous and perfectly formed, with delicate petals that shimmered like silk in the morning sunlight.
Page 3
When the flower finally opened its petals completely, the woman gasped in amazement and delight. Sitting in the very center of the bloom was the tiniest, most perfect little girl she had ever imagined. The child was no bigger than the woman's thumb, with golden hair and eyes that sparkled like precious jewels.
Page 4
The woman named her tiny daughter Thumbelina because of her remarkably small size. She created a miniature bed from a polished walnut shell, with soft violet petals for blankets and a rose petal for a pillow. Thumbelina was perfectly content in her cozy home, and the woman finally had the beloved child she had always dreamed of having.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. cherish - to love and take care of something very precious
2. miniature - very small; a tiny version of something

	•	A beautiful flower
2. Why was the little girl named Thumbelina?

	•	She was no bigger than a thumb

Chapter 2: Kidnapped by a Toad
Page 1
One peaceful summer night, as Thumbelina slept soundly in her walnut shell bed, an enormous, ugly toad crept silently through the open window. The greedy toad had been watching the tiny girl for several days, planning to steal her away to be a bride for her equally unpleasant son who lived in the muddy swamp.
Page 2
Without making a sound, the crafty toad carefully lifted Thumbelina's entire walnut shell bed and carried it away into the darkness. The sleeping child never knew she was being transported far from her beloved home until she awakened the next morning to find herself stranded on a lily pad in the middle of a murky pond.
Page 3
"Perfect!" croaked the old toad triumphantly from the muddy bank. "You will make a wonderful wife for my handsome son! You cannot escape from that lily pad, so you must stay there until we prepare for your wedding ceremony." Thumbelina was terrified and began to cry desperately, longing to return to her comfortable home.
Page 4
The kind fish swimming beneath the lily pad heard Thumbelina's pitiful sobs and took pity on the frightened little girl. Working together with their sharp teeth, they quietly gnawed through the thick stem of the lily pad, setting it free to float downstream like a tiny boat carrying Thumbelina far away from the horrible toads.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. stranded - left alone in a place with no way to escape
2. gnawed - chewed or bit through something
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions

	•	To make her work
2. How did Thumbelina escape from the lily pad?

	•	Fish gnawed through the lily pad stem
	•	The wind blew her away

Chapter 3: Adventures with the Beetle
Page 1
As Thumbelina floated downstream on her lily pad boat, a magnificent cockchafer beetle with shiny golden wings swooped down from a nearby tree branch. "What a curious little creature!" he exclaimed with great interest. "You are quite unusual, but I find you absolutely charming!" He lifted Thumbelina gently and carried her up into his tree home.
Page 2
The beetle treated Thumbelina very kindly at first, offering her sweet nectar from flowers and soft moss to rest upon. But when his family and friends came to visit, they were not nearly as welcoming as he had been. "She's so strange!" they whispered disapprovingly. "She has no proper wings, and she doesn't look like us at all!"
Page 3
The other beetles criticized Thumbelina's appearance so harshly that the poor cockchafer beetle began to doubt his own judgment. Though he had initially found her beautiful and interesting, he started to believe that perhaps his friends and family were right, and that Thumbelina was indeed too different to fit in with their community.
Page 4
Feeling embarrassed and uncertain, the beetle decided he could no longer keep Thumbelina with him. He flew her down to the forest floor and left her there alone, apologizing sadly but explaining that she simply didn't belong in his world. Thumbelina found herself abandoned once again, with nowhere to go and no one to care for her.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. disapprovingly - in a way that shows you don't like or approve of something
2. criticized - said negative things about someone or something
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions

	•	His friends and family criticized her appearance
	•	She wanted to leave

Chapter 4: A Long, Difficult Winter
Page 1
All through the warm summer and colorful autumn months, poor Thumbelina lived alone in the forest, struggling to survive on her own. She wove herself a tiny bed from grass and hung it underneath a large leaf for protection from rain. She drank dewdrops and ate nectar from flowers, but life was lonely and increasingly difficult.
Page 2
When winter arrived with its bitter cold and heavy snow, Thumbelina's situation became truly desperate. The flowers died, the leaves fell from the trees, and there was no food to be found anywhere in the frozen forest. She wrapped herself in a dead leaf, but it provided little warmth against the harsh winter weather.
Page 3
Nearly frozen and weak from hunger, Thumbelina stumbled through the snow until she discovered a small hole in the ground that led to the underground home of a field mouse. The kind mouse took pity on the shivering, starving girl and invited her inside where it was warm and cozy, with plenty of stored food for the winter months.
Page 4
"You may stay here through the winter," offered the generous field mouse, "but you must earn your keep by cleaning my home and telling me stories to pass the long, dark days." Thumbelina gratefully accepted this arrangement, relieved to have found shelter and safety from the dangerous winter storms that raged outside.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. desperate - in a very bad situation with little hope
2. arrangement - an agreement or plan between people
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

	•	Cook all the meals

Chapter 5: The Swallow's Secret
Page 1
One day while exploring the underground tunnels near the field mouse's home, Thumbelina discovered a swallow lying motionless on the cold earth. The poor bird appeared to be dead, with his beautiful feathers ruffled and his wings folded tightly against his body. Thumbelina felt great sympathy for the unfortunate creature.
Page 2
But when Thumbelina gently placed her ear against the swallow's breast, she heard the faint sound of a heartbeat! The bird was not dead at all, but nearly frozen from the terrible cold. Every day thereafter, Thumbelina secretly visited the swallow, bringing him water and tiny pieces of food, and covering him with soft materials to keep him warm.
Page 3
Through Thumbelina's patient, loving care, the swallow gradually regained his strength and health. As he recovered, he told her wonderful stories about the warm countries he had visited during his travels, describing beautiful flowers, bright sunshine, and magical places where it was never cold or dark for long periods of time.
Page 4
When spring finally arrived and the swallow was completely well again, he offered to carry Thumbelina away to those warm, beautiful lands he had described so vividly. "Come with me," he urged kindly. "I can show you places where you will be happy and free, far from this cold underground world where you don't truly belong."
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. sympathy - feeling sorry for someone who is suffering
2. vividly - in a very clear and detailed way
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions

	•	Bring her food every day

Chapter 6: Finding Her True Home
Page 1
Thumbelina accepted the swallow's generous offer with immense joy and excitement. She climbed carefully onto his back and held tightly to his soft feathers as they soared high above the earth, leaving behind the dark underground world where she had spent the long, difficult winter months. Below them, the countryside looked like a beautiful patchwork quilt.
Page 2
They flew for many days over mountains, rivers, and vast forests until they reached a land where the sun always shone brightly and exotic flowers bloomed everywhere in magnificent colors. The swallow gently set Thumbelina down in the center of the most spectacular garden she had ever seen, filled with blossoms of every imaginable hue and fragrance.
Page 3
As Thumbelina explored this enchanted garden with wonder and delight, she discovered that she was not alone. Living among the flowers were other tiny people exactly her size, each with delicate wings like butterflies and kind, welcoming smiles. They were flower fairies, and they had been waiting to meet someone just like Thumbelina for a very long time.
Page 4
The king of the flower fairies offered Thumbelina a pair of beautiful transparent wings and a crown of the finest flower petals. "Welcome home," he said warmly. "You belong here with us, where you will never be too different or too small." Thumbelina finally found her true family and lived happily ever after in the magical garden where she perfectly belonged.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. immense - extremely large or great
2. transparent - clear; you can see through it
