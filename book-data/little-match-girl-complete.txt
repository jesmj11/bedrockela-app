Chapter 1: A Cold New Year's Eve
Page 1
It was New Year's Eve, and the winter snow fell heavily on the quiet city streets. The cold wind whistled through the narrow alleys, and most people were safe inside their warm homes, preparing to celebrate the arrival of the new year with their loving families and friends.
Page 2
But not everyone had a warm place to go. A little girl walked slowly through the snowy streets, carrying a small basket filled with matches to sell. Her thin clothes provided little protection from the bitter cold, and her worn shoes had holes that let the icy snow touch her freezing feet.
Page 3
The little match girl had been walking all day, trying desperately to sell her matches so she could earn enough money to buy food and perhaps find shelter for the night. But the busy people hurried past her without stopping, too focused on their own holiday preparations to notice the shivering child.
Page 4
As darkness began to fall and the street lamps flickered on one by one, the little girl realized she had not sold a single match all day. Her hands were numb from the cold, and her stomach ached with hunger. She was afraid to go home because she had no money to show for her long day of work.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. desperately - with great need and urgency; in a hopeless way
2. shivering - shaking from cold or fear

	•	Matches
	•	Candy
2. Why was the little girl afraid to go home?
	•	She was lost
	•	She had no money to show for her work
	•	It was too dark

Chapter 2: Finding Shelter
Page 1
As the night grew colder and more snow began to fall, the little match girl looked for a place to rest and escape the harsh winter wind. She discovered a small corner between two tall buildings where the walls provided some protection from the worst of the bitter cold and swirling snow.
Page 2
The little girl curled up in the narrow space, pulling her thin coat tightly around her small body. She tucked her freezing hands under her arms and tried to warm her numb fingers, but the cold seemed to seep through everything she wore, chilling her to the very bone.
Page 3
Around her, she could see the warm golden light glowing from the windows of nearby houses. Inside those cozy homes, families were gathered around crackling fireplaces, sharing delicious meals and laughing together as they prepared to welcome the new year with joy and celebration.
Page 4
The little match girl pressed her face against the cold brick wall and closed her eyes, wishing with all her heart that she could be warm and safe inside one of those happy homes. But she knew she had to stay where she was, hoping that somehow she would make it through this long, freezing night.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. harsh - very severe and difficult to bear
2. cozy - warm, comfortable, and inviting
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. Where did the little girl find shelter?
	•	In a warm house
	•	In a corner between two buildings
	•	Under a bridge
2. What could the little girl see from her hiding place?
	•	Other children playing
	•	Warm golden light from windows of houses
	•	Snow-covered trees

Chapter 3: The First Match
Page 1
As the cold grew more unbearable, the little match girl remembered the matches in her basket. "Perhaps," she thought hopefully, "if I light just one small match, it might warm my frozen hands for a few precious moments." She knew she should save them to sell, but the cold was becoming too much to endure.
Page 2
With her trembling fingers, she carefully took one match from her basket and struck it against the rough brick wall. The tiny flame burst to life, creating a small circle of warm, bright light that danced and flickered in the cold winter air around her numb hands.
Page 3
As the little girl stared into the glowing flame, something magical began to happen. The small light seemed to grow larger and brighter, and suddenly she could see the most wonderful vision appearing before her eyes. She saw a beautiful iron stove, with a warm fire burning inside and heat radiating outward.
Page 4
The little match girl reached out her cold hands toward the wonderful warmth of the imaginary stove, feeling as if the heat was actually touching her frozen fingers and bringing life back to her numb body. For those brief, magical moments, she felt perfectly warm and comfortable for the first time all day.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. unbearable - too difficult or painful to accept or endure
2. radiating - spreading out in all directions from a central point
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. Why did the little girl decide to light a match?
	•	To see in the dark
	•	The cold was becoming too much to endure
	•	She wanted to play with fire
2. What vision did she see in the match flame?
	•	A warm iron stove
	•	Her family
	•	A beautiful garden

Chapter 4: More Magical Visions
Page 1
But the first match burned out quickly, and the wonderful vision of the warm stove vanished completely, leaving the little girl colder than before. The bitter wind returned, and her fingers began to freeze again. Desperate for more warmth and comfort, she decided to light another precious match from her dwindling supply.
Page 2
The second match flared to life with an even brighter flame, and this time the little girl saw an incredible feast spread before her. There was a magnificent roasted goose, golden brown and steaming hot, sitting on a beautiful china platter surrounded by delicious vegetables and fresh bread that smelled absolutely heavenly.
Page 3
The little match girl's empty stomach rumbled loudly as she imagined tasting the wonderful meal. She could almost feel the warm, delicious food filling her belly and giving her strength. The vision seemed so real that she reached out her hand, trying to touch the magnificent feast that appeared before her hungry eyes.
Page 4
But once again, the match burned down to her fingertips and went out, taking the beautiful vision of food with it. The little girl was left alone in the cold corner, hungrier than ever before. Yet despite her disappointment, she felt a small spark of hope growing in her heart from these magical moments.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. dwindling - becoming smaller or fewer; running out
2. magnificent - extremely beautiful and impressive
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. What happened when the first match burned out?
	•	The vision continued
	•	The vision vanished and she felt colder
	•	Someone came to help her
2. What did the little girl see in the second match?
	•	Another stove
	•	A magnificent feast with roasted goose
	•	A warm bed

Chapter 5: A Special Vision
Page 1
The little match girl lit a third match, and this time the flame revealed the most beautiful Christmas tree she had ever seen. It was tall and majestic, decorated with thousands of sparkling candles, colorful ornaments, and shimmering tinsel that caught the light and danced like tiny stars in the darkness.
Page 2
Beneath the glorious tree were piles of wrapped presents in bright colors, tied with beautiful ribbons and bows. The little girl imagined what wonderful surprises might be hidden inside those tempting packages, and for a moment she felt the joy and excitement that Christmas morning brings to fortunate children around the world.
Page 3
As she gazed at the enchanting scene, the little match girl remembered happier times from her past when she was younger and her beloved grandmother was still alive. Grandmother had always made Christmas special with homemade cookies, warm hugs, and stories told by the fireplace on cold winter evenings just like this one.
Page 4
The memory of her dear grandmother filled the little girl's heart with both sadness and comfort. Grandmother had been the only person who had ever truly loved and cared for her, and she missed her gentle voice and kind smile more than anything else in the whole world. These precious memories helped warm her heart even when her body was cold.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. majestic - very grand and impressive; having great dignity
2. enchanting - delightfully charming and magical
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions

	•	Another feast

	•	A warm house
2. Who did the Christmas tree remind the little girl of?
	•	Her mother
	•	Her beloved grandmother
	•	Her sister

Chapter 6: A Peaceful End
Page 1
As the little match girl lit her fourth and final match, something truly miraculous happened. In the bright, warm glow of the flame, she saw her dear grandmother appearing before her, looking just as kind and loving as she remembered. Grandmother's gentle face was filled with compassion and her arms were opened wide with welcome.
Page 2
"Come with me, my precious child," whispered Grandmother softly. "You will never be cold or hungry or lonely again. I will take you to a place where it is always warm and bright, where there is plenty of food to eat, and where you will be surrounded by love and happiness forever."
Page 3
The little match girl reached out eagerly toward her beloved grandmother, and as their hands touched, she felt a wonderful warmth spreading through her entire body. All the pain and cold and hunger melted away completely, replaced by the most peaceful and comfortable feeling she had ever experienced in her short, difficult life.
Page 4
When people found the little match girl the next morning, she was smiling peacefully, surrounded by the burned matches that had brought her such beautiful visions during her final night. Though her earthly journey had ended, her spirit had found the warmth, love, and comfort that had been missing from her life, and she was finally at peace.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. miraculous - amazing and wonderful; like a miracle
2. compassion - deep care and sympathy for someone who is suffering
