Chapter 1: A House Full of Mice
Page 1
In a large old house with many dark corners, hidden passageways, and forgotten storage rooms, there lived a thriving community of mice who had made themselves quite comfortable in their cozy home. These clever little creatures had established an entire civilization within the walls, complete with families, neighborhoods, and well-worn paths that connected all the best sources of food and shelter throughout the building.
Page 2
The mice had organized themselves into a sophisticated society where everyone had important roles to play. Some mice served as scouts, venturing out to find new sources of food and reporting back on any dangers they discovered. Others worked as gatherers, collecting crumbs and storing them in secret pantries for times when food might be scarce. The elder mice shared their wisdom about avoiding traps and predators.
Page 3
Life was generally quite pleasant for the mouse community. They had learned to time their activities carefully, venturing out to search for food only when the house was quiet and still. During the day, they would rest safely in their hidden nests, and at night, they would emerge to explore the kitchen, dining room, and pantry for any tasty morsels that might have been dropped or left behind.
Page 4
The mice had grown confident and perhaps a bit careless in their comfortable routine. They had been living in the house for so many generations that they felt perfectly safe and secure in their established territories. Little did they know that their peaceful existence was about to be dramatically disrupted by the arrival of a new resident who would change everything about their quiet, orderly lives.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. thriving - growing and doing well
2. sophisticated - complex and well-organized

2. How did the mice feel about their situation?

	•	Confident and perhaps a bit careless
	•	Angry and upset

Chapter 2: A New and Dangerous Resident
Page 1
The peaceful existence of the mouse community came to an abrupt end on the day when the human owners of the house decided they needed help controlling what they saw as a growing pest problem. They brought home a large, sleek black cat with bright green eyes and razor-sharp claws - a natural predator who had been specifically chosen for her reputation as an excellent mouse hunter.
Page 2
This cat was no ordinary house pet who might be satisfied with sleeping in sunny spots and playing with toy mice. She was a serious, dedicated hunter who took her job very seriously and possessed all the skills and instincts needed to be extremely effective at catching real mice. Her keen senses could detect the slightest movement or the faintest sound that might indicate the presence of prey.
Page 3
The cat immediately set about establishing her dominance over the house, patrolling every room and corridor with the methodical thoroughness of a professional. She investigated every possible hiding place, sniffed at every crack and crevice, and memorized the locations of all the mouse highways and gathering spots that the previous residents had used for so many years without fear of discovery.
Page 4
Within just a few days of the cat's arrival, the mouse community's comfortable way of life had been completely shattered. Mice who had once moved freely through the house now found themselves trapped in their hiding places, afraid to venture out even for essential food gathering. The cat's presence had transformed their safe home into a dangerous territory where every movement carried the risk of capture or worse.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. predator - an animal that hunts and eats other animals
2. dominance - power and control over others
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions

	•	To catch birds in the garden
2. How did the cat's arrival change life for the mice?

	•	Their safe home became a dangerous territory
	•	It made their lives easier

Chapter 3: A Desperate Council Meeting
Page 1
As the situation grew more desperate with each passing day, the wisest and most experienced mice in the community decided that something drastic had to be done. They called for an emergency council meeting, sending secret messages through their network of tunnels and hidden passages to gather all the mice together in the deepest, most secure part of their underground territory.
Page 2
Mice came from every corner of the house to attend this urgent gathering - young mice who were learning to fend for themselves, middle-aged mice with families to protect, and elderly mice whose long experience had taught them to recognize truly serious threats. They assembled in a large chamber behind the kitchen wall, speaking in hushed whispers about the terrible danger that now controlled their home.
Page 3
The eldest mouse, whose gray whiskers and careful movements showed his many years of survival experience, called the meeting to order. "My friends," he said solemnly, "we face the greatest threat our community has ever encountered. This cat is not like the lazy house cats we've heard about in other homes. She is a dedicated hunter who will not rest until she has made our lives impossible."
Page 4
Many mice shared stories of narrow escapes and dangerous encounters with the cat. Some had lost friends and family members to her quick reflexes and sharp claws. Others reported that entire sections of the house were now completely off-limits because the cat had learned to patrol them so thoroughly. The mood in the chamber grew more grim as each mouse described how the cat had disrupted their normal routines and threatened their survival.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. drastic - extreme and likely to cause big changes
2. encountered - met or faced
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. Why did the mice call an emergency council meeting?
	•	To plan a party
	•	Because they faced a desperate situation with the cat
	•	To discuss the weather
2. What did the eldest mouse say about this cat?
	•	That she was friendly and harmless
	•	That she was a dedicated hunter, unlike lazy house cats
	•	That she would leave soon

Chapter 4: A Bold Proposal
Page 1
After listening to all the reports and complaints about their dangerous situation, a young mouse who was known for his creative thinking stood up and addressed the worried assembly. "I believe I have a solution to our problem," he announced with more confidence than most of the older mice felt was appropriate given their dire circumstances. "We need an early warning system that will tell us when the cat is approaching."
Page 2
The young mouse continued to explain his ingenious plan. "What we need is to put a bell around the cat's neck. Then, whenever she moves, the bell will ring and warn us of her approach. We'll be able to hear her coming from far away and have plenty of time to hide safely in our burrows. No longer will she be able to sneak up on us and catch us by surprise."
Page 3
The proposal created immediate excitement throughout the chamber. Mice began chattering enthusiastically about how brilliant this plan was and how it would solve all their problems at once. They could imagine themselves moving freely through the house again, knowing that the ringing bell would always give them advance notice when danger was near. It seemed like the perfect solution to their terrible predicament.
Page 4
The mice began planning how wonderful their lives would be once the cat wore a warning bell. They could resume their normal activities, venture out to search for food without fear, and enjoy the comfortable existence they had known before this dangerous predator arrived. The more they thought about it, the more convinced they became that this young mouse had found the answer to all their troubles.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. dire - extremely serious or dangerous
2. ingenious - very clever and creative
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. What was the young mouse's solution?
	•	To move to a different house
	•	To put a bell around the cat's neck for early warning
	•	To make friends with the cat
2. How did the other mice react to this proposal?
	•	They were confused and worried
	•	They became excited and thought it was brilliant
	•	They completely ignored the idea

Chapter 5: A Crucial Question
Page 1
While the mice continued to celebrate what they believed was the perfect solution to their problem, an old mouse who had been quietly listening to the discussion slowly raised his paw to request attention. This elderly mouse was respected throughout the community for his practical wisdom and his ability to see potential problems that others might overlook in their enthusiasm for new ideas.
Page 2
"Young mouse," the elder said in a gentle but serious voice that commanded the attention of the entire assembly, "your idea about the bell is certainly very clever, and I can see how it would solve our problem beautifully if it could be accomplished. But there is one small detail that I think we need to consider carefully before we celebrate this solution."
Page 3
The chamber fell completely silent as all the mice turned their attention to the wise old mouse, sensing that he was about to raise an important point that might affect their wonderful plan. The elder looked around at all the eager faces and asked the question that would change everything: "Who exactly is going to put the bell around the cat's neck?"
Page 4
This simple question hit the assembly like a thunderbolt, instantly transforming their excitement into stunned silence. Every mouse suddenly realized the obvious problem they had overlooked in their enthusiasm: approaching the dangerous cat closely enough to attach a bell would be incredibly risky, requiring someone to get within reach of those sharp claws and quick reflexes that had already claimed several members of their community.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. accomplished - successfully completed or achieved
2. thunderbolt - a sudden shocking surprise
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. What important question did the old mouse ask?
	•	Where can we buy a bell?
	•	Who exactly is going to put the bell around the cat's neck?
	•	What kind of bell should they use?
2. How did the mice react to this question?
	•	They became even more excited
	•	Their excitement turned to stunned silence
	•	They started laughing loudly

Chapter 6: The Difference Between Ideas and Action
Page 1
The mice looked around at each other nervously, each hoping that someone else would volunteer for the dangerous mission of approaching the cat. But as the silence stretched on, it became clear that no mouse was willing to risk their life by getting close enough to the deadly predator to attach a bell to her collar, no matter how much they all agreed that it was a wonderful idea in theory.
Page 2
The young mouse who had proposed the plan began to realize that his brilliant solution had a serious flaw. While the idea of using a bell was indeed clever and would work perfectly if it could be implemented, the practical reality of actually carrying out the plan made it essentially impossible. Good ideas, he was learning, are only valuable if they can actually be put into practice.
Page 3
The wise old mouse nodded thoughtfully as he watched the other mice come to this important realization. "This teaches us a valuable lesson," he said gently. "It's easy to propose solutions to difficult problems, but we must always consider whether our ideas can actually be carried out safely and successfully. Sometimes the most brilliant plans are useless if no one can implement them."
Page 4
The council meeting ended without a solution to their cat problem, but the mice had learned something important about the difference between having good ideas and being able to act on them. They realized they would need to be more careful and practical in their thinking, focusing on plans that could actually be carried out rather than just ideas that sounded good but were too dangerous to implement. Sometimes wisdom lies in recognizing the limitations of even our best ideas.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. implemented - put into action; carried out
2. limitations - restrictions or weaknesses that prevent something from working
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions

	•	The cat was too small
2. What lesson did the mice learn?
	•	That cats are always friendly
	•	That good ideas are only valuable if they can actually be put into practice
	•	That bells don't work
