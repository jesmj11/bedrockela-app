Chapter 1: A Mother's Warning
Page 1
In a cozy burrow beneath the roots of a magnificent fir tree at the edge of a peaceful woodland, there lived a devoted mother rabbit named Mrs. Rabbit who had dedicated her life to raising her four beloved children with wisdom, patience, and unwavering maternal care. Her three well-behaved daughters - Flopsy, Mopsy, and Cotton-tail - consistently demonstrated the kind of obedient behavior and respectful attitudes that filled their mother's heart with pride and confidence about their future success in navigating the complex challenges of rabbit society.
Page 2
However, Mrs. Rabbit's youngest child, a mischievous little buck named Peter, possessed a restless spirit and an unfortunate tendency toward impulsive behavior that caused his mother considerable anxiety and concern about his safety and future welfare. While his sisters listened attentively to maternal guidance and followed household rules with commendable consistency, Peter seemed constitutionally incapable of accepting boundaries or acknowledging the wisdom of parental authority in matters of personal security and appropriate conduct.
Page 3
On this particular morning, as Mrs. Rabbit prepared to venture out on important errands that would require her to leave her children unsupervised for several hours, she gathered her four offspring around her for a serious discussion about expected behavior and absolute safety requirements during her absence. The gravity of her tone and the intensity of her maternal concern made it clear that this was not merely routine advice, but rather crucial instructions that could mean the difference between safety and disaster.
Page 4
"Now, my dear children," Mrs. Rabbit began with solemn authority, "you may play freely in the meadow and explore the safe areas of our woodland home, but you must never, under any circumstances, venture near Mr. McGregor's vegetable garden. That dangerous place cost your poor father his life when he became trapped there, and I will not risk losing any of you to the same terrible fate that befell him." Her voice trembled with emotion as she recalled the tragic loss that had shaped their family's cautious approach to survival.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. constitutionally - by basic nature or character; fundamentally
2. befell - happened to; occurred to someone

Chapter 2: The Fatal Temptation
Page 1
As soon as Mrs. Rabbit departed on her necessary business errands and disappeared from sight down the woodland path, Peter's three sisters demonstrated their mature understanding of maternal expectations by gathering blackberries in the approved meadow area where they could enjoy themselves safely while remaining well within the boundaries that their mother had clearly established for their protection. Their responsible behavior reflected not only their respect for parental authority, but also their practical understanding of the serious consequences that could result from reckless disobedience.
Page 2
Peter, however, possessed a fundamentally different temperament that made him constitutionally incapable of accepting restrictions on his personal freedom, regardless of how reasonable or well-intentioned those limitations might be. The moment his mother was out of sight, he began to contemplate the forbidden territory that she had specifically warned him to avoid, and the more he thought about Mr. McGregor's vegetable garden, the more irresistibly attractive it became to his rebellious and adventurous spirit.
Page 3
The psychological dynamics of forbidden fruit began to exert their powerful influence over Peter's decision-making processes as he imagined the delicious vegetables that must be growing in abundance within Mr. McGregor's carefully cultivated garden. Fresh lettuce, crisp radishes, tender green beans, and other mouth-watering delicacies seemed to call to him with irresistible appeal, drowning out the voice of caution that weakly reminded him of his mother's stern warnings and the tragic fate that had befallen his father.
Page 4
Unable to resist the mounting pressure of curiosity and appetite that overwhelmed his better judgment, Peter made the fateful decision to abandon his mother's explicit instructions and venture toward the dangerous territory that had been declared absolutely off-limits for compelling reasons of personal safety. As he hopped away from the secure meadow where his sisters continued their innocent berry-picking activities, he felt a mixture of excitement and nervous apprehension about the transgressive adventure he was about to undertake despite knowing it violated every principle of obedience and family loyalty.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. irresistibly - in a way that cannot be resisted; very tempting
2. transgressive - violating rules or accepted behavior

Chapter 3: Entering Forbidden Territory
Page 1
When Peter finally arrived at the imposing stone wall that surrounded Mr. McGregor's meticulously maintained vegetable garden, he discovered a small gap beneath the wooden gate that provided just enough space for a determined young rabbit to squeeze through and gain unauthorized access to the forbidden paradise that lay beyond. His heart pounded with a mixture of excitement and trepidation as he contemplated this crucial moment of decision, knowing that crossing this threshold would represent a definitive act of disobedience that could not be undone or explained away.
Page 2
Despite the warning voice of conscience that whispered reminders about his mother's explicit instructions and his father's tragic fate, Peter's curiosity and appetite proved stronger than his sense of caution and filial duty. He wriggled through the narrow opening with determined persistence, emerging on the other side into a wonderland of agricultural abundance that exceeded even his most optimistic expectations about the rewards that awaited those brave enough to venture into prohibited territory.
Page 3
The garden stretched before Peter like a magnificent buffet of natural delicacies, with neat rows of perfectly cultivated vegetables displaying their tempting wares in irresistible profusion. Crisp lettuce leaves glistened with morning dew, plump radishes peeked invitingly from the rich earth, and tender young carrots promised sweet satisfaction to any creature bold enough to harvest them. The sensory overload of colors, textures, and anticipated flavors overwhelmed Peter's already weakened resistance to temptation.
Page 4
Without further hesitation or consideration of potential consequences, Peter began his illicit feast by consuming enormous quantities of fresh lettuce, followed by several French beans, and topped off with a generous helping of radishes that satisfied his appetite while simultaneously increasing his sense of triumphant rebellion against maternal authority. The immediate pleasure of forbidden fruit seemed to justify his disobedient behavior, and for a brief moment, he congratulated himself on his bold decision to ignore his mother's restrictive and apparently unnecessary warnings.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. trepidation - a feeling of fear or anxiety about something that may happen
2. illicit - forbidden by law, rules, or custom

Chapter 4: The Consequences of Gluttony
Page 1
Unfortunately for Peter's continued enjoyment of his unauthorized feast, his greedy overindulgence in the garden's abundant offerings began to produce uncomfortable physiological consequences that transformed his initial pleasure into increasing physical distress. His stomach, unaccustomed to such rich fare and certainly not prepared for the excessive quantities he had consumed with such reckless abandon, began to rebel against this sudden dietary overload with painful cramping and nauseous sensations that grew progressively worse with each passing moment.
Page 2
As Peter's digestive discomfort intensified and his earlier sense of triumphant rebellion gave way to genuine physical misery, he began to understand why moderation and maternal guidance might serve important purposes beyond mere arbitrary restriction of youthful freedom. The immediate gratification he had pursued so eagerly was proving to be a hollow victory that carried with it a painful price that he had failed to consider during his impulsive decision-making process.
Page 3
While Peter was struggling with the unpleasant consequences of his gluttonous behavior and beginning to reconsider the wisdom of his rebellious adventure, the sound of heavy footsteps approaching through the garden paths suddenly penetrated his self-absorbed preoccupation with digestive distress. The rhythmic thump-thump of boots on earth grew steadily louder and more ominous, accompanied by the unmistakable sound of gardening tools being carried by someone who was clearly engaged in serious agricultural work.
Page 4
Terror replaced stomach pain as Peter realized that his worst fears were about to be realized: Mr. McGregor himself was approaching the very section of garden where the young rabbit had been conducting his illicit feast, and discovery seemed inevitable given Peter's weakened condition and limited mobility due to his overindulgence. The moment of reckoning that his mother's warnings had been designed to prevent was now imminent, and Peter understood with crystal clarity that his disobedience had placed him in exactly the kind of mortal danger that had claimed his father's life.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. physiological - relating to the body's physical and biological functions
2. imminent - about to happen very soon

Chapter 5: A Desperate Chase
Page 1
The appearance of Mr. McGregor himself, armed with a hoe and displaying the kind of determined expression that suggested serious intent to protect his agricultural investment from unauthorized raiders, transformed Peter's uncomfortable situation into a life-threatening crisis that required immediate evasive action. "Stop, thief!" roared the angry gardener as soon as he spotted the trespassing rabbit, his voice carrying across the peaceful garden with such fury and determination that Peter's blood froze with terror at the realization that his mother's worst fears were being fulfilled.
Page 2
Despite his physical discomfort and digestive distress from overeating, Peter's survival instincts immediately overrode his stomach pain as adrenaline flooded his system and provided the emergency energy necessary for desperate flight from mortal danger. He bounded away through the garden rows with panic-driven speed, his heart pounding frantically as Mr. McGregor pursued him with grim determination, wielding gardening tools as weapons while shouting threats and curses that left no doubt about his hostile intentions toward vegetable thieves.
Page 3
The terrifying chase led Peter through a maze of garden paths, around tool sheds, between rows of precious vegetables, and over various obstacles that threatened to trip him up and deliver him into the merciless hands of his determined pursuer. His earlier confidence and sense of rebellious triumph had completely evaporated, replaced by pure animal terror and the desperate desire to escape alive from the consequences of his disobedient behavior that had placed him in exactly the kind of perilous situation his mother had warned him to avoid.
Page 4
During his frantic flight, Peter lost his beautiful blue jacket when it became entangled on a gooseberry bush, and his shoes slipped off his feet as he scrambled desperately to maintain his lead over Mr. McGregor's relentless pursuit. These material losses, while distressing under normal circumstances, seemed insignificant compared to the much greater loss of his life that would surely result if he failed to reach the safety of the garden gate before his pursuer could close the distance between them and exact the ultimate penalty for trespassing and theft.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. adrenaline - a hormone that gives extra energy and strength during emergencies
2. relentless - never stopping; continuing without mercy

Chapter 6: Lessons Learned Through Suffering
Page 1
Through a combination of desperate speed, fortunate circumstances, and perhaps some divine intervention on behalf of foolish young rabbits who have learned harsh lessons about the consequences of disobedience, Peter managed to reach the garden gate and squeeze through the same opening that had granted him access to his ill-fated adventure. He collapsed on the safe side of the wall, gasping for breath and trembling with exhaustion, terror, and the profound realization that his mother's warnings had been based on genuine wisdom rather than arbitrary maternal restrictions.
Page 2
As Peter slowly made his way home through the familiar woodland paths that now seemed like a paradise of safety compared to the dangerous territory he had just escaped, he reflected painfully on the series of poor decisions that had led to his current miserable condition. His stomach still ached from overindulgence, his feet were sore and dirty from running without shoes, and his pride was thoroughly wounded by the loss of his favorite jacket and the humiliating experience of being chased like a common criminal.
Page 3
When Mrs. Rabbit returned home from her errands that evening, she found Flopsy, Mopsy, and Cotton-tail enjoying a pleasant supper of bread and milk as a reward for their obedient behavior during her absence, while Peter lay sick and miserable in bed, still suffering from the combined effects of dietary excess and psychological trauma from his terrifying encounter with mortal danger. The contrast between his sisters' contentment and his own suffering provided a vivid illustration of the different consequences that flow from wise versus foolish choices.
Page 4
Mrs. Rabbit, demonstrating the perfect balance of maternal discipline and compassionate care that characterizes truly wise parenting, provided Peter with chamomile tea to soothe his troubled stomach while making it clear through her disappointment and concern that his disobedient adventure had caused considerable anxiety and confirmed her worst fears about his judgment and decision-making abilities. Peter, thoroughly chastened by his painful experience, finally understood that parental rules exist not to limit freedom arbitrarily, but to protect those we love from dangers they may be too inexperienced to recognize or avoid.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. intervention - the action of becoming involved to help or change a situation
2. chastened - made to feel ashamed or subdued by experience
