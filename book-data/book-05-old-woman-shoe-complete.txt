The Old Woman Who Lived in a Shoe
BedrockELA 2nd Grade Chapter Book
Quarter 1, Book 5
6 Chapters with Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions

Chapter 1: A Very Big Family
Page 1
Once upon a time, there lived an old woman who had so many children she didn't know what to do! She had twelve little ones, and they were always busy and loud.
Page 2
The problem was that their house was much too small. It was so tiny that the children could barely fit inside. They were always bumping into each other and getting in the way.
Page 3
The old woman loved her children very much, but she was tired and worried. How could she find a bigger place for her big family? Where could they all live together?
Page 4
One day, while walking through the town, the old woman saw something very strange. There, in an empty field, sat the biggest shoe she had ever seen! It was as big as a house!
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. barely - only just; almost not at all
2. worried - feeling troubled or anxious about something
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions
1. How many children did the old woman have?
	•	Ten
	•	Twelve
	•	Fifteen
2. What was the problem with their house?
	•	It was too old
	•	It was much too small
	•	It was too expensive

Chapter 2: The Giant Shoe
Page 1
The old woman walked closer to the giant shoe. It was made of brown leather and had long laces just like a regular shoe. But it was enormous! It had windows and a door cut into it.
Page 2
She knocked on the door. No one answered. She peeked through the windows. The shoe was empty inside! It had been turned into a house, but no one was living in it.
Page 3
"This is perfect!" thought the old woman. "There's plenty of room for all of us here!" The shoe had a big main room, a kitchen, and even an upstairs area.
Page 4
The old woman hurried home to tell her children the good news. "Pack up your things!" she called out. "We're moving to a new home! And wait until you see where we're going to live!"
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. enormous - extremely large
2. plenty - more than enough
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. What was the giant shoe made of?
	•	Plastic and rubber
	•	Brown leather
	•	Wood and metal
2. What did the old woman find when she looked inside the shoe?
	•	Other people living there
	•	It was empty with lots of room
	•	It was full of old furniture

Chapter 3: Moving Day
Page 1
The children were so excited about their new home! They had never seen anything like a shoe house before. They packed up all their toys, clothes, and belongings as fast as they could.
Page 2
When they arrived at the giant shoe, the children could not believe their eyes. "We're going to live in a shoe?" they laughed. "This is the best house ever!" they cheered.
Page 3
They ran inside to explore their new home. There was so much space! The children could run around without bumping into each other. Each child could have their own special corner.
Page 4
The old woman smiled as she watched her children explore. For the first time in a long while, she felt happy and peaceful. Her family finally had room to grow and play.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. belongings - things that you own
2. peaceful - calm and quiet
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the children feel about their new home?
	•	Sad and disappointed
	•	Excited and happy
	•	Scared and worried
2. What could each child have in the new house?
	•	Their own special corner
	•	A separate bedroom
	•	A private bathroom

Chapter 4: Making It Home
Page 1
Now the family needed to make the shoe feel like home. The old woman and her children worked together to clean and organize their new house. Everyone had a job to do.
Page 2
The older children swept the floors and washed the windows. The middle children arranged the furniture and put away the dishes. Even the youngest ones helped by putting their toys in the right places.
Page 3
They hung curtains on the windows and put rugs on the floors. The old woman planted a small garden outside the shoe. Soon their new home looked cozy and welcoming.
Page 4
That evening, they all sat down together for dinner in their new home. The old woman looked around at her children and smiled. They had turned the giant shoe into a wonderful family home.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. organize - to put things in order and arrange them neatly
2. cozy - warm, comfortable, and inviting
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. Who helped make the shoe into a home?
	•	Only the old woman
	•	The whole family worked together
	•	Only the older children
2. What did the old woman plant outside?
	•	A big tree
	•	A small garden
	•	Some flowers

Chapter 5: Visitors and Friends
Page 1
Word spread quickly through the town about the family who lived in a shoe. People were curious and came to see this unusual house. At first, some visitors just stared and pointed.
Page 2
But when people met the old woman and her children, they saw how kind and friendly the family was. The children were polite and well-behaved. They welcomed visitors with warm smiles.
Page 3
Soon the neighbors began bringing gifts. Someone brought fresh bread. Another person brought vegetables from their garden. A kind baker brought cookies for all the children.
Page 4
The old woman was touched by everyone's kindness. "Thank you all," she said. "We may live in a shoe, but we have the best neighbors in the world!" Everyone laughed and agreed.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. curious - wanting to know or learn about something
2. touched - feeling grateful and moved by kindness
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. Why did people come to see the shoe house?

	•	They were curious about the unusual house
	•	They wanted to complain about it
2. How did the neighbors help the family?
	•	They ignored them
	•	They brought gifts like food
	•	They asked them to leave

Chapter 6: Happy Ever After
Page 1
Months passed, and the shoe house became the happiest home in the whole town. The children had plenty of room to play and learn. They helped each other and shared everything.
Page 2
The old woman was no longer tired and worried. She had time to read stories to her children, teach them new things, and play games with them. The house was filled with laughter.
Page 3
Other families in town came to visit and learn from them. They saw how the family worked together and cared for each other. The shoe house became famous for being full of love.
Page 4
And so the old woman and her twelve children lived happily in their shoe house. They learned that a home is not about how it looks, but about the love and care inside it.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. laughter - the sound people make when they are happy and laughing
2. famous - well-known by many people
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the old woman feel in her new home?
	•	Still tired and worried
	•	Happy and no longer worried
	•	Sad and lonely
2. What did the family learn about homes?
	•	Bigger is always better
	•	A home is about love and care, not how it looks
	•	Only fancy houses are good homes
