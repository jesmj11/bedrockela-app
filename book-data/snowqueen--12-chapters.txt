Chapter 1: The Magic Mirror
Long, long ago, there lived a wicked troll who created the most terrible magic mirror in the world. This mirror had a horrible power - it made everything good and beautiful look ugly and evil.
When someone looked into this mirror, flowers appeared wilted, kind faces looked mean, and sunny days seemed gloomy. The mirror twisted everything beautiful into something horrible.
The troll was very proud of his evil creation. He decided to carry the mirror up to heaven to trick the angels into seeing themselves as ugly and mean.
But as the troll flew higher and higher with his magic mirror, it became heavier and harder to hold. His hands began to shake from the effort.
Suddenly, the mirror slipped from his grasp! It fell down, down, down toward the earth, spinning and tumbling through the clouds.
When the mirror hit the ground, it shattered into millions of tiny pieces. The sharp fragments scattered all over the world, blown by the wind to every corner of the earth.
These tiny pieces were even more dangerous than the whole mirror had been. When a piece got into someone's eye, they could only see the bad in everything. When a piece entered someone's heart, their heart turned cold as ice.

Chapter 2: Best Friends
In a busy city, there lived two children who were the very best of friends. The boy was named Kay, and the girl was named Gerda. They lived in houses right next to each other.
Between their houses was a small garden where beautiful red roses grew. Kay and Gerda loved to sit among the roses and play together every day.
Kay was clever and liked to learn new things. Gerda was kind and gentle, always ready to help others. They shared everything and were never apart for long.
In winter, when snow covered the rose garden, the children would sit by warm windows and listen to Kay's grandmother tell wonderful stories.
'Tell us about the Snow Queen!' they would beg. Grandmother would describe a beautiful but cold woman who ruled over winter and ice.
'The Snow Queen flies through the air in her sleigh,' Grandmother said. 'When she breathes on windows, they become covered with ice flowers. But don't worry, children - she can only come where there is no love.'
Kay and Gerda would hold hands and smile. They had so much love for each other that they were sure the Snow Queen could never come near them.

Chapter 3: The Broken Mirror
One winter day, Kay and Gerda were looking out the window at the falling snow. The flakes were beautiful, each one different and perfectly formed.
'Look, Gerda!' said Kay excitedly. 'The snowflakes look like tiny white flowers dancing in the air!'
But as they watched, something terrible happened. Two tiny pieces of the troll's broken mirror flew through the air - one struck Kay in the eye, and the other pierced his heart.
Kay blinked hard, trying to clear his eye, but the mirror fragment was too small to see. 'Ow!' he cried. 'Something got in my eye!'
'Let me look,' said Gerda kindly, trying to help her friend. But Kay pushed her away roughly.
'Don't touch me!' Kay snapped. 'I'm fine!' But he wasn't fine at all. The mirror pieces were already beginning to work their evil magic.
Kay looked out at the snow again, but now instead of beautiful flowers, he saw only ugly, sharp shapes. Everything looked different - cold and mean and unpleasant.

Chapter 4: Kay Changes
From that day forward, Kay was completely different. The mirror fragment in his heart made him cold and cruel. The piece in his eye made him see only ugliness everywhere.
When spring came and the roses bloomed in their little garden, Kay looked at them with disgust. 'These roses are wilted and ugly,' he said. 'They smell terrible.'
Poor Gerda couldn't understand what had happened to her best friend. 'But Kay,' she said sadly, 'these are the same roses we've always loved.'
'You're stupid, Gerda,' Kay said meanly. 'You don't know anything about beautiful things. These roses are disgusting.'
Kay no longer wanted to play with Gerda. Instead, he spent his time making fun of their neighbors and being unkind to everyone around him.
When winter came again, Kay preferred to play alone in the town square with his sled. He would tie his sled to other children's sleds and race through the snowy streets.
Gerda watched sadly from her window as Kay played roughly with the other children. She missed her kind, gentle friend terribly.

Chapter 5: The Snow Queen's Visit
One snowy afternoon, Kay was playing in the town square when the most beautiful white sleigh appeared. It was pulled by pure white horses and driven by someone wrapped in white fur.
The driver was tall and elegant, with skin as white as snow and eyes as cold as winter ice. It was the Snow Queen herself!
'Would you like to ride with me?' asked the Snow Queen in a voice like tinkling icicles.
Before he knew what he was doing, Kay tied his little sled to the Snow Queen's magnificent sleigh. Away they flew, faster and faster through the snowy streets.
At first, Kay enjoyed the wild ride. But soon the sleigh was moving too fast, and they were flying high above the town. Kay tried to untie his sled, but the rope wouldn't come loose.
'Don't be afraid,' said the Snow Queen, turning to look at him with her cold, beautiful face. 'Are you cold? Come sit beside me, and I'll wrap you in my furs.'
The Snow Queen kissed Kay on the forehead, and immediately all his memories of Gerda and his home began to fade away. He forgot about love and warmth and friendship.

Chapter 6: Gerda's Sorrow
When Kay didn't come home that evening, everyone in the town began to search for him. They looked everywhere - in every house, every shop, and every alley.
Gerda was heartbroken. She ran through the snowy streets calling Kay's name until her voice was hoarse and her feet were frozen.
'Kay! Kay! Where are you?' she cried. But there was no answer except the howling of the winter wind.
Days turned into weeks, and weeks turned into months. Spring came, but Kay was still missing. Everyone else had given up hope, but Gerda refused to stop believing.
'Kay is alive somewhere,' she told herself firmly. 'I can feel it in my heart. And if he's alive, then I must find him and bring him home.'
One warm spring morning, Gerda made up her mind. She would leave home and search for Kay, no matter how long it took or how far she had to travel.
She put on her best red shoes, the ones Kay had always said looked so pretty, and kissed her grandmother goodbye. 'I'll find Kay and bring him home,' she promised.

Chapter 7: The Journey Begins
Gerda walked to the river that flowed past her town. 'Dear river,' she said, 'will you help me find my friend Kay? I'll give you my red shoes if you'll carry me to him.'
She threw her red shoes into the water, but the waves brought them back to shore. The river seemed to be saying it hadn't seen Kay and couldn't help her.
But Gerda climbed into a small boat anyway. The gentle current carried the boat downstream, past fields of flowers and green meadows.
After many hours, the boat stopped at a beautiful garden where an old woman lived in a cottage covered with roses and climbing vines.
'My poor child,' said the kind old woman, 'where are you going all alone in that little boat?'
Gerda told her story, and the old woman felt sorry for the brave little girl. 'Stay with me,' she said. 'I have the most beautiful garden in the world, and you'll be happy here.'
The garden was indeed magical and beautiful, but Gerda knew she couldn't stay. 'Thank you for your kindness,' she said, 'but I must find Kay. He needs me.'

Chapter 8: The Crow's Help
As Gerda continued her journey, she met a friendly crow who was very wise and knew many secrets about the world.
'I'm looking for my best friend Kay,' Gerda explained. 'He was taken away by the Snow Queen, and I must find him.'
The crow thought carefully. 'I may know something that could help you,' he said. 'In the kingdom nearby, there's a prince who married a very clever princess just recently.'
'Maybe the prince is your friend Kay!' suggested the crow. 'Many young men came to try to win the princess, and one of them was chosen.'
Gerda's heart filled with hope. 'Please, dear crow, will you help me get into the palace so I can see if the prince is really Kay?'
The crow agreed to help. That night, he led Gerda through the palace gardens and into the royal chambers where the prince and princess slept.
But when Gerda saw the prince, her heart sank. He wasn't Kay at all. The kind princess woke up and listened to Gerda's story with sympathy and kindness.

Chapter 9: The Robber Girl
The princess gave Gerda warm clothes, a golden carriage, and food for her journey. 'I hope you find your friend,' she said kindly.
But as Gerda traveled through a dark forest in her golden carriage, robbers attacked! They dragged her from the carriage and brought her to their hideout.
The leader of the robbers had a daughter who was wild and fierce. But when she heard Gerda's story, something touched her heart.
'I've never had a real friend,' said the robber girl. 'All I have are these animals I keep as pets. But your story makes me sad.'
She showed Gerda her favorite reindeer. 'This reindeer comes from the far North,' she explained. 'He tells me stories about his homeland, where ice and snow never melt.'
The reindeer spoke up: 'I know the land you seek, little girl. The Snow Queen has her palace there, in the frozen wilderness. I have seen her flying overhead.'
The robber girl made a decision. 'I'm going to help you,' she told Gerda. 'Take my reindeer - he knows the way to the Snow Queen's palace. He'll carry you there safely.'

Chapter 10: The Reindeer's Gift
The faithful reindeer carried Gerda far to the North, through blizzards and over frozen mountains. They traveled for days and nights without stopping.
Finally, they reached the land where the Snow Queen ruled. Everything was white and frozen - the trees, the ground, even the air seemed to sparkle with ice crystals.
'I can carry you no farther,' said the reindeer gently. 'The Snow Queen's magic is too strong here. You must go the rest of the way alone.'
Gerda was frightened, but she was determined to find Kay. 'Thank you, dear reindeer,' she said. 'You've brought me so far. Now I must be brave and finish the journey myself.'
As she walked through the frozen landscape, Gerda's breath came out in white puffs. The cold was so intense that her fingers and toes grew numb.
Suddenly, she was surrounded by snow soldiers - the Snow Queen's guards! But as they approached, Gerda began to pray, and her warm breath turned into angels who fought off the icy soldiers.
Protected by the angels, Gerda walked on until she saw a magnificent palace made entirely of ice and snow, gleaming like a giant diamond in the arctic light.

Chapter 11: The Ice Palace
The Snow Queen's palace was the most beautiful and terrible place Gerda had ever seen. The walls were made of glittering ice, and the floors were so smooth they looked like frozen lakes.
In the very center of the palace was a great hall where the Snow Queen sat on her throne. And there, at her feet, sat Kay!
But Kay looked nothing like the friend Gerda remembered. His skin was blue-white from the cold, and his eyes were empty and expressionless. He sat perfectly still, like a statue.
Kay was playing a strange game with pieces of ice, trying to spell out the word 'Eternity' with the frozen fragments. The Snow Queen had promised him freedom if he could solve the puzzle.
'Kay!' cried Gerda, running toward her friend. 'Kay, it's me! It's Gerda! I've come to take you home!'
But Kay didn't respond. He couldn't even hear her voice. The ice had frozen his heart so completely that he felt nothing - no love, no warmth, no recognition.
Gerda threw her arms around Kay and held him close, crying warm tears of love and sorrow. 'Please remember me,' she whispered. 'Please come back to me.'

Chapter 12: Love Melts Ice
Gerda's warm tears fell on Kay's chest, right where his heart was frozen. The tears were so full of love that they melted through the ice and reached his heart.
As the warmth of Gerda's love touched his heart, Kay began to remember. He thought of their rose garden, their games together, and all the happy times they had shared.
Suddenly, Kay burst into tears. His crying was so strong that it washed the mirror fragment out of his eye. Now he could see clearly again!
'Gerda!' he cried, recognizing his dear friend at last. 'Oh, Gerda! You came to find me! How did you get here? Where have I been?'
They hugged each other tightly, laughing and crying with joy. Their happiness was so pure and strong that the ice pieces on the floor began to dance and spin by themselves.
When the ice pieces stopped spinning, they had formed the word 'Eternity' perfectly! Kay was free at last from the Snow Queen's power.
The Snow Queen returned to find her palace empty. Kay and Gerda had already begun their long journey home, helped again by the reindeer, the robber girl, and all the friends Gerda had made.
When they finally reached their home, they discovered they had both grown taller during their adventure. But their hearts were still full of the same love and friendship they had always shared.
They sat once again in their rose garden, older and wiser but still the very best of friends. And they knew that no magic mirror or Snow Queen could ever separate them again, because true friendship is the strongest magic of all.

The End
