The Velveteen Rabbit
Chapter Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions
3rd Grade Teacher's Guide

Chapter 1: Christmas Morning
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. velveteen - a soft fabric that feels like velvet
	•	2. whiskers - long hairs that grow near an animal's mouth
	•	3. handsome - good-looking; attractive
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What holiday was it in the story?
	•	2. What presents did the Boy receive besides the velveteen rabbit?
	•	3. Describe what the velveteen rabbit looked like.
	•	4. Why was the rabbit forgotten? What toy did the Boy play with instead?
	•	5. How do you think the rabbit felt when he was forgotten?

Chapter 2: The Nursery
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. mechanical - having moving parts that work like a machine
	•	2. ordinary - normal; nothing special
	•	3. rudder - a part that helps steer a boat
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where did Nana put the velveteen rabbit?
	•	2. Why were the mechanical toys proud of themselves?
	•	3. What could the toy mouse and model boat do?
	•	4. Why did the rabbit feel ordinary?
	•	5. How did the other toys treat the velveteen rabbit?

Chapter 3: The Skin Horse's Wisdom
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. wisdom - knowledge and good judgment
	•	2. seams - the lines where pieces of fabric are sewn together
	•	3. eager - excited and wanting to learn or do something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who was kind to the velveteen rabbit in the nursery?
	•	2. How was the Skin Horse different from the other toys?
	•	3. What did the Skin Horse say really mattered?
	•	4. According to the Skin Horse, what happens when a child loves you?
	•	5. What question did the rabbit ask at the end of the chapter?

Chapter 4: What Is Real?
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. honest - telling the truth; not lying
	•	2. joints - places where two parts connect and can move
	•	3. deserve - to earn something; to be worthy of something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the Skin Horse say Real isn't about?
	•	2. How does someone become Real, according to the Skin Horse?
	•	3. Does it hurt to become Real? What did the Skin Horse say?
	•	4. What might happen to a toy when it becomes Real?
	•	5. Why can't a Real toy be ugly?

Chapter 5: The Boy's Favorite
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. bedtime - the time when someone goes to sleep
	•	2. jealously - feeling angry because someone has something you want
	•	3. tucked - placed carefully and snugly
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Why did Nana give the Boy the velveteen rabbit one evening?
	•	2. What did the Boy say about the rabbit when he held him?
	•	3. What happened from that night on?
	•	4. How did the other toys feel about the rabbit's new role?
	•	5. What was the rabbit beginning to understand?

Chapter 6: Adventures Begin
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. adventures - exciting experiences or activities
	•	2. explored - looked around and discovered new places
	•	3. shabby - worn out and looking old
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What season came to the house and garden?
	•	2. Where did the rabbit go during the Boy's adventures?
	•	3. What was happening to the rabbit's fur?
	•	4. Did the rabbit mind that he was getting worn and shabby? Why?
	•	5. How did the Boy feel about his rabbit's appearance?

Chapter 7: Spring in the Garden
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. blooming - when flowers open up and show their colors
	•	2. burrows - holes in the ground where animals live
	•	3. chattering - talking quickly and excitedly
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the Boy make in the dirt for his rabbit?
	•	2. What important word did the Boy use to describe his rabbit?
	•	3. How did the rabbit feel when he heard this word?
	•	4. What game did the Boy plan for the next day?
	•	5. What did the Skin Horse tell the rabbit that night?

Chapter 8: Meeting the Real Rabbits
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. investigate - to look at something closely to learn about it
	•	2. amazed - very surprised and impressed
	•	3. muscles - parts of the body that help us move
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where was the velveteen rabbit left one evening?
	•	2. What creatures came out of the raspberry bushes?
	•	3. What did the real rabbits invite the velveteen rabbit to do?
	•	4. Why couldn't the velveteen rabbit join their game?
	•	5. How were the real rabbits different from the velveteen rabbit?

Chapter 9: The Sad Discovery
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. confusion - feeling puzzled or mixed up
	•	2. sniffed - smelled something by breathing in through the nose
	•	3. sawdust - tiny pieces of wood
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the real rabbits discover about the velveteen rabbit?
	•	2. How did the real rabbits know he wasn't really real?
	•	3. What can real rabbits do that the velveteen rabbit couldn't?
	•	4. How did this discovery make the velveteen rabbit feel?
	•	5. What did the real rabbits say about love before they left?

Chapter 10: The Fever
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. fever - when someone's body gets very hot because they are sick
	•	2. comfort - help someone feel better when they are sad or scared
	•	3. germs - tiny things that can make people sick
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What happened to the Boy after the sad evening with the rabbits?
	•	2. How did the velveteen rabbit help during the Boy's illness?
	•	3. What did the Boy tell his rabbit about how he felt?
	•	4. What did the doctor say had to happen to all the Boy's toys?
	•	5. Where was the sack with the toys taken?

Chapter 11: The Fairy's Gift
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. sprouted - began to grow from the ground
	•	2. fairy - a magical being with special powers
	•	3. stardust - tiny sparkles of magic
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What happened when the rabbit's tear fell to the ground?
	•	2. Who was the beautiful fairy that appeared?
	•	3. What did the fairy say about the Boy's love?
	•	4. What did the rabbit still worry about?
	•	5. What special gift did the fairy give the rabbit?

Chapter 12: Real at Last
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. tingling - a feeling like tiny prickles on your skin
	•	2. muscles - parts inside the body that help you move
	•	3. glimpse - a quick look at something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What happened to the rabbit after the fairy's kiss?
	•	2. How was the rabbit different after the magic?
	•	3. What could the rabbit do with his new body?
	•	4. What question did the rabbit ask about the Boy?
	•	5. What is the main lesson of this story?

Discussion Questions for the Complete Story
	•	1. How did the velveteen rabbit change throughout the story?
	•	2. What does it mean to be 'Real' in this story?
	•	3. Why was the Boy's love so important to the rabbit?
	•	4. How did the Skin Horse help the rabbit understand about being Real?
	•	5. What do you think the author wanted us to learn from this story?
	•	6. Have you ever had a special toy that was important to you? How did it make you feel?
	•	7. Do you think love can make things special? Give examples.
	•	8. How did the rabbit show courage in this story?

Writing Prompts
	•	1. Write about a special toy or object that is important to you. Why is it special?
	•	2. Imagine you could become Real like the velveteen rabbit. What would you want to do first?
	•	3. Write a letter from the velveteen rabbit to the Boy, thanking him for his love.
	•	4. Create a new adventure for the rabbit after he becomes Real.
	•	5. Write about a time when someone's love made you feel special.
