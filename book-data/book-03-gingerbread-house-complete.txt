The Gingerbread House
BedrockELA 2nd Grade Chapter Book
Quarter 1, Book 3
6 Chapters with Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions

Chapter 1: Lost in the Woods
Page 1
Once upon a time, two children named Hansel and Gretel lived with their father. Their family was very poor and had very little food to eat.
Page 2
One day, their father took them for a walk in the deep woods. He told them to wait while he gathered firewood. But when they looked for him, he was gone.
Page 3
"Where is Father?" asked Gretel. "I don't know," said Hansel. "But don't worry. I left a trail of breadcrumbs so we can find our way home." But the birds had eaten all the crumbs!
Page 4
The children were lost in the dark woods. They walked and walked, looking for the path home. As night fell, they were scared and hungry. Where could they go?
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. poor - having very little money or food
2. trail - a path or track left behind
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions
1. What were the children's names?
	•	Jack and Jill
	•	Hansel and Gretel
	•	Tom and Sue
2. What happened to Hansel's trail of breadcrumbs?
	•	They got wet in the rain
	•	The birds ate them
	•	The wind blew them away

Chapter 2: A Sweet Discovery
Page 1
The next morning, Hansel and Gretel woke up cold and hungry. They had slept under a big tree all night. They needed to find food and shelter soon.
Page 2
As they walked deeper into the woods, they smelled something wonderful. It was sweet and delicious. "Do you smell that?" asked Gretel. "It smells like cookies!"
Page 3
They followed the sweet smell until they came to a clearing. There, in front of them, stood the most amazing house they had ever seen. It was made entirely of gingerbread and candy!
Page 4
The walls were made of gingerbread. The roof was covered with frosting. The windows were made of clear sugar candy. Gumdrops decorated the door. It looked good enough to eat!
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. shelter - a place that protects you from weather
2. clearing - an open space in the woods with no trees
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. What did the children smell in the woods?
	•	Flowers
	•	Something sweet like cookies
	•	Smoke
2. What was the house made of?
	•	Wood and stone
	•	Gingerbread and candy
	•	Bricks and mud

Chapter 3: The Hungry Children
Page 1
Hansel and Gretel could not believe their eyes. A house made of candy! They were so hungry that their stomachs were growling loudly.
Page 2
"Can we eat it?" asked Gretel. "I don't see anyone around," said Hansel. "Maybe just a little bite won't hurt." They walked closer to the amazing house.
Page 3
Hansel broke off a piece of gingerbread from the wall. It was soft and sweet and tasted like the best cookie he had ever eaten. Gretel licked some frosting from the roof.
Page 4
Soon both children were eating pieces of the house. They ate gumdrops from the door and sugar candy from the windows. They had never tasted anything so delicious!
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. growling - making a low rumbling sound (like a hungry stomach)
2. delicious - tasting very good
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. Why were the children's stomachs growling?
	•	They were angry
	•	They were hungry
	•	They were scared
2. What did Hansel eat first?
	•	Frosting from the roof
	•	Gingerbread from the wall
	•	Candy from the windows

Chapter 4: The Witch Appears
Page 1
Suddenly, the door of the gingerbread house opened with a loud creak. Out came a very old woman with a crooked nose and a wicked smile.
Page 2
"Who is eating my house?" she cackled. Hansel and Gretel were so scared they could not move. The old woman looked at them with hungry eyes.
Page 3
"Oh, what lovely children!" she said in a sweet voice. "You must be hungry. Come inside and I will give you a feast. I have cookies and cakes and all sorts of treats!"
Page 4
The children did not know that this old woman was really a wicked witch. She pretended to be kind, but she had a terrible plan for Hansel and Gretel.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. creak - a squeaking sound that old doors make
2. feast - a large, special meal with lots of food
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. What did the old woman look like?
	•	Young and pretty
	•	Old with a crooked nose
	•	Tall and thin
2. What did the old woman really want to do?
	•	Help the children find their way home
	•	She had a terrible plan for them
	•	Teach them how to bake

Chapter 5: Trapped!
Page 1
The witch led Hansel and Gretel into her house. Inside, there were tables covered with every kind of sweet treat you could imagine. But something felt wrong.
Page 2
After the children ate, the witch showed her true nature. She grabbed Hansel and locked him in a cage! "You will stay here until you are fat enough!" she cackled.
Page 3
"Let my brother go!" cried Gretel. But the witch just laughed. "You will help me cook and clean. And every day, I will feed your brother to make him fatter."
Page 4
Now Hansel and Gretel knew they were in terrible danger. The witch planned to eat them! They needed to find a way to escape before it was too late.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. nature - what someone is really like inside
2. escape - to get away from a dangerous place
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. What did the witch do to Hansel?
	•	She gave him more food
	•	She locked him in a cage
	•	She sent him home
2. What did the witch want Gretel to do?
	•	Play games
	•	Cook and clean
	•	Sleep all day

Chapter 6: Gretel's Clever Plan
Page 1
For many days, Gretel worked for the witch while Hansel stayed in the cage. Every day the witch checked to see if Hansel was getting fatter. But Hansel was clever too.
Page 2
When the witch asked Hansel to hold out his finger, he held out a thin chicken bone instead. The witch had poor eyesight and could not tell the difference.
Page 3
Finally, the witch got tired of waiting. She told Gretel to heat up the big oven. "Fat or thin, I will cook your brother today!" she said with an evil grin.
Page 4
But when the witch bent over to check the oven, Gretel gave her a big push! The witch fell right into the hot oven. Gretel quickly freed Hansel, and they ran away to safety, finally finding their way home.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. eyesight - the ability to see
2. safety - being safe from danger
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions
1. How did Hansel trick the witch?
	•	He hid from her
	•	He held out a chicken bone instead of his finger
	•	He pretended to be sick
2. How did Gretel defeat the witch?
	•	She pushed her into the oven
	•	She locked her in the cage
	•	She ran away and hid
