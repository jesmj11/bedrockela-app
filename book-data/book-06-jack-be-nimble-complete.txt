Chapter 1: Jack Loves to Jump
Page 1
There once was a boy named Jack who loved to jump over things. He was very quick and nimble. Every day, Jack would practice jumping over logs, rocks, and puddles.
Page 2
Jack lived in a small village where people used candles for light at night. In those days, there were no electric lights. Tall candlesticks with bright flames lit up the houses.
Page 3
All the children in the village knew about Jack's jumping skills. They would gather around to watch him practice. "Jump over this!" they would call out, pointing to different objects.
Page 4
But Jack wanted a bigger challenge. He had jumped over everything in the village except for one thing. The tall candlestick in the town square was his biggest dream to jump over.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. nimble - able to move quickly and easily
2. challenge - something difficult that tests your skills

Chapter 2: The Big Candlestick
Page 1
In the center of the village stood a very tall candlestick. It was made of shiny brass and was as tall as a grown-up person. Every evening, the villagers would light its big candle.
Page 2
The candlestick was much taller than anything Jack had ever jumped over before. When he looked up at it, it seemed to reach toward the sky. The flame on top danced in the wind.
Page 3
Jack's friends shook their heads. "That's too high, Jack," they said. "You'll never be able to jump over that!" But Jack was determined. He believed he could do it if he practiced enough.
Page 4
Jack studied the candlestick carefully. He walked around it many times, looking at it from every angle. He needed to figure out the best way to approach his biggest jump yet.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. brass - a shiny yellow metal
2. determined - having made up your mind to do something

Chapter 3: Getting Ready
Page 1
Jack knew he needed to train harder than ever before. Every morning, he would wake up early to practice his jumping. He started with small objects and worked his way up to bigger ones.
Page 2
He jumped over fence posts, then over hay bales, then over his neighbor's gate. Each day, Jack could jump a little higher than the day before. His legs were getting stronger.
Page 3
Jack also practiced his running. He knew he would need to run very fast to get enough speed for such a high jump. He ran through the fields and around the village every day.
Page 4
After weeks of training, Jack felt ready. His friends gathered to watch as he stood in front of the tall candlestick. Tonight would be the night he would try his greatest jump.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. train - to practice to get better at something
2. speed - how fast something moves

Chapter 4: The Big Moment
Page 1
As the sun began to set, the whole village gathered around the town square. Everyone wanted to see if Jack could really jump over the tall candlestick. Even the grown-ups came to watch.
Page 2
The candlestick was lit, and its bright flame flickered in the evening breeze. Jack took a deep breath and stepped back to get a running start. His heart was beating fast with excitement.
Page 3
"Jack be nimble, Jack be quick!" called out his friends. Jack smiled and nodded. He had been nimble and quick all his life. Now it was time to prove it in the biggest way possible.
Page 4
Jack began to run toward the candlestick. Faster and faster he went, building up speed with each step. The crowd held their breath as Jack got closer and closer to the tall flame.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. flickered - moved back and forth quickly like a flame in the wind
2. excitement - the feeling of being very happy and eager
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

Chapter 5: Jack Jumps the Candlestick
Page 1
With all his speed, Jack reached the candlestick and leaped high into the air! Up, up, up he went, flying over the bright flame. For a moment, he seemed to hang in the air like a bird.
Page 2
The crowd watched in amazement as Jack soared over the candlestick. His jump was perfect! He cleared the flame completely and landed safely on the other side with a soft thud.
Page 3
"He did it! He did it!" everyone cheered. Jack had jumped over the candlestick! All his practice and hard work had paid off. He had achieved his biggest dream.
Page 4
Jack stood up and brushed off his clothes. He was grinning from ear to ear. The villagers clapped and cheered for their nimble, quick friend who had done the impossible.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. leaped - jumped high and far
2. amazement - great surprise and wonder

Chapter 6: Jack's Big Lesson
Page 1
From that day on, Jack became famous throughout the village and beyond. People would come from far away just to see the boy who jumped over the candlestick. But Jack learned something important.
Page 2
He learned that the real joy was not in being famous. The real joy came from setting a goal, working hard to reach it, and never giving up. His success came from practice and determination.
Page 3
Jack began teaching other children how to jump and be nimble. He showed them that with practice and patience, they could achieve their dreams too. Everyone could learn to be quick and nimble.
Page 4
And so, whenever children heard the rhyme "Jack be nimble, Jack be quick," they remembered the lesson. With hard work and practice, anyone can jump over their own candlestick and achieve their dreams.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. famous - known by many people
2. patience - the ability to wait calmly and keep trying
