Chapter 1

VOCABULARY

proportion  —  The correct or appropriate relation of parts to a whole; proper balance.

COMPREHENSION QUESTIONS

1.  Ishmael says he goes to sea when the world becomes 'too much.' What does this tell us about his relationship with ordinary life?

C) He is running away from a specific problem he does not want to face

2.  Ishmael says the ocean gave him 'larger problems to look at, which had roughly the same effect' as solving his original ones. What does he mean?

B) That encountering something enormous puts your own concerns in perspective without actually resolving them — the scale shift is itself the cure

C) That whaling was so dangerous it made his old worries seem trivial

D) That he never actually resolved any of his problems

B) Ahab's story — that the whale is his enemy and killing it will bring justice — was compelling enough to move a whole crew, but it was not true; the whale had no enmity toward him

C) Ishmael is arguing that Ahab's story was poorly told

D) Stories should always be fact-checked before being believed

Answer: B

2.  Ishmael concludes that 'the universe is not organized around our suffering' — and calls this 'both the worst thing about it and the most liberating.' How can indifference be liberating?

A) Indifference means no one is watching, so you can do whatever you want

B) If the universe is not organized against you specifically, you are free — you do not have to fight it or flee it or seek its approval; you can simply live within it on your own terms

C) Indifference is liberating because it removes responsibility

D) Ishmael has become indifferent himself and finds this freeing
