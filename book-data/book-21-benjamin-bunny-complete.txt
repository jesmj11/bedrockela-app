The Tale of Benjamin Bunny
BedrockELA 2nd Grade Chapter Book
Quarter 2, Book 7
6 Chapters with Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions

Chapter 1: Benjamin Visits Peter
Page 1
One morning, little Benjamin Bunny was sitting on a bank when he saw his cousin Peter Rabbit coming down the lane. Peter looked very sad and his jacket was missing. His little blue jacket was nowhere to be seen.
Page 2
"What happened to you, cousin Peter?" asked Benjamin kindly. Peter hung his head in shame. "I went into Mr. McGregor's garden," he whispered. "And I lost my jacket when I had to escape quickly."
Page 3
Benjamin's eyes grew wide with excitement rather than worry. He had always been much more confident and mischievous than his cousin. Unlike Peter, Benjamin was not afraid of adventures, even dangerous ones.
Page 4
"Come with me!" said Benjamin boldly. "My father told me that Mr. McGregor and his wife have gone out for the day. We can sneak into the garden and get your jacket back before they return home."
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. mischievous - playful in a naughty way; likely to get into trouble
2. confident - sure of oneself; not afraid to try new things

Chapter 2: Into the Forbidden Garden
Page 1
Peter was nervous about returning to Mr. McGregor's garden, but Benjamin was very persuasive. "Don't worry," Benjamin said cheerfully. "I know exactly where there is a gap in the fence. We can slip through easily and quietly."
Page 2
Benjamin led Peter to a loose board in the garden fence. Sure enough, they were able to squeeze through the narrow opening. Once inside the garden, Benjamin felt completely at home, while Peter trembled with every step.
Page 3
The garden was beautiful and full of tempting vegetables. There were rows of crisp lettuce, fat radishes, and sweet carrots. Benjamin immediately began nibbling on the delicious plants, but Peter was too worried to eat anything.
Page 4
"We should look for my jacket," whispered Peter anxiously. Benjamin nodded, but he continued to munch contentedly on a particularly tender young onion. He seemed to have forgotten that they were supposed to be on a rescue mission.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. persuasive - good at convincing others to do something
2. contentedly - in a happy, satisfied way
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions

	•	They jumped over the fence
	•	They squeezed through a gap in the fence
	•	They dug a tunnel
2. What did Benjamin do once he was in the garden?
	•	Looked for the jacket immediately
	•	Started eating the vegetables
	•	Hid behind a tree

Chapter 3: Finding the Jacket
Page 1
After much searching, Peter finally spotted his little blue jacket hanging on a scarecrow in the middle of the garden. The scarecrow was wearing Peter's jacket and his little shoes too! It looked quite ridiculous dressed up like a rabbit.
Page 2
Benjamin thought this was absolutely hilarious. He laughed so hard that he nearly choked on the onion he was still chewing. "Look at that silly scarecrow!" he giggled. "Mr. McGregor has made it look just like you, cousin Peter!"
Page 3
The two little rabbits approached the scarecrow carefully. Benjamin climbed up the wooden post and began pulling off Peter's belongings. First came the jacket, then the tiny shoes. Benjamin stuffed them into a large handkerchief to carry them safely.
Page 4
But Benjamin wasn't finished yet. He filled the handkerchief with onions from Mr. McGregor's garden as well. "These will make a lovely present for my father," he said cheerfully, completely ignoring Peter's worried expression about taking even more vegetables.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. ridiculous - very silly or foolish looking
2. hilarious - extremely funny
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. Where did Peter find his jacket?
	•	Under a vegetable plant
	•	On a scarecrow
	•	In Mr. McGregor's house
2. What else did Benjamin take besides Peter's clothes?
	•	Carrots
	•	Onions
	•	Lettuce

Chapter 4: Trouble Arrives
Page 1
Just as Benjamin was tying up the handkerchief full of onions and clothes, Peter's sharp ears heard a familiar and terrifying sound. "Listen!" Peter whispered urgently. "Do you hear that? Someone is coming down the road toward the garden gate!"
Page 2
It was Mr. McGregor returning home much earlier than expected! They could hear his heavy footsteps getting closer and closer. The two little rabbits looked at each other with wide, frightened eyes. They needed to escape immediately, but where could they hide?
Page 3
Benjamin, who had been so brave and confident all morning, suddenly became just as scared as Peter. His hands shook as he grabbed the heavy bundle of stolen goods. "Quick!" he stammered. "We must find somewhere to hide until he goes away!"
Page 4
The two frightened rabbits scurried around the garden desperately looking for a safe hiding place. They could hear Mr. McGregor's voice getting louder as he approached. Their adventure had suddenly turned from fun and games into a dangerous situation that required quick thinking.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. urgently - in a way that shows something is very important and needs immediate attention
2. desperately - in a way that shows you really, really need something
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

	•	Mrs. McGregor
	•	Mr. McGregor
	•	Another rabbit
2. How did Benjamin's attitude change when he heard Mr. McGregor?
	•	He became even more confident
	•	He became just as scared as Peter
	•	He didn't change at all

Chapter 5: Trapped Under the Basket
Page 1
In their panic to hide, Peter and Benjamin made a terrible mistake. They crawled under a large wicker basket that was sitting in the garden, thinking it would provide good shelter. Unfortunately, they had chosen the worst possible hiding spot in the entire garden.
Page 2
When Mr. McGregor entered his garden, he immediately noticed that his vegetables had been disturbed. Someone had been nibbling his plants! He looked around suspiciously and spotted the basket. Something was definitely moving underneath it, and he could hear small, frightened voices whispering.
Page 3
Without hesitation, Mr. McGregor sat down heavily on top of the basket, trapping the two little rabbits completely. Peter and Benjamin were now prisoners, stuck in the dark underneath the wicker basket with no way to escape. They could hardly breathe in the cramped space.
Page 4
"I know you're under there, you mischievous rabbits!" chuckled Mr. McGregor. "You won't be stealing any more of my vegetables today!" The two cousins trembled with fear as they realized they were completely at the mercy of the angry gardener. What would happen to them now?
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. suspiciously - in a way that shows you think something is wrong
2. cramped - very tight and uncomfortable with no room to move
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. Where did the rabbits try to hide?
	•	Behind a tree
	•	Under a wicker basket
	•	In a hole
2. How did Mr. McGregor trap the rabbits?
	•	He caught them with his hands
	•	He sat down on top of the basket
	•	He put a net over them

Chapter 6: A Narrow Escape
Page 1
For what seemed like hours, Peter and Benjamin remained trapped under the heavy basket. They were growing very uncomfortable and worried that they might never escape. Then, suddenly, they heard a familiar voice calling from outside the garden. It was old Mr. Benjamin Bunny, Benjamin's father!
Page 2
Mr. Benjamin Bunny had come looking for his son when Benjamin failed to return home for lunch. When he saw Mr. McGregor sitting on the basket, he immediately understood what had happened. The wise old rabbit waited patiently for the perfect moment to help his son and nephew escape.
Page 3
When Mr. McGregor finally got up to tend to his other garden chores, Mr. Benjamin Bunny quickly lifted the basket. "Come out immediately!" he whispered sternly to the two frightened young rabbits. "And bring that handkerchief with you - we must return home at once!"
Page 4
The three rabbits hurried safely back to their burrow, where Peter gratefully put on his rescued jacket and shoes. Benjamin's father kept the onions as a reward for the successful rescue mission, but he also gave both young rabbits a stern lecture about the dangers of trespassing in Mr. McGregor's garden.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. patiently - in a calm way, willing to wait without complaining
2. trespassing - going onto someone else's property without permission
