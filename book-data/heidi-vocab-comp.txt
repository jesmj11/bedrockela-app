Heidi
Chapter Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions
3rd Grade Teacher's Guide

Chapter 1: Up the Mountain
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. steep - going up or down at a sharp angle
	•	2. belongings - things that you own
	•	3. steeple - a tall, pointed tower on a church
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How old was Heidi at the beginning of the story?
	•	2. Who was taking Heidi up the mountain?
	•	3. Why had Heidi never met her grandfather before?
	•	4. Why did Aunt Dete have to leave Heidi with her grandfather?
	•	5. How did Heidi feel about climbing up the mountain?

Chapter 2: Meeting Grandfather
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. fierce - strong and intense; frightening
	•	2. gruffly - speaking in a rough, unfriendly way
	•	3. responsibility - a duty to take care of something or someone
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What was Grandfather doing when they arrived?
	•	2. How did Grandfather react when he saw Heidi?
	•	3. What did Aunt Dete do after bringing Heidi?
	•	4. How did Heidi react to meeting her grandfather?
	•	5. What did Grandfather finally say to Heidi?

Chapter 3: A New Home
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. loft - an upper room or space under a roof
	•	2. cozy - warm, comfortable, and snug
	•	3. creamy - smooth and rich like cream
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where did Grandfather tell Heidi she could sleep?
	•	2. What could Heidi see from her window in the loft?
	•	3. What did Grandfather give Heidi for dinner?
	•	4. What did Heidi say about living on the mountain?
	•	5. Why was Grandfather surprised by Heidi's reaction?

Chapter 4: Peter and the Goats
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. goatherd - a person who takes care of goats
	•	2. pasture - a field where animals eat grass
	•	3. meadows - grassy fields, especially in mountains
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What woke Heidi up the next morning?
	•	2. Who was Peter and what was his job?
	•	3. What were the names of Grandfather's two goats?
	•	4. Where did Peter invite Heidi to go?
	•	5. How did the goats react to meeting Heidi?

Chapter 5: Life on the Mountain
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. grazed - ate grass in a field
	•	2. peaks - the tops of mountains
	•	3. lullaby - a gentle song to help someone sleep
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did Heidi do every day with Peter?
	•	2. What did Peter teach Heidi about the mountains?
	•	3. How did Heidi help Grandfather with his work?
	•	4. How did Grandfather's mood change?
	•	5. What sounds did Heidi hear at night that helped her sleep?

Chapter 6: The Village Below
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. blind - unable to see
	•	2. blurry - not clear; hard to see
	•	3. pastor - a religious leader of a church
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who did Peter take Heidi to meet in the village?
	•	2. What was wrong with Peter's grandmother?
	•	3. Why couldn't the grandmother read her prayer book anymore?
	•	4. What did Heidi wish she could do to help?
	•	5. Why wouldn't Grandfather let Heidi go to school?

Chapter 7: Taken Away
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. opportunity - a chance to do something good
	•	2. companion - a friend who spends time with you
	•	3. mansion - a very large, fancy house
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How much time had passed when Aunt Dete returned?
	•	2. What opportunity did Aunt Dete say she had found for Heidi?
	•	3. How did Heidi feel about leaving the mountain?
	•	4. What did Grandfather say when Aunt Dete took Heidi away?
	•	5. How did Heidi feel as she was taken down the mountain?

Chapter 8: The Big City
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. marble - a smooth, shiny stone used in buildings
	•	2. chandeliers - fancy lights that hang from the ceiling
	•	3. housekeeper - a person who manages a household
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How was Frankfurt different from the mountains?
	•	2. What was the Sesemann house like inside?
	•	3. Who was Miss Rottenmeier and what did she think of Heidi?
	•	4. Who was Clara and what was wrong with her?
	•	5. What did Clara ask Heidi to tell her about?

Chapter 9: Clara's Friend
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. tutor - a teacher who gives private lessons
	•	2. manners - polite ways of behaving
	•	3. stuffy - not having enough fresh air
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What new skills did Heidi learn in the city?
	•	2. Why did everything feel strange to Heidi?
	•	3. What stories did Clara love to hear?
	•	4. What did Heidi draw for Clara?
	•	5. How did Heidi feel as time passed in the city?

Chapter 10: Homesick
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. homesick - sad because you miss your home
	•	2. concern - worry about someone or something
	•	3. examine - to look at carefully
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What happened to Heidi's health as months passed?
	•	2. What did Heidi dream about every night?
	•	3. What did Heidi tell Clara she missed most?
	•	4. What did the doctor say was wrong with Heidi?
	•	5. What decision did Mr. Sesemann make?

Chapter 11: Return to the Mountain
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. journey - a trip from one place to another
	•	2. spirits - mood; how happy or sad someone feels
	•	3. contain - to keep something inside; to control
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did Heidi feel as the train climbed into the mountains?
	•	2. Who met Heidi at the village station?
	•	3. How did the goats react when they saw Heidi?
	•	4. How did Grandfather greet Heidi when she returned?
	•	5. Where did Heidi sleep her first night back home?

Chapter 12: Clara's Visit
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. improve - to make better
	•	2. amazed - very surprised in a good way
	•	3. miracle - something wonderful that seems impossible
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who came to visit Heidi a few months later?
	•	2. How did Clara react to seeing the mountains?
	•	3. What happened to Clara's health during her visit?
	•	4. What amazing thing was Clara able to do by the end of summer?
	•	5. How did the story end for both Heidi and Clara?

Discussion Questions for the Complete Story
	•	1. How did Heidi's relationship with her grandfather change throughout the story?
	•	2. What did the mountain represent to Heidi? Why was it so important to her?
	•	3. Compare Heidi's life on the mountain to her life in the city. What were the benefits of each?
	•	4. How did Heidi and Clara help each other as friends?
	•	5. What does this story teach us about the importance of home and nature?
	•	6. Why do you think the fresh mountain air helped Clara get better?
	•	7. What character traits made Heidi a good friend to Clara?
	•	8. How did both girls grow and change because of their friendship?

Writing Prompts
	•	1. Describe your own special place where you feel happiest. What makes it special to you?
	•	2. Write a letter from Heidi to Clara, telling her about something new that happened on the mountain.
	•	3. Imagine you could visit Heidi's mountain. Write about what you would do there.
	•	4. Write about a time when you missed home or felt homesick. How did you feel better?
	•	5. Create a new adventure for Heidi and Peter with the goats in the high meadows.
