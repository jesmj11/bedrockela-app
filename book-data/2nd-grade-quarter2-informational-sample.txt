Text 1: Volcanoes
A volcano is a mountain with an opening that connects to deep inside the Earth. Far below the ground, rock gets so hot that it melts into a thick liquid called magma. This magma is lighter than solid rock, so it rises up through cracks in the Earth's crust. When pressure builds up, the magma can shoot out of the volcano's opening in an explosive eruption.
Once magma reaches the surface, it's called lava, and it flows down the mountainsides like a river of melted rock. Lava is extremely hot - over 2,000 degrees Fahrenheit! Eventually, the lava cools down and hardens into new rock. Some volcanoes erupt often, while others stay quiet for hundreds of years. Scientists study volcanoes carefully to predict eruptions and keep people safe.

Text 2: Veterinarians
Veterinarians, or "vets," are doctors who specialize in taking care of animals. They examine pets like dogs, cats, birds, and rabbits when they are sick or hurt. Vets also work with farm animals like cows, horses, and pigs to keep them healthy. Some veterinarians even care for wild animals in zoos or help injured wildlife in nature.
To become a veterinarian, a person must go to school for many years to learn about animal anatomy, diseases, and treatments. Vets use many of the same tools as doctors for people, like stethoscopes to listen to heartbeats and X-ray machines to see inside animals' bodies. They also perform surgeries, give vaccinations, and prescribe medicines to help animals feel better and stay healthy.

Text 3: Dolphins
Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals that live in oceans around the world. Unlike fish, dolphins are warm-blooded and breathe air through a blowhole on top of their heads. They must come to the surface regularly to breathe, but they can hold their breath for up to 15 minutes while diving deep underwater to hunt for fish, squid, and shrimp.
These amazing animals are known for their playful behavior and complex social lives. Dolphins live in groups called pods and communicate with each other using clicks, whistles, and body language. They are excellent problem solvers and have been observed using tools, teaching their young important skills, and even helping humans who are in trouble at sea. Scientists continue to study dolphins to learn more about their remarkable intelligence.

Text 4: Tornadoes
A tornado is a powerful, spinning column of air that extends from a thunderstorm cloud down to the ground. Tornadoes form when warm, moist air near the ground meets cold, dry air high in the atmosphere, creating instability. When winds at different heights blow in different directions, they can cause the air to rotate horizontally. Rising air then tilts this rotation vertically, creating the spinning funnel cloud.
Tornadoes can be extremely dangerous, with winds that spin faster than 300 miles per hour in the strongest storms. They can destroy buildings, uproot trees, and toss cars through the air like toys. The United States experiences more tornadoes than anywhere else in the world, especially in an area called "Tornado Alley" in the central plains. Weather scientists use special radar technology to detect tornadoes and warn people to take shelter.

Text 5: Pharmacists
Pharmacists are healthcare professionals who specialize in medications and how they affect the human body. When doctors write prescriptions for patients, pharmacists carefully prepare the exact medicines needed, making sure the dosage and instructions are correct. They also check for potential problems, like whether different medicines might interact dangerously with each other or cause allergic reactions.
Beyond filling prescriptions, pharmacists provide valuable health advice to their communities. They can recommend over-the-counter medicines for common problems like headaches or colds, give vaccinations, and answer questions about how to take medications safely. Pharmacists must complete years of specialized education and pass licensing exams to ensure they have the knowledge needed to help keep people healthy and safe.

Text 6: Octopuses
Octopuses are fascinating sea creatures with eight arms covered in powerful suction cups. These intelligent invertebrates have no bones in their bodies, which allows them to squeeze through incredibly small spaces - any opening larger than their beak! Octopuses have three hearts that pump blue blood through their bodies, and their large brains make them some of the smartest animals in the ocean.
One of the most amazing abilities of octopuses is their skill at camouflage. They can instantly change both the color and texture of their skin to match their surroundings, making them nearly invisible to predators and prey. Octopuses are also excellent problem solvers who can open jars, navigate mazes, and use tools like rocks and shells. Unfortunately, most octopuses only live for about one to two years.

Text 7: Glaciers
A glacier is a massive sheet of ice that forms when snow accumulates for many years without completely melting. As more snow piles up, the weight compresses the lower layers into dense ice. Over thousands of years, these ice sheets can become hundreds of feet thick and begin to move slowly downhill due to gravity, like very slow rivers of ice.
Glaciers are incredibly powerful forces that shape the landscape around them. As they move, they carve deep valleys, create lakes, and transport huge boulders for miles. Today, most of the world's glaciers are found in places like Antarctica, Greenland, and high mountain ranges. Scientists study glaciers carefully because they contain important information about Earth's climate history and are sensitive indicators of climate change.
Comprehension Questions

	•	rain turns into ice
2. How do glaciers change the landscape?
	•	they carve valleys, create lakes, and move boulders
	•	they make the ground flat
	•	they only melt and disappear

Text 8: Engineers
Engineers are problem-solvers who design and build things that make our lives better and safer. There are many different types of engineers: civil engineers design roads and bridges, electrical engineers work with power systems and electronics, mechanical engineers create machines and engines, and aerospace engineers develop airplanes and spacecraft. All engineers use math, science, and creativity to solve complex challenges.
To become an engineer, a person must study mathematics and science extensively in college. Engineers must think carefully about safety, cost, and how their designs will affect people and the environment. They often work in teams, testing their ideas multiple times before building the final product. From the smartphones we use to the buildings we live in, engineers have helped create almost everything in our modern world.

Text 9: Coral Reefs
Coral reefs are underwater ecosystems built by tiny marine animals called coral polyps. These small creatures create hard, colorful skeletons from calcium carbonate, and over thousands of years, millions of these skeletons build up to form large reef structures. Coral reefs are found in warm, shallow ocean waters and are often called "rainforests of the sea" because they support incredible biodiversity.
Thousands of different species call coral reefs home, including colorful fish, sea turtles, sharks, and countless invertebrates. The reefs provide food, shelter, and breeding grounds for these animals. Coral reefs also protect coastlines from storms and waves, and provide food and income for millions of people worldwide. Unfortunately, pollution, warming ocean temperatures, and other human activities are threatening coral reefs around the globe.

	•	large fish
	•	underwater plants
2. Why are coral reefs important?
	•	they support biodiversity and protect coastlines
	•	they are pretty to look at
	•	they make the water warmer

Text 10: Architects
Architects are professionals who design buildings and other structures where people live, work, and play. They create detailed plans that show how buildings should look and function, considering factors like safety, beauty, cost, and how the building will be used. Architects must understand engineering principles, local building codes, and environmental concerns when creating their designs.
Before any construction begins, architects work closely with their clients to understand their needs and preferences. They create drawings, blueprints, and sometimes even computer models to show what the finished building will look like. Architects also coordinate with engineers, contractors, and other specialists throughout the building process to ensure that their vision becomes reality safely and efficiently.

2. Who do architects work with during the building process?
	•	engineers, contractors, and other specialists
	•	only their clients
	•	nobody else
