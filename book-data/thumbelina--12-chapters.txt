Chapter 1: The Magic Seed
There once lived a woman who wanted a child more than anything in the world. She had wished for a baby for many years, but her wish never came true.
One day, she decided to visit a wise old witch who lived at the edge of town. The witch was known for helping people with impossible problems.
'I want a child so badly,' the woman told the witch. 'Even a tiny child would make me happy.'
The witch smiled kindly. 'I can help you,' she said. She gave the woman a small barley seed. 'Plant this in a flower pot and water it carefully.'
The woman hurried home with the magic seed. She planted it in her prettiest flower pot and watered it every day. She spoke to it gently and waited with hope.
After just a few days, a green shoot appeared. It grew quickly into a beautiful plant with one large bud at the top.
The bud looked like a tulip, but it was bigger and more colorful than any tulip the woman had ever seen. She couldn't wait for it to bloom.

Chapter 2: Born from a Flower
One morning, the woman noticed that the bud was ready to open. She leaned close and gently kissed the colorful petals.
Suddenly, the flower burst into bloom with a soft musical sound. The petals opened like the pages of a book, revealing something amazing inside.
Sitting in the center of the flower was the tiniest, most beautiful girl the woman had ever seen. She was no bigger than a thumb, with golden hair and the sweetest smile.
'I'll call you Thumbelina,' said the woman joyfully. 'You're the perfect size, and you're more precious than any treasure.'
The woman made Thumbelina a bed from a polished walnut shell. She used violet petals for blankets and a rose petal for a pillow.
During the day, Thumbelina sailed on a plate of water in a tulip petal boat. She used two white horsehairs as oars to row around her tiny lake.
At night, she sang the most beautiful songs. Her voice was like tiny silver bells ringing in the evening breeze. The woman had never been happier.

Chapter 3: The Toad's Plan
One night while Thumbelina slept in her walnut shell bed, an old toad hopped through the open window. She saw the tiny girl and had a terrible idea.
'What a perfect wife she would make for my son!' croaked the toad. 'She's so pretty and small. My son will love her.'
The toad carefully picked up the walnut shell with sleeping Thumbelina inside. She hopped out the window and down to the muddy stream behind the house.
Far out in the stream was a large lily pad where the toad lived with her ugly son. The son had big bulging eyes and a warty green body.
'Look what I brought you!' said the mother toad excitedly. 'A beautiful bride! She's perfect for you, my dear son.'
The son toad croaked with delight when he saw Thumbelina. 'She's wonderful, Mother! When can we be married?'
'We must prepare for the wedding,' said the mother toad. 'I'll put her on a lily pad in the middle of the stream so she can't run away.'

Chapter 4: Escape on the Lily Pad
When Thumbelina woke up, she found herself on a lily pad surrounded by water. She could see the two toads preparing for a wedding on the far shore.
'Oh no!' cried Thumbelina. 'I don't want to marry that toad! He's slimy and ugly, and I want to go home to my dear mother!'
She began to cry, and her tears fell into the stream like tiny diamonds. The fish swimming below heard her sobs and felt sorry for her.
'We must help this poor little girl,' said the fish to each other. 'She doesn't belong with those horrible toads.'
Working together, the fish nibbled through the green stem that held the lily pad in place. Soon the lily pad broke free and began floating downstream.
Thumbelina sailed away from the toads, using her sash as a sail to catch the breeze. 'Thank you, dear fish!' she called as she floated to freedom.
She drifted past meadows and forests, seeing many beautiful sights. A lovely white butterfly landed on her lily pad and became her traveling companion.

Chapter 5: The Beetle's Rejection
As Thumbelina floated down the stream, a large black beetle flew down from a tree. He grabbed her around her tiny waist and carried her up to his branch.
'How pretty you are!' said the beetle, looking at Thumbelina with his big round eyes. 'You would make a lovely beetle wife.'
But when the other beetles came to see Thumbelina, they wrinkled their noses in disgust. 'She's so strange!' they buzzed. 'She only has two legs, not six like us!'
'Look, she doesn't even have antennae!' laughed another beetle. 'And she's not black and shiny like a proper beetle should be!'
The beetles made fun of Thumbelina until the first beetle began to think she wasn't pretty after all. 'Maybe they're right,' he said sadly.
'I'm sorry, little one,' said the beetle. 'I can't keep you if the others don't like you.' He flew down and set Thumbelina gently on a daisy.
Thumbelina sat alone on the flower, feeling very sad. First the toads wanted to marry her to someone terrible, and now the beetles rejected her completely.

Chapter 6: Alone in the Field
All summer long, Thumbelina lived alone in the forest. She wove herself a bed from grass blades and hung it under a large burdock leaf to keep dry.
She drank dewdrops from flower petals and ate sweet honey from the flowers. The forest creatures were kind to her, and she made friends with butterflies and bees.
But as autumn came, the flowers began to die. The leaves turned brown and fell from the trees. Cold winds blew through the forest.
Thumbelina's dress became torn and dirty. She shivered in the cold and could find very little food. The burdock leaf above her bed was turning brown and brittle.
Soon winter arrived with snow and ice. Thumbelina was so cold that her teeth chattered. She wrapped herself in a fallen leaf, but it wasn't warm enough.
'I must find shelter,' she said bravely. 'I can't survive the winter out here alone.' She walked through the frozen field, looking for help.
At the edge of the field, she found a small hole in the ground. It was the home of a field mouse who had plenty of food stored for winter.

Chapter 7: Winter with the Field Mouse
The field mouse was a tidy, careful creature who kept her underground home very clean and organized. She had stored grain and nuts for the cold months.
'Please help me,' begged Thumbelina. 'I'm so cold and hungry. I'll work hard if you let me stay with you through the winter.'
The field mouse looked at the tiny, shivering girl and felt sorry for her. 'You may stay,' she said. 'But you must keep my house clean and tell me stories.'
Thumbelina was grateful for the warm, dry home. She swept and dusted every day. In the evenings, she told the field mouse beautiful stories about the world above ground.
'You're a good worker and storyteller,' said the field mouse. 'I have a neighbor, Mr. Mole, who is very rich. Perhaps you'd like to meet him.'
The mole lived in tunnels deep underground. He wore a black velvet coat and had tiny eyes that could barely see. He never came up to see the sun or flowers.
When the mole heard Thumbelina's sweet voice, he fell in love immediately. 'She would make me a perfect wife,' he told the field mouse quietly.

Chapter 8: The Mole's Proposal
Mr. Mole invited Thumbelina and the field mouse to visit his underground kingdom. His tunnels were long and dark, lit only by glowing mushrooms.
'Look at my beautiful home,' said the mole proudly. 'I have rooms full of treasure and food that will last forever. You could be very happy here.'
As they walked through one tunnel, they found a swallow lying still on the ground. The mole kicked at it with his foot. 'Silly bird! It should have flown south.'
'Now it's dead from the cold,' said the field mouse. 'That's what happens to creatures who love the sky too much instead of staying safe underground.'
Thumbelina felt sad when she saw the beautiful bird. She remembered how wonderful it was to see the sky and flowers. The underground world felt like a prison to her.
That evening, the mole asked the field mouse for permission to marry Thumbelina. 'She's perfect for me,' he said. 'The wedding will be in spring.'
The field mouse was delighted. 'What a wonderful match!' she told Thumbelina. 'You'll be rich and safe forever in the mole's tunnels.'
But Thumbelina felt her heart break. She didn't want to live underground forever, never seeing flowers or sunshine again.

Chapter 9: The Swallow's Story
That night, Thumbelina couldn't sleep. She kept thinking about the poor swallow lying cold in the mole's tunnel. She decided to go see it once more.
Quietly, she crept through the dark tunnel until she found the bird. She knelt beside it and placed her hand on its feathers. Suddenly, she felt something wonderful!
The swallow's heart was still beating! It wasn't dead at all, just very cold and weak. 'Oh, you poor bird,' whispered Thumbelina. 'I must help you.'
She ran back to get a soft cloth and some warm water. All night, she cared for the swallow, covering it gently and bringing it drops of water.
By morning, the swallow opened its bright eyes. 'Thank you, sweet child,' it chirped softly. 'You saved my life. I was injured and couldn't fly south with the other birds.'
'Don't talk too loudly,' warned Thumbelina. 'The mole and field mouse don't like birds. They think you should live underground like they do.'
All winter, Thumbelina secretly cared for the swallow. She brought it food and water, and they became the best of friends.

Chapter 10: A Kind Deed
As spring arrived, the swallow grew strong and healthy again. Its feathers were glossy, and it could flutter its wings eagerly.
'I must fly away now,' the swallow told Thumbelina. 'The warm weather is calling me to join my friends. Come with me! We can fly far from here.'
Thumbelina wanted to go more than anything, but she shook her head sadly. 'The field mouse has been kind to me all winter. I can't leave her now.'
'You have such a good heart,' said the swallow. 'But are you sure? The mole wants to marry you, and you'll have to live underground forever.'
'I know,' sighed Thumbelina. 'But I must keep my promise to the field mouse. She helped me when I had nowhere else to go.'
The swallow sang a beautiful goodbye song for Thumbelina. Then it flew up through a crack in the tunnel ceiling, up into the bright spring sky.
Thumbelina watched until she couldn't see her friend anymore. Now she felt more alone than ever, knowing her wedding day was coming soon.

Chapter 11: Flying South
Summer came, and the field mouse and mole began preparing for the wedding. 'You must make yourself a beautiful wedding dress,' the field mouse told Thumbelina.
Thumbelina spent her days sewing, but her heart was heavy. Soon she would live deep underground and never see the sun again.
On the day before the wedding, Thumbelina asked to go outside one last time. 'Please,' she begged. 'Let me say goodbye to the sunshine and flowers.'
As she stood in the warm sunlight, crying softly, she heard a familiar song above her head. 'Tweet-tweet!' It was her dear friend, the swallow!
'Thumbelina!' called the swallow joyfully. 'I've come back for you! I can't let you marry that mole and live underground. Come with me now!'
'But what about the field mouse?' asked Thumbelina. 'She'll be so upset if I leave.'
'She'll understand someday,' said the swallow gently. 'You saved my life, and now I want to save yours. Climb on my back, and we'll fly to a warm, beautiful land.'
Thumbelina made her choice. She climbed onto the swallow's back and held tight to its soft feathers. Up, up they flew, into the bright blue sky.

Chapter 12: The Flower Kingdom
The swallow carried Thumbelina far away to a warm country where flowers bloomed all year long. Beautiful white palaces sat among gardens of roses and jasmine.
'This is where I spend my winters,' said the swallow. 'Isn't it wonderful? Look down at that white flower - there's someone special I want you to meet.'
In the center of a large white flower sat a tiny person just like Thumbelina! He had gossamer wings on his back and wore a golden crown.
'Welcome!' said the tiny prince. 'I'm the king of the flower people. We've all been waiting to meet someone as special as you.'
All around the garden, Thumbelina saw other tiny people with beautiful wings, living happily in the flowers. They were just her size and welcomed her warmly.
'Will you be my queen?' asked the flower prince. 'We could rule the garden kingdom together and help all the flowers bloom.'
Thumbelina's heart filled with joy. At last, she had found where she truly belonged! She said yes, and the flower people gave her gossamer wings of her own.
From that day forward, Thumbelina lived happily in the flower kingdom. She helped flowers bloom and made friends with all the garden creatures. The swallow visited her every spring, and they remained the best of friends forever.

The End
