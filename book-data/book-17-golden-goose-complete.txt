Chapter 1: A Farmer's Good Fortune
Page 1
In a modest farming community where hardworking people struggled each day to earn enough money to support their families and maintain their simple way of life, there lived a poor but honest farmer named Harold who possessed very few material possessions beyond the basic necessities required for survival. His small cottage was humble but clean, his few acres of land were carefully tended despite their limited productivity, and his livestock consisted of only the most essential animals needed for subsistence farming.
Page 2
Among Harold's modest collection of farm animals, there was one particularly unremarkable white goose that he had acquired several years earlier in a trade with a traveling merchant who claimed the bird came from a distant land with special properties. The farmer had been somewhat skeptical of the merchant's mysterious claims about the goose's supposed magical abilities, but he needed the bird to help provide eggs and meat for his family's nutritional needs.
Page 3
For several seasons, the plain-looking goose had lived quietly in Harold's modest farmyard, apparently no different from any other domestic fowl in the region. She consumed the same simple feed as the other birds, required the same basic care and attention, and seemed to possess no extraordinary characteristics that would distinguish her from countless other geese owned by farmers throughout the countryside. Harold had almost forgotten about the merchant's unusual claims.
Page 4
One bright morning in early spring, as Harold performed his routine daily chores of feeding his animals and checking their nests for fresh eggs, he discovered something that would change his life forever. There, nestled carefully in the goose's simple straw nest, lay what appeared to be an ordinary egg - except that this egg gleamed with the unmistakable luster of pure gold, its surface reflecting the morning sunlight with brilliant, precious radiance that took his breath away.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. subsistence - having just enough to survive; the minimum needed to live
2. luster - a gentle shine or glow

Chapter 2: Wealth Beyond Imagination
Page 1
Harold's initial reaction to the miraculous discovery was a mixture of disbelief, wonder, and cautious excitement as he carefully lifted the extraordinarily heavy golden egg from the nest with trembling hands. He examined the precious object from every angle, testing its authenticity by feeling its substantial weight and observing how the metal's surface reflected light with the characteristic properties of genuine gold that he had seen in the few pieces of jewelry owned by the wealthy merchants who occasionally visited their remote village.
Page 2
When Harold took the golden egg to the village goldsmith for professional evaluation and verification, the expert confirmed that the egg was indeed composed of the purest gold he had ever examined, with a value that exceeded Harold's annual farm income by a considerable margin. The goldsmith offered to purchase the remarkable treasure immediately, providing Harold with more money than he had ever possessed in his entire lifetime of agricultural labor.
Page 3
The poor farmer could hardly comprehend his extraordinary good fortune as he returned home with a heavy purse filled with gold coins, his mind racing with possibilities for how this unexpected wealth could improve his family's living conditions and secure their future prosperity. He could repair his aging cottage, purchase better farming equipment, buy additional livestock, and perhaps even acquire more fertile land to expand his agricultural operations significantly.
Page 4
But the most remarkable aspect of Harold's miraculous discovery was yet to be revealed. The following morning, when he approached the goose's nest with eager anticipation, he found another golden egg waiting for him, gleaming just as brilliantly as the first one had. Day after day, week after week, the magical goose continued to produce these precious gifts with reliable consistency, transforming Harold from a struggling subsistence farmer into one of the wealthiest men in his entire region.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real
2. consistency - the quality of always behaving in the same way

Chapter 3: Growing Prosperity and Comfort
Page 1
As the months passed and Harold's wealth continued to accumulate steadily through his goose's remarkable daily production of golden eggs, his entire lifestyle underwent a dramatic transformation that elevated him from poverty to prosperity beyond anything he had ever dared to imagine. He used his newfound riches wisely and responsibly, investing in practical improvements that would benefit both his family and his farming operations for generations to come.
Page 2
Harold constructed a magnificent new farmhouse to replace his humble cottage, complete with spacious rooms, fine furniture, and all the modern conveniences available to wealthy landowners of his era. He purchased the finest agricultural equipment that money could buy, acquired additional fertile acreage to expand his operations, and invested in premium livestock that would enhance both the productivity and prestige of his farming enterprise significantly.
Page 3
The magical goose that had made all of this prosperity possible was given the most luxurious accommodations that Harold could provide, including a specially constructed golden pen with the finest bedding, the most nutritious feed available, and constant protection from any potential threats or disturbances. Harold treated this extraordinary bird with the reverence and care that such a miraculous creature deserved, understanding that his continued wealth depended entirely upon her well-being and contentment.
Page 4
For several years, Harold lived contentedly with this arrangement, appreciating the steady flow of golden eggs that provided him with a comfortable income and financial security that allowed him to sleep peacefully each night without worrying about money, food, or shelter. He became known throughout the region as a generous and successful farmer who had been blessed with extraordinary good fortune, and his reputation for honesty and integrity remained intact despite his newfound wealth.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. reverence - deep respect and admiration
2. contentedly - in a satisfied and happy way

Chapter 4: The Seed of Greed
Page 1
Unfortunately, as Harold's wealth continued to grow and his lifestyle became increasingly luxurious, a subtle but dangerous change began to occur in his character and mindset that would eventually threaten everything he had achieved. The steady accumulation of riches had awakened within him a hunger for even greater wealth and faster acquisition of treasure, replacing his original gratitude and contentment with an insatiable desire for more immediate and substantial rewards from his miraculous goose.
Page 2
Where Harold had once been perfectly satisfied with receiving one precious golden egg each day, he now found himself growing impatient with what he began to perceive as the goose's slow and inefficient production schedule. "Why should I wait for one egg per day," he began to wonder with increasing frustration, "when there must be dozens or even hundreds of golden eggs stored inside this remarkable bird, waiting to be harvested all at once?"
Page 3
This dangerous line of thinking consumed more and more of Harold's attention as his greed gradually overpowered his common sense and practical wisdom. He spent sleepless nights calculating how much wealthier he could become if he could somehow access the goose's entire supply of golden eggs immediately, rather than waiting patiently for the natural daily process that had served him so well for several prosperous years.
Page 4
Harold's obsession with obtaining instant wealth began to affect his judgment and decision-making abilities in increasingly troubling ways. He started viewing his miraculous goose not as a living creature that deserved care and respect, but simply as a container holding vast riches that belonged to him by right. The gratitude and reverence he had once felt for this extraordinary gift were slowly being replaced by impatience, frustration, and a dangerous sense of entitlement that would soon lead him to make a catastrophic mistake.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. insatiable - impossible to satisfy; always wanting more
2. entitlement - the belief that you deserve something

	•	With even more respect than before

Chapter 5: A Fatal Decision
Page 1
Harold's greed and impatience finally reached a critical breaking point when he could no longer tolerate what he perceived as the unbearably slow pace of his wealth accumulation through the goose's daily egg production. His obsessive thoughts about instant riches had completely overwhelmed his capacity for rational thinking and sound judgment, leading him to formulate a reckless plan that he believed would solve all of his imaginary problems and provide him with immediate access to unlimited wealth.
Page 2
"If this goose can produce one golden egg each day," Harold reasoned with fatally flawed logic, "then she must have dozens or perhaps hundreds of these precious eggs stored somewhere inside her body, waiting to be released naturally over time. Why should I continue to wait patiently for this slow process when I could simply open the goose and claim all of those golden treasures immediately?"
Page 3
This catastrophically misguided plan consumed Harold's thoughts completely as he convinced himself that killing his miraculous goose would be a wise investment in his financial future rather than the act of supreme folly that it actually represented. He had become so blinded by greed that he could no longer see the obvious truth: that destroying the source of his wealth would inevitably result in the permanent loss of everything he had gained through the goose's magical abilities.
Page 4
On a dark morning when his patience had finally been exhausted by what he perceived as an intolerable delay in achieving maximum wealth, Harold approached his precious goose with a sharp knife, his hands trembling with a mixture of excitement and nervous anticipation about the vast riches he expected to discover. Without giving himself time to reconsider the irreversible nature of his actions or to remember the years of prosperity and security that this remarkable bird had already provided, he committed the ultimate act of destructive greed.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. fatally - in a way that causes death or disaster
2. irreversible - impossible to undo or change back

	•	Love for the goose
	•	Greed
	•	Fear

Chapter 6: The Bitter Consequences
Page 1
When Harold cut open his miraculous goose with trembling hands and desperate expectations of discovering countless golden eggs hidden within her body, he was confronted instead with the shocking and devastating reality that there were no golden treasures waiting inside. The goose was simply a normal bird with ordinary internal organs, no different from any other domestic fowl except for her extraordinary magical ability, which had now been permanently destroyed by his act of supreme greed and impatience.
Page 2
The horrible truth of what he had done struck Harold with the force of a physical blow as he stared down at the lifeless body of the creature that had provided him with years of prosperity, security, and comfort. In his blind pursuit of immediate wealth, he had not only destroyed his source of daily golden eggs, but had also eliminated any possibility of future riches from this miraculous gift that could never be replaced or restored.
Page 3
Harold's grief and regret were overwhelming as he realized the full magnitude of his catastrophic mistake. The steady income that had transformed him from a poor farmer into a wealthy landowner was gone forever, and without the daily production of golden eggs, his extravagant lifestyle could not be sustained for much longer. He had traded years of guaranteed prosperity for nothing more than empty dreams of instant riches that had proven to be completely illusory.
Page 4
As Harold sat in his luxurious farmhouse surrounded by all the material possessions that his greed had now made impossible to maintain, he understood too late the profound wisdom of the old saying that warns against killing the goose that lays the golden eggs. His impatience and insatiable desire for more had cost him everything he had worked to achieve, teaching him through bitter experience that contentment with steady blessings is far more valuable than the dangerous pursuit of unrealistic dreams that promise everything but deliver only loss and regret.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. magnitude - the great size or extent of something
2. illusory - based on illusion; not real
