Chapter 1: The Longing
Once upon a time, there lived a man and woman who had wanted a child for many years. They loved each other dearly and dreamed of having a baby to complete their family.
Their small house sat at the edge of a village, and from their back window, they could see into a beautiful garden filled with the most wonderful plants and flowers.
The garden belonged to a powerful witch who was feared throughout the land. No one dared to enter her garden, for everyone knew terrible things happened to those who trespassed.
One day, the woman discovered she was finally going to have a baby. She was so happy that she spent hours sitting by the window, looking out at the world with joy.
As she gazed into the witch's garden, she noticed a patch of the most beautiful rapunzel plants. The leafy greens looked so fresh and delicious that her mouth began to water.
'Oh, how I long for some of that rapunzel,' she said to herself. 'It looks so crisp and green. I've never wanted anything so much in my life.'
Day after day, her craving for the rapunzel grew stronger. She could think of nothing else. Soon, she could barely eat any other food.

Chapter 2: The Enchanted Garden
The woman grew pale and thin from her longing for the rapunzel. Her husband became very worried about her health and the health of their unborn baby.
'My dear wife,' he said, 'you must eat something. You're wasting away! Tell me what you need, and I'll get it for you.'
'I need the rapunzel from the witch's garden,' she whispered weakly. 'If I don't get some soon, I fear I may die from wanting it so much.'
The husband loved his wife more than anything in the world. He couldn't bear to see her suffering, even though he was terrified of the witch.
That evening, as darkness fell, he climbed over the high wall into the witch's garden. His hands shook with fear, but his love for his wife gave him courage.
He quickly gathered some of the beautiful rapunzel plants and hurried home. His wife ate them eagerly, and for the first time in weeks, she smiled.
'This is the most delicious food I've ever tasted,' she said gratefully. 'Thank you, my brave husband, for risking so much for me.'

Chapter 3: Caught by the Witch
But now that the woman had tasted the rapunzel, her craving became even stronger. She begged her husband to get more of the magical plants.
'Please, my love,' she pleaded. 'Just once more. I need it so badly, and our baby needs me to be strong.'
The next night, the man climbed over the wall again. But this time, as he bent down to pick the rapunzel, a terrible voice spoke behind him.
'How dare you steal from my garden!' shrieked the witch. She appeared out of nowhere, her wild hair flying and her eyes glowing with anger.
The man fell to his knees, trembling with fear. 'Please forgive me,' he stammered. 'I only took it because my wife is expecting a baby, and she was dying from wanting your rapunzel.'
The witch's eyes narrowed as she studied the frightened man. 'So,' she said slowly, 'your wife is going to have a child, is she?'
'Yes,' whispered the man. 'Please don't hurt us. I'll do anything you ask, just don't harm my family.'

Chapter 4: The Terrible Bargain
The witch smiled, but it was not a kind smile. 'Very well,' she said. 'I will not harm you or your wife. In fact, I'll give you all the rapunzel she desires.'
The man's heart leaped with relief, but the witch wasn't finished speaking.
'However,' she continued, her voice growing cold, 'there is one condition for my mercy. When your child is born, you must give the baby to me.'
'No!' cried the man. 'I cannot give away my child! Please, ask for anything else!'
'This is my only offer,' said the witch firmly. 'Give me the child, or face terrible punishment for stealing from me. Choose now!'
The man thought of his weak, hungry wife waiting at home. If he refused, the witch might harm them all. If he agreed, at least his wife would live to see their child born.
With tears in his eyes, he nodded. 'I agree,' he whispered. 'But please, treat the child kindly.'

Chapter 5: Life in the Tower
When the baby was born, she was the most beautiful child anyone had ever seen. She had golden hair that shone like sunlight and bright blue eyes that sparkled like stars.
As promised, the witch came to take the baby away. The parents wept bitterly, but they had no choice but to keep their bargain.
The witch named the child Rapunzel, after the plant that had caused all the trouble. She took the baby far away to a tower deep in the forest.
The tower was very tall and had no door and no stairs. The only way in or out was through a small window at the very top.
As Rapunzel grew up, the witch cared for her needs but kept her completely isolated from the world. Rapunzel never saw another person and didn't even know other people existed.
The witch visited every day by calling up to the window: 'Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!' Then Rapunzel would lower her incredibly long, golden braid so the witch could climb up.
Rapunzel spent her days reading books, singing songs, and watching the seasons change through her window. But deep in her heart, she felt lonely and wondered what lay beyond the forest.

Chapter 6: The Golden Hair
By the time Rapunzel turned sixteen, her hair had grown incredibly long. It was the most beautiful hair in the world, as fine as spun gold and stronger than rope.
Every morning, Rapunzel would brush her hair and braid it carefully. The braid was so long that when she let it down from the window, it reached all the way to the ground.
Rapunzel had a beautiful voice and loved to sing. She would sit by her window and sing songs that she made up herself, filling the forest with lovely music.
The birds would gather on her windowsill to listen, and squirrels and rabbits would come out of hiding to hear her sweet melodies.
'I wonder if anyone else in the world loves music like I do,' Rapunzel would say to the birds. 'I wonder if there are others who sing and laugh and dream.'
The witch had told her that the world was dangerous and full of wicked people, but Rapunzel couldn't help wondering if that was really true.
Sometimes she would dream of walking through green meadows or dancing under the stars. But when she woke up, she was still alone in her tower.

Chapter 7: A Voice in the Forest
One day, a young prince was riding through the forest when he heard the most beautiful singing he had ever heard in his life.
The voice was so lovely and pure that it seemed to come from heaven itself. The prince followed the sound until he came to the tall tower.
He looked up and saw Rapunzel at her window, her golden hair catching the sunlight as she sang. The prince had never seen anyone so beautiful.
The prince wanted to meet this wonderful girl, but he could see no way to enter the tower. There was no door, and the walls were too smooth to climb.
He rode home with his heart full of music and his mind full of questions. 'Who is this girl?' he wondered. 'Why is she locked away in that tower?'
The next day, the prince returned to the tower, hoping to hear the beautiful voice again. This time, he hid behind the trees to watch.
Soon he heard someone approaching through the forest. An old woman came to the base of the tower and called out: 'Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!'

Chapter 8: The Prince's Discovery
The prince watched in amazement as a long, golden braid fell from the window. The old woman climbed up the hair like a rope and disappeared into the tower.
'So that's how to get in!' thought the prince. He waited until the old woman left, then he approached the tower himself.
'Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!' he called, copying the old woman's voice as best he could.
Down came the beautiful golden braid. The prince climbed up carefully, his heart pounding with excitement and nervousness.
When he reached the window, Rapunzel gasped in surprise. 'Who are you?' she asked, backing away in fear. 'You're not Dame Gothel!'
'Please don't be frightened,' said the prince gently. 'I heard your beautiful singing and wanted to meet you. I'm a prince from the kingdom beyond the forest.'
'A prince?' said Rapunzel in wonder. 'I didn't know there were other people in the world. Dame Gothel said I was the only one.'

Chapter 9: Secret Meetings
The prince told Rapunzel about the world beyond her tower - about cities and villages, about other people who laughed and sang and lived happy lives.
'There are so many wonderful things to see,' he said. 'Beautiful gardens, sparkling rivers, and festivals where people dance under the stars.'
Rapunzel listened with wide eyes. Everything the prince described sounded magical and exciting, so different from what Dame Gothel had told her.
'Would you like to see these things?' asked the prince. 'I could take you away from this tower to my kingdom, where you would be free.'
'I would love that more than anything,' said Rapunzel. 'But Dame Gothel says the world is dangerous. She says she keeps me here to protect me.'
'Does being locked in a tower feel like protection to you?' asked the prince kindly. 'Or does it feel like a prison?'
From that day on, the prince visited Rapunzel every afternoon when Dame Gothel was away. They would talk and laugh together, and Rapunzel felt happier than she ever had before.

Chapter 10: The Witch's Fury
One day, without thinking, Rapunzel said to Dame Gothel, 'Why are you so much heavier to pull up than the prince? He climbs much faster than you do!'
Dame Gothel's face turned white with rage. 'What prince?' she shrieked. 'How dare you lie to me! I am the only person who comes here!'
Too late, Rapunzel realized her mistake. She tried to explain, but Dame Gothel was furious beyond reason.
'You ungrateful child!' screamed the witch. 'After all I've done to protect you, you've been meeting with strangers behind my back!'
Before Rapunzel could say another word, Dame Gothel grabbed a pair of scissors and cut off all of Rapunzel's beautiful hair.
'There!' said the witch, holding the golden braids. 'Now your precious prince won't be able to climb up to see you anymore!'
Then she used her magic to transport Rapunzel far away to a lonely desert, where she left the girl to wander alone and frightened.

Chapter 11: Lost and Found
That evening, the prince came to the tower as usual. 'Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair!' he called.
Down came the golden braid, but when the prince climbed up, he found Dame Gothel waiting for him instead of Rapunzel.
'Looking for your little songbird?' cackled the witch. 'She's gone forever! You'll never see her again!'
In his shock and despair, the prince fell from the tower window. He landed in a patch of thorny bushes that saved his life but scratched his eyes so badly that he became blind.
For months, the prince wandered through the forest, calling Rapunzel's name. He lived on berries and roots, never giving up hope of finding her.
Meanwhile, Rapunzel survived in the desert by her courage and cleverness. She found water at oases and ate fruits and nuts from the few trees that grew there.
One day, the prince's wandering brought him to the edge of the desert. In the distance, he heard a familiar sound - someone singing a sad, beautiful song.

Chapter 12: Love's True Power
'Rapunzel!' cried the prince, stumbling toward the voice. 'Is it really you?'
'My dear prince!' sobbed Rapunzel, running to him. She was overjoyed to see him again, but heartbroken when she saw that he was blind.
'What has Dame Gothel done to you?' she cried, holding his face gently in her hands. Tears of love and sorrow fell from her eyes onto his face.
The moment her tears touched his eyes, something magical happened. The prince's sight was restored, and he could see Rapunzel's beautiful face again.
'Your love has healed me,' said the prince in wonder. 'Just as your voice first healed my lonely heart.'
Together, they traveled to the prince's kingdom, where they were married with great celebration. Rapunzel's parents, who had never stopped hoping to see their daughter again, were invited to the wedding.
The family was reunited at last, and they forgave each other for the painful choices they had made so long ago.
Rapunzel and the prince ruled their kingdom with wisdom and kindness. They lived happily ever after, and their palace was always filled with music, laughter, and love.

The End
