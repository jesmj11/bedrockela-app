Text 1: Honeybees
Honeybees are amazing insects that live together in groups called colonies. A colony can have thousands of bees working together! The bees build their homes out of wax in places like hollow trees or special boxes called hives. Each bee has a job to do, like gathering food, taking care of baby bees, or protecting the hive.
Honeybees make honey by collecting nectar from flowers. They use their long tongues to suck up the sweet liquid from inside the flowers. Back at the hive, they turn the nectar into honey and store it in waxy cells. People have been keeping bees for thousands of years because honey tastes good and never spoils!

	•	a colony
	•	a swarm
2. How do bees make honey?
	•	by collecting nectar from flowers and turning it into honey
	•	by eating sugar and making it into honey
	•	by finding honey in the forest

Text 2: Apple Trees
Apple trees are fruit trees that grow in many parts of the world. These trees can live for a very long time, sometimes over 100 years! Apple trees need good soil, sunshine, and water to grow healthy and strong. In the spring, apple trees bloom with beautiful white or pink flowers that smell sweet.
After the flowers fall off, tiny apples start to grow. It takes all summer for the apples to get big and ripe. Different kinds of apple trees make different types of apples. Some apples are red, some are green, and some are yellow. People pick apples in the fall when they are ready to eat.

	•	in the fall
	•	in the spring
	•	in the winter
2. What do apple trees need to grow?
	•	good soil, sunshine, and water
	•	only water
	•	only sunshine

Text 3: Fire Trucks
Fire trucks are special vehicles that help firefighters save people and put out fires. These big red trucks carry lots of important equipment like hoses, ladders, and axes. Fire trucks also have a tank that holds hundreds of gallons of water. When there is a fire, the truck can pump water through long hoses to spray on the flames.
Fire trucks have loud sirens and flashing lights to warn other drivers to get out of the way. The firefighters need to get to fires quickly to help people and stop the fire from spreading. Some fire trucks have very tall ladders that can reach high windows in tall buildings. This helps firefighters rescue people who are trapped upstairs.

	•	only water
	•	food and blankets
2. Why do fire trucks have sirens and flashing lights?
	•	to look pretty
	•	to warn other drivers to get out of the way
	•	to make noise for fun

Text 4: Penguins
Penguins are black and white birds that cannot fly, but they are amazing swimmers! Most penguins live in cold places near the ocean, especially around Antarctica. They have thick feathers and fat under their skin to keep them warm in the icy water. Penguins waddle when they walk on land, but they can slide on their bellies across the ice.
Penguins eat fish, squid, and tiny sea animals called krill. They can dive deep underwater and hold their breath for a long time while they hunt for food. Baby penguins are called chicks, and their parents take turns keeping the eggs warm and bringing food. Penguin families stay together and help take care of each other.
Comprehension Questions
1. Where do most penguins live?
	•	in hot deserts
	•	in cold places near the ocean
	•	in the forest
2. What do penguins eat?
	•	fish, squid, and krill
	•	grass and leaves
	•	nuts and berries

Text 5: The Sun
The Sun is a star that gives Earth light and heat. Even though the Sun looks small in the sky, it is actually huge – much, much bigger than Earth! The Sun is so hot that nothing can live on it. The temperature on the Sun is thousands of degrees, hot enough to melt any metal instantly.
Without the Sun, there would be no life on Earth. Plants need sunlight to grow, and animals need plants for food. The Sun also makes the water cycle work by heating up water and turning it into clouds. During the day, the Sun lights up our part of Earth, and at night, our part of Earth faces away from the Sun so it gets dark.
Comprehension Questions
1. What is the Sun?
	•	a planet
	•	a star
	•	a moon
2. Why do we need the Sun?
	•	it gives Earth light and heat that plants and animals need
	•	it makes pretty colors in the sky
	•	it helps us tell time

Text 6: Librarians
Librarians are people who work in libraries to help others find books and information. They know where to find all kinds of books – stories, science books, history books, and more. Librarians also help people use computers to research topics they want to learn about. Many librarians have special training to learn how to organize books and help people.
Besides helping people find books, librarians do many other jobs. They read stories to children, plan fun programs and activities, and teach people how to use the library. Some libraries have librarians who specialize in helping children or teenagers. Librarians work hard to make sure everyone feels welcome in the library and can find what they need.
Comprehension Questions
1. What is the main job of librarians?
	•	to help people find books and information
	•	to write books
	•	to clean the library
2. What other activities do librarians do?
	•	read stories and plan programs
	•	cook food for visitors
	•	fix broken computers

Text 7: Butterflies
Butterflies start their lives as tiny eggs laid on leaves. When the eggs hatch, little caterpillars come out and start eating the leaves. The caterpillars eat and eat until they grow bigger and bigger. After a few weeks, each caterpillar makes a hard shell called a chrysalis around itself.
Inside the chrysalis, something amazing happens! The caterpillar changes into a beautiful butterfly with colorful wings. This change is called metamorphosis. When the butterfly is ready, it breaks out of the chrysalis and flies away. Adult butterflies drink sweet nectar from flowers and help plants by carrying pollen from flower to flower.
Comprehension Questions
1. What do caterpillars do after they hatch from eggs?
	•	they start eating leaves
	•	they fly away
	•	they hide under rocks
2. What is the change from caterpillar to butterfly called?
	•	growing
	•	metamorphosis
	•	sleeping

Text 8: Dentists
Dentists are doctors who take care of people's teeth and gums. They help keep our mouths healthy by cleaning our teeth and checking for problems. Dentists use special tools to look inside our mouths and take X-ray pictures of our teeth. They can see cavities, which are little holes in teeth caused by bacteria.
When dentists find cavities, they can fix them by filling the holes with special materials. Dentists also teach people how to brush and floss their teeth properly to prevent cavities. Most people should visit the dentist twice a year for a checkup and cleaning. Going to the dentist regularly helps keep our teeth strong and healthy for our whole lives.
Comprehension Questions
1. What are cavities?
	•	little holes in teeth caused by bacteria
	•	white spots on teeth
	•	new teeth growing in
2. How often should people visit the dentist?
	•	once a year
	•	twice a year
	•	every month

Text 9: Rain
Rain is water that falls from clouds in the sky. It starts when the sun heats up water in oceans, rivers, and lakes, turning it into invisible water vapor that rises into the air. High up in the sky, the water vapor cools down and forms tiny water droplets that stick to dust particles in the air.
These tiny droplets join together to make bigger droplets inside clouds. When the droplets get heavy enough, they fall down as rain. Rain is very important because it gives plants the water they need to grow. It also fills up rivers, lakes, and underground water sources that people and animals need for drinking.
Comprehension Questions
1. How does rain start?
	•	the sun heats up water and turns it into water vapor
	•	clouds get angry and cry
	•	the wind pushes water from the ocean
2. Why is rain important?
	•	it gives plants water and fills up rivers and lakes
	•	it washes cars
	•	it makes rainbows

Text 10: Police Officers
Police officers are people who work to keep communities safe. They patrol neighborhoods in police cars, on foot, or on bicycles to watch for problems and help people who need assistance. Police officers learn about laws and how to help solve conflicts between people. They also direct traffic and make sure drivers follow traffic rules.
When someone breaks the law or when there is an emergency, people call the police for help. Police officers respond quickly to help solve problems and keep everyone safe. They also visit schools and community centers to teach children about safety and being good citizens. Many police officers work with special partners like police dogs who help them do their jobs.
Comprehension Questions
1. What is the main job of police officers?
	•	to keep communities safe
	•	to fix broken cars
	•	to deliver mail
2. Where do police officers patrol?
	•	neighborhoods in cars, on foot, or on bicycles
	•	only inside police stations
	•	only on highways
