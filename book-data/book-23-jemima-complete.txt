Chapter 1: Jemima's Maternal Yearning
Page 1
In a bustling farmyard where domestic fowl of all varieties lived together under the careful supervision of the farmer and his wife, there resided a beautiful white duck named Jemima Puddle-Duck who possessed an exceptionally elegant appearance and graceful demeanor that distinguished her from all the other barnyard residents. Her pristine white feathers gleamed in the morning sunlight, and her bright orange bill and webbed feet gave her a dignified bearing that commanded respect from chickens, geese, and other waterfowl throughout the agricultural community.
Page 2
However, despite her outward beauty and apparent contentment with farm life, Jemima harbored a deep and persistent frustration that consumed her thoughts and filled her heart with growing resentment toward the humans who managed her daily existence. More than anything else in the world, she longed to fulfill her natural maternal instincts by hatching a clutch of ducklings that she could raise, protect, and nurture with the devoted care that only a loving mother could provide to her precious offspring.
Page 3
Unfortunately for Jemima's maternal ambitions, the farmer's wife had established a firm policy of collecting all duck eggs immediately after they were laid, preventing any possibility of natural incubation and hatching that would allow Jemima to experience the profound joy of motherhood. This systematic interference with her biological purposes created an emotional conflict that grew more intense with each passing day, as Jemima watched her precious eggs disappear into the farmhouse kitchen rather than developing into the ducklings she desperately desired to nurture.
Page 4
"It is perfectly outrageous!" Jemima declared indignantly to anyone who would listen to her complaints about this unfair treatment. "I am quite capable of hatching my own eggs and raising my own ducklings without any human interference or assistance. Why should I be denied the fundamental right to experience motherhood simply because the farmer's wife prefers to use my eggs for cooking rather than allowing them to serve their natural biological purpose of creating new life?"
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. maternal - relating to being a mother or motherhood
2. incubation - the process of keeping eggs warm so they will hatch

Chapter 2: A Secretive Plan
Page 1
Driven by her overwhelming desire for motherhood and growing frustration with the constraints imposed by farm life, Jemima decided to take matters into her own wings by developing a secretive plan that would allow her to circumvent the farmer's wife's egg-collecting routine and achieve her maternal goals through independent action. She would venture beyond the familiar safety of the farmyard into the mysterious depths of the surrounding countryside, where she could find a private nesting location far from human interference and supervision.
Page 2
Early one misty morning, while the farmer and his wife were still sleeping and the other barnyard animals had not yet begun their daily activities, Jemima quietly slipped away from her familiar surroundings and began waddling determinedly across the dewy meadows toward the dark woods that bordered the agricultural property. Her heart beat with excitement and nervous anticipation as she embarked upon this bold adventure, believing that her superior intelligence and natural instincts would guide her safely through any challenges she might encounter.
Page 3
Despite her confidence and determination, Jemima had never before ventured so far from the protective boundaries of the farm, and she soon discovered that navigating through unfamiliar terrain while searching for suitable nesting sites presented unexpected difficulties and potential dangers that she had not anticipated during her careful planning. The dense forest seemed much larger and more confusing than it had appeared from the comfortable distance of the farmyard, and strange sounds and shadows made her increasingly aware of her vulnerability.
Page 4
As Jemima wandered deeper into the woodland, desperately searching for the perfect location to establish her secret nest, she began to realize that her romantic notions about independent motherhood had failed to account for the practical realities of survival in the wild. The forest floor was covered with thorny undergrowth that caught her delicate feathers, strange rustling sounds suggested the presence of unknown creatures, and the growing distance from familiar territory made her feel increasingly isolated and uncertain about her ambitious plan.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. circumvent - to find a way around an obstacle or problem
2. vulnerability - the state of being exposed to harm or attack

Chapter 3: An Elegant Stranger
Page 1
Just as Jemima was beginning to feel overwhelmed by the challenges of her woodland adventure and starting to question the wisdom of her independent expedition, she encountered a distinguished gentleman who appeared to offer exactly the kind of assistance and understanding that she desperately needed. This elegant stranger possessed refined manners, spoke with sophisticated vocabulary, and demonstrated such charming courtesy toward her predicament that Jemima immediately felt relieved to have found someone who appreciated her intelligence and respected her maternal ambitions.
Page 2
The mysterious gentleman, who introduced himself with impeccable politeness and expressed genuine interest in her situation, had russet-colored fur, pointed ears, and a magnificently bushy tail that gave him an aristocratic appearance befitting his cultured manner of speaking and obvious education. "My dear madam," he said with gracious formality, "I can see that you are a duck of exceptional refinement and intelligence who deserves far better treatment than you have apparently received from those ignorant farmers who fail to appreciate your superior qualities."
Page 3
Jemima was absolutely delighted to encounter someone who not only understood her frustrations but also validated her belief that she possessed exceptional abilities and deserved special consideration that the farmer's wife had unfairly denied her. The gentleman's flattering words and sympathetic attitude made her feel appreciated and important in ways that she had never experienced during her years of mundane farm life, where her intelligence and maternal instincts had been consistently undervalued and dismissed.
Page 4
"I would be delighted to assist a lady of your obvious breeding and intelligence," the stranger continued with elaborate courtesy that made Jemima's heart flutter with pleasure and gratitude. "I happen to know of the perfect location for a discriminating mother such as yourself to establish a private nursery where you can hatch your precious eggs in complete security and comfort, far from the crude interference of those simple-minded farmers who clearly lack the sophistication necessary to appreciate your exceptional maternal capabilities."
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. distinguished - having an elegant and impressive appearance
2. validated - confirmed that someone's feelings or opinions are worthwhile
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions

	•	White fur and long ears
2. How did the stranger make Jemima feel?
	•	Frightened and suspicious
	•	Appreciated, important, and validated
	•	Bored and uninterested

Chapter 4: The Perfect Nesting Place
Page 1
The charming gentleman led Jemima deeper into the forest along winding paths that seemed to grow narrower and more secluded with each step, until they arrived at what appeared to be an ideally situated woodland shed tucked away in a private clearing surrounded by tall trees and dense undergrowth. "Here, my dear lady, is the perfect sanctuary for a sophisticated mother such as yourself," he announced with theatrical flourish, gesturing grandly toward the rustic building that seemed to offer exactly the kind of privacy and security that Jemima had been seeking.
Page 2
The shed's interior had been prepared with considerable attention to comfort and convenience, featuring soft hay arranged in an inviting nesting area, fresh water available in a clean basin, and various amenities that suggested someone had invested significant time and effort in creating an attractive environment for visiting waterfowl. Jemima was impressed by the thoughtful arrangements and touched by what she interpreted as evidence of the gentleman's genuine concern for her welfare and maternal success.
Page 3
"This is absolutely perfect!" exclaimed Jemima with undisguised delight and gratitude as she explored the cozy accommodations that seemed designed specifically to meet her needs and preferences. "You are truly a gentleman of exceptional kindness and understanding to provide such wonderful facilities for a stranger. I cannot adequately express my appreciation for your generosity in offering me this marvelous opportunity to achieve my maternal dreams in such comfortable and secure surroundings."
Page 4
The mysterious benefactor accepted her effusive praise with modest dignity, waving away her thanks with elegant gestures while assuring her that he was simply delighted to assist a lady of her obvious quality and refinement. "Think nothing of it, dear madam," he replied with courtly grace. "It brings me great pleasure to help someone who possesses such admirable determination and intelligence. Please make yourself completely at home, and feel free to begin your nesting activities whenever you feel ready to proceed with your important maternal project."
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. sanctuary - a safe place of refuge or protection
2. benefactor - a person who gives help or money to others

Chapter 5: Growing Suspicions
Page 1
As Jemima settled into her new woodland home and began the careful process of laying her precious eggs in the comfortable nest that her mysterious benefactor had provided, she initially felt nothing but satisfaction and gratitude for the way her independent adventure was progressing toward its desired conclusion. However, as days passed and she spent more time in the forest sanctuary, certain peculiar details about her situation began to penetrate her consciousness and create the first stirrings of uneasiness about her generous host's true intentions.
Page 2
The gentleman visitor appeared with suspicious regularity, always arriving precisely when Jemima was away from her nest gathering food or exercising her wings, and she began to notice that he displayed an unusually keen interest in the progress of her egg-laying activities that seemed to extend beyond mere polite curiosity. His questions about her nesting timeline grew more specific and insistent, and his elegant manners occasionally slipped to reveal glimpses of a more predatory nature that contradicted his carefully maintained facade of courteous concern.
Page 3
Moreover, Jemima began to observe disturbing evidence scattered around the woodland shed that suggested previous occupants had not enjoyed happy endings to their nesting experiences. Small bones, scattered feathers from various waterfowl, and other ominous remnants created a gradually mounting sense of dread that forced her to confront the possibility that her charming benefactor might not be the altruistic gentleman he appeared to be, but rather someone with far more sinister motivations for his apparent generosity.
Page 4
When her mysterious host began making seemingly innocent suggestions about seasoning herbs that would enhance the flavor of her eggs, and offered to help her gather sage, thyme, and other culinary ingredients that seemed more appropriate for cooking than nesting, Jemima's growing suspicions finally crystallized into the horrifying realization that she had fallen victim to an elaborate deception designed to lure her into a vulnerable position where she and her precious eggs could be easily harvested for someone else's dinner table.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. predatory - seeking to exploit or harm others
2. sinister - suggesting evil or harm

Chapter 6: A Timely Rescue
Page 1
Fortunately for Jemima's survival, her prolonged absence from the farm had not gone unnoticed by Kep, the intelligent and loyal collie dog who served as the farm's primary guardian and protector of all livestock under his care. This devoted canine had become increasingly concerned about the missing duck and had been conducting systematic searches throughout the surrounding countryside, following scent trails and investigating potential hiding places where a naive waterfowl might have encountered dangerous predators or fallen victim to the various hazards that threatened unwary farm animals.
Page 2
When Kep finally discovered Jemima's location through his persistent detective work, he immediately recognized the perilous situation she had unknowingly created for herself by trusting a creature whose true identity as a cunning fox made him one of the most dangerous enemies that any domestic fowl could possibly encounter. The experienced farm dog understood that immediate intervention was necessary to prevent a tragedy, and he quickly formulated a rescue plan that would require precise timing and coordinated action to execute successfully.
Page 3
Just as the deceptive fox was preparing to spring his carefully laid trap and harvest his anticipated feast of duck and eggs, Kep arrived with a small pack of farm dogs whose fierce barking and aggressive posturing immediately alerted Jemima to the true nature of her predicament while simultaneously frightening her would-be predator into abandoning his elaborate scheme. The fox's carefully maintained disguise of genteel courtesy evaporated instantly when confronted with genuine danger, revealing his true cowardly nature beneath the sophisticated facade he had used to manipulate his intended victim.
Page 4
Under Kep's protective escort, Jemima returned safely to the familiar security of her farmyard home, though her adventure had taught her valuable lessons about the dangers of naivety, the importance of skepticism when dealing with overly charming strangers, and the wisdom of appreciating the genuine care and protection provided by those who truly have her best interests at heart. While she never did achieve her dream of independent motherhood, she learned to find contentment in the safety and security of farm life, understanding that some freedoms come with risks that far outweigh their potential rewards.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. intervention - the action of coming between to help or change a situation
2. naivety - lack of experience or wisdom; being too trusting
