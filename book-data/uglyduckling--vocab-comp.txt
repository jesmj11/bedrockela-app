The Ugly Duckling
Chapter Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions
3rd Grade Teacher's Guide

Chapter 1: The Nest by the Lake
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. rushes - tall water plants that grow near lakes
	•	2. impatient - not wanting to wait any longer
	•	3. nestled - settled comfortably and safely
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where did the mother duck build her nest?
	•	2. How long had the mother duck been sitting on her eggs?
	•	3. How many eggs were in the nest?
	•	4. How was the fifth egg different from the other four?
	•	5. What did the mother duck use to make her nest comfortable?

Chapter 2: The Last Egg
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. chirped - made short, high sounds like a bird
	•	2. tumbled - rolled or fell in a clumsy way
	•	3. overjoyed - extremely happy
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What sound did the mother duck hear when the eggs started hatching?
	•	2. How many ducklings hatched from the first four eggs?
	•	3. What did the four baby ducklings look like?
	•	4. Which egg still hadn't hatched?
	•	5. What did the old duck think the large egg might be?

Chapter 3: The Different Duckling
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. stumbled - walked unsteadily, almost falling
	•	2. curiosity - wanting to know about something
	•	3. musical - having a pleasant sound like music
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How was the last duckling different from his brothers and sisters?
	•	2. What color were his feathers?
	•	3. How did his voice sound compared to the other ducklings?
	•	4. How did the mother duck react to her unusual baby?
	•	5. What did the mother duck plan to do the next day?

Chapter 4: Learning to Swim
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. sparkled - shone with small, bright lights
	•	2. naturally - easily, without having to learn
	•	3. grace - smooth and beautiful movement
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where did the mother duck take her babies for their first swimming lesson?
	•	2. How well did the four yellow ducklings swim?
	•	3. What was the gray duckling worried about?
	•	4. How well could the gray duckling swim compared to his siblings?
	•	5. What impressed the mother duck about the gray duckling's swimming?

Chapter 5: Cruel Words
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. whisper - talk very quietly
	•	2. awkward - clumsy and not graceful
	•	3. embarrassed - feeling ashamed or uncomfortable
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the other ducks say when they saw the gray duckling?
	•	2. What did the chickens call him?
	•	3. How did his own brothers and sisters start treating him?
	•	4. How did the cruel words make the gray duckling feel?
	•	5. What did the gray duckling wonder about himself?

Chapter 6: Running Away
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. mockery - making fun of someone in a mean way
	•	2. criticism - pointing out what's wrong with someone
	•	3. defending - protecting someone from attack
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the farm children do to the gray duckling?
	•	2. How did his own family begin treating him?
	•	3. What difficult decision did the duckling make one evening?
	•	4. When did he decide to leave the farm?
	•	5. What did he hope to find by leaving home?

Chapter 7: The Wild Marsh
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. marsh - wetland with soft, muddy ground
	•	2. cattails - tall plants that grow in marshes
	•	3. scattered - spread out in different directions
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where did the gray duckling go after leaving the farm?
	•	2. Who did he meet in the wild marsh?
	•	3. What scary thing happened one morning?
	•	4. How did the wild ducks react to the hunters?
	•	5. What did the duckling think when the hunting dog ran away from him?

Chapter 8: The Old Woman's Cottage
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. crooked - bent and not straight
	•	2. pity - feeling sorry for someone
	•	3. smugly - in a proud, self-satisfied way
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who lived in the small cottage the duckling found?
	•	2. What did the old woman hope the duckling would do for her?
	•	3. What skills did the hen and cat ask the duckling about?
	•	4. What was the duckling unable to do?
	•	5. What did the hen and cat decide about the duckling?

Chapter 9: A Long, Hard Winter
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. bitter - extremely cold and harsh
	•	2. shivered - shook from being cold
	•	3. confusion - disorder and chaos
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What made finding food difficult during winter?
	•	2. What happened when the duckling tried to keep a pond from freezing?
	•	3. Who rescued the duckling from the ice?
	•	4. What accident happened at the farmer's house?
	•	5. How did the duckling spend the rest of the winter?

Chapter 10: Spring Returns
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. spirits - feelings and mood
	•	2. soared - flew high in the sky
	•	3. magnificent - extremely beautiful and impressive
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did spring affect the duckling's mood?
	•	2. What new ability did the duckling discover he had?
	•	3. What did he see from the air that interested him?
	•	4. What beautiful birds were swimming in the pond?
	•	5. What did the duckling decide to do even though he was afraid?

Chapter 11: The Beautiful Swans
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. glided - moved smoothly and easily
	•	2. welcoming - friendly and accepting
	•	3. reflection - image seen in water or a mirror
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the young bird expect the swans to do to him?
	•	2. How did the swans actually react when he approached them?
	•	3. What did the young bird think was wrong with him?
	•	4. What did the swans tell him about his appearance?
	•	5. What amazing thing did he see when he looked at his reflection?

Chapter 12: Finding His True Self
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. amazement - great surprise and wonder
	•	2. hardships - difficult and painful experiences
	•	3. celebrate - enjoy and be happy about something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the young swan realize about himself?
	•	2. How did the other swans welcome him?
	•	3. What did the children say when they saw him?
	•	4. How did all his difficult experiences help him?
	•	5. What important lesson did he learn about being different?

Discussion Questions for the Complete Story
	•	1. How did being different from others affect the young swan's early life?
	•	2. What kept the young swan going during his most difficult times?
	•	3. How did each person or animal the young swan met teach him something?
	•	4. What does this story teach us about judging others by their appearance?
	•	5. How did the young swan's difficult experiences make him stronger?
	•	6. What does it mean to 'find your true self' like the swan did?
	•	7. How can we be kinder to those who seem different from us?
	•	8. What does this story teach us about growing up and changing?

Writing Prompts
	•	1. Write about a time when you felt different from others and how you handled it.
	•	2. Describe what you think the young swan's life was like after he found the other swans.
	•	3. Write a letter from the swan to his mother duck, explaining what he learned about himself.
	•	4. Create a story about another young animal who discovers something special about themselves.
	•	5. Write about someone you know who turned out to be different and wonderful than others expected.
