The Three Little Kittens
BedrockELA 2nd Grade Chapter Book
Quarter 1, Book 1
6 Chapters with Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions

Chapter 1: The Lost Mittens
Page 1
Three little kittens lived with their mother cat. Their names were Mittsy, Bitsy, and Ditsy. Each kitten had warm red mittens. One cold morning, they wanted to go outside to play in the snow.
Page 2
They put on their mittens and ran outside. They played and played in the snow. They built snowballs and snow cats. When it was time for lunch, something terrible happened.
Page 3
"Oh no!" cried Mittsy. "Where are my mittens?" "Mine are gone too!" said Bitsy. "And mine!" wailed Ditsy. The three little kittens had lost their mittens! They walked slowly back to the house.
Page 4
Mother Cat was waiting with hot soup. But when she saw them without mittens, she was not happy. "You lost your mittens? You naughty kittens! No soup until you find them!"
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. terrible - very bad or awful
2. naughty - not behaving well; mischievous
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions
1. What are the names of the three kittens?
	•	Fluffy, Puffy, and Muffy
	•	Mittsy, Bitsy, and Ditsy
	•	Kitty, Misty, and Whiskers
2. Why was Mother Cat upset with the kittens?
	•	They were too loud
	•	They lost their mittens
	•	They didn't eat their breakfast

Chapter 2: Searching High and Low
Page 1
The three little kittens were very sad. Their mother would not give them any food until they found their lost mittens. Their tummies were starting to growl.
Page 2
"We must find our mittens," said Mittsy. "Let's look everywhere!" The kittens started searching inside the house. First, they looked under their beds. No mittens there.
Page 3
Next, they searched in the toy box. They found balls and blocks, but no mittens. They looked behind the big chair where Father Cat liked to nap. Still no mittens.
Page 4
The kittens looked in every room of the house. They searched high and low. They looked in corners and under rugs. But their mittens were nowhere to be found.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. growl - to make a low, rumbling sound (like a hungry stomach)
2. searching - looking carefully for something
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. Why were the kittens' tummies growling?
	•	They were tired
	•	They were hungry
	•	They were angry
2. Where did the kittens look first for their mittens?
	•	In the toy box
	•	Under their beds
	•	Behind the chair

Chapter 3: Back Outside
Page 1
The three little kittens put on their winter coats and went back outside. The snow was still falling softly. "Let's think," said Mittsy. "Where did we play first?"
Page 2
"We built a snowman by the big tree," remembered Bitsy. The kittens hurried over to the tree. They looked all around the snowman they had made.
Page 3
They dug in the snow with their paws. "Nothing here," sighed Ditsy. Then Mittsy had an idea. "Remember when we were throwing snowballs at the garden gate?"
Page 4
The kittens hurried to the garden gate. They started digging and digging. Suddenly, Bitsy felt something soft and warm in the snow. They had found all their mittens!
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. remembered - brought back to mind; recalled
2. hurried - moved quickly
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. Where did the kittens find their mittens?
	•	By the big tree
	•	At the garden gate
	•	Under the snowman
2. Who found the first mitten?
	•	Mittsy
	•	Bitsy
	•	Ditsy

Chapter 4: Good News for Mother
Page 1
The three little kittens were so excited! They had found their lost mittens. Now Mother Cat would not be angry anymore. And maybe she would give them some food!
Page 2
They ran as fast as they could back to the house. "Mother! Mother!" they called as they burst through the door. Mother Cat looked up from her knitting.
Page 3
The kittens held up their mittens proudly. "Look! We found our mittens!" said Mittsy. "All of them!" added Bitsy. "They were by the garden gate!" explained Ditsy.
Page 4
Mother Cat smiled for the first time that day. "What! You found your mittens? You good little kittens! Now you shall have some pie!" The kittens cheered with joy.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. excited - very happy and eager
2. proudly - with pride; showing you feel good about something
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. How did Mother Cat feel when the kittens found their mittens?
	•	Angry
	•	Happy
	•	Sad
2. What did Mother Cat promise to give the kittens?
	•	Soup
	•	Pie
	•	Milk

Chapter 5: A Messy Mistake
Page 1
The three little kittens hung their wet mittens on hooks by the warm fire. Steam rose from the wool as they began to dry. Then the kittens sat down to eat their pie.
Page 2
It was the best apple pie they had ever tasted! The pie was so good that the kittens ate very quickly. They were so hungry from searching for their mittens all day.
Page 3
As they ate, pie filling dripped on their paws. Apple pieces fell on their fur. Soon the kittens were quite messy. When they put on their mittens, something terrible happened again.
Page 4
Mother Cat looked at them and frowned. "What! You soiled your mittens? You naughty kittens!" The mittens were covered with pie filling and crumbs. "Oh no!" cried the kittens.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. dripped - fell in drops
2. soiled - made dirty
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the kittens' mittens get dirty?
	•	From playing in mud
	•	From eating pie messily
	•	From the snow
2. What was the pie made of?
	•	Cherry
	•	Apple
	•	Blueberry

Chapter 6: Clean Mittens, Happy Hearts
Page 1
Mother Cat shook her head at her messy little kittens. "You must wash your mittens before you can have any milk for dinner," she said. The kittens looked at their dirty mittens.
Page 2
Mother Cat showed them how to fill a basin with warm, soapy water. The kittens took turns washing their mittens. They scrubbed gently to get out all the pie filling.
Page 3
They rinsed them in clean water. "Look how clean they are now!" said Bitsy. The mittens were bright red again. "You washed your mittens? You good little kittens! Now you shall have some milk!"
Page 4
From that day on, the three little kittens never lost their mittens again. And they always ate their pie very, very carefully. They had learned important lessons about taking care of their things.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. basin - a bowl-shaped container for holding water
2. scrubbed - rubbed hard to clean
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions
1. What did the kittens use to wash their mittens?
	•	Cold water
	•	Warm, soapy water
	•	Hot water
2. What lesson did the kittens learn?
	•	Never go outside
	•	Take care of their things
	•	Never eat pie
