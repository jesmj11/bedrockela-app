Chapter 1: Lucie Loses Her Handkerchiefs
Page 1
Once upon a time there was a little girl named Lucie who lived at a farm called Little-town. Lucie was a good little girl, but she had one troublesome habit that worried her mother greatly. She was always losing her pocket-handkerchiefs! No matter how carefully her mother prepared them each morning.
Page 2
One particular morning, Lucie had lost not only her handkerchiefs, but also her little pinafore as well. Her mother had spent considerable time that week making the pinafore especially beautiful, with delicate embroidery and tiny pearl buttons. Now it was nowhere to be found, and Lucie felt quite distressed about the mysterious disappearance.
Page 3
"I must find my pinafore," declared Lucie determinedly. "Mother worked so hard to make it perfect for me, and I simply cannot tell her that I've lost it again!" She decided to search the hillside where she often played, thinking perhaps she had dropped the precious garment during yesterday's adventures.
Page 4
Lucie climbed up the hill behind the farm, calling out hopefully as she searched. "Have you seen my handkerchiefs?" she asked a white hen who was pecking busily in the grass. But the hen just clucked and continued hunting for seeds, paying no attention whatsoever to Lucie's important mission to find her lost belongings.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. troublesome - causing problems or difficulties; bothersome
2. distressed - upset and worried; feeling troubled

	•	Her shoes
	•	Her little pinafore
	•	Her hat

Chapter 2: Following the Mysterious Trail
Page 1
As Lucie continued searching the hillside, she noticed something quite peculiar. There were tiny footprints in the mud - much smaller than her own feet, but definitely footprints nonetheless. The mysterious tracks seemed to lead upward toward the top of the hill, winding around rocks and through patches of heather.
Page 2
"How very curious!" thought Lucie as she examined the unusual footprints more closely. They appeared to be made by someone wearing tiny shoes or boots, but who could be living up here on the lonely hillside? The tracks were fresh, as if someone had passed by only recently, perhaps even that very morning.
Page 3
Driven by overwhelming curiosity and the desperate need to find her missing belongings, Lucie decided to follow the mysterious trail. The footprints led her up a steep, winding path that she had never noticed before, though she had played on this hillside many times throughout her childhood.
Page 4
As she climbed higher and higher, Lucie began to hear the most extraordinary sounds drifting down from above. There was a rhythmic thumping noise, like someone beating something vigorously, accompanied by the splash and bubble of water boiling. Someone was definitely working hard at some mysterious task up ahead of her on the mountain.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. peculiar - strange or unusual; odd
2. overwhelming - very strong or powerful; hard to resist
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. What did Lucie notice in the mud?
	•	Animal tracks
	•	Tiny footprints
	•	Her lost handkerchief
2. What sounds did Lucie hear as she climbed higher?
	•	Birds singing
	•	Rhythmic thumping and water boiling
	•	Wind blowing

Chapter 3: Discovering Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle
Page 1
At the very top of the hill, Lucie discovered a most remarkable sight. There was a small door built right into the hillside, with smoke curling cheerfully from a tiny chimney hidden among the rocks. The mysterious sounds were coming from inside this cozy underground home, and Lucie could hardly believe her eyes.
Page 2
Gathering all her courage, Lucie knocked politely on the little door. "Come in!" called a pleasant voice from within. Lucie pushed open the door and stepped inside to find the most extraordinary kitchen she had ever seen, filled with steaming tubs and lines of clean washing hanging everywhere.
Page 3
Standing in the middle of this busy laundry was the most unusual person Lucie had ever encountered. It was a very small, elderly woman with a brown wrinkled face and bright black eyes. She wore a striped yellow and brown print gown, and a white apron, and her cap was fastened with two curious-looking pins.
Page 4
"Good morning!" said the little woman cheerfully, curtsying politely to her unexpected visitor. "I am Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle, the finest washerwoman in all the countryside. I do hope you've come to collect your laundry, my dear. I've been working so hard to get everything perfectly clean and pressed for you and your friends."
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. remarkable - amazing or surprising; worthy of notice
2. encountered - met or came across unexpectedly
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions

	•	A small door built into the hillside

2. What was Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle's job?

	•	A washerwoman
	•	A gardener

Chapter 4: The Wonderful Laundry
Page 1
Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle led Lucie around her extraordinary laundry, proudly showing off all the beautiful clean clothes hanging on lines throughout the cozy underground room. There were tiny garments of every description, all washed and pressed to absolute perfection, ready to be returned to their grateful owners throughout the countryside.
Page 2
"Here are Peter Rabbit's blue jacket and shoes," explained Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle, pointing to a familiar-looking outfit. "And these belong to Squirrel Nutkin - such a mischievous little fellow, always getting his tail muddy! I've worked very hard to get all these grass stains out of his fur."
Page 3
Lucie gasped with delight and amazement as Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle continued her tour. "And here," said the kind washerwoman with a knowing smile, "are your own dear handkerchiefs, all clean and sweet-smelling!" There indeed were three pocket-handkerchiefs, beautifully ironed and folded, just waiting for Lucie to take them home.
Page 4
"And most importantly," continued Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle triumphantly, "here is your precious little pinafore!" She held up the delicate garment, which looked even more beautiful than when it was new. Every tiny stain had been removed, and the embroidery seemed to sparkle in the warm light of the underground laundry room.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. description - different kinds or types
2. triumphantly - in a proud and victorious way

Chapter 5: Tea Time and Stories
Page 1
Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle invited Lucie to sit down for a cup of tea and some delicious homemade shortbread cookies. As they sat together in the cozy laundry kitchen, Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle told Lucie fascinating stories about all the different woodland creatures whose clothes she washed regularly throughout each busy week.
Page 2
"The animals are always getting into such adventures!" chuckled Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle as she poured steaming tea into delicate china cups. "Poor Tom Kitten split his jacket completely in half last week, and Benjamin Bunny brought me a whole basketful of clothes covered in onion juice from Mr. McGregor's garden!"
Page 3
Lucie listened with growing wonder to these enchanting tales of the secret lives of woodland animals. She had never imagined that the creatures living around her farm had such exciting adventures, or that they wore proper clothes that needed regular washing and mending just like her own garments.
Page 4
As the afternoon grew later, Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle carefully folded Lucie's clean laundry and placed everything in a neat bundle. "You must take these home to your dear mother," she said kindly. "And remember, whenever you lose your handkerchiefs again, you know exactly where to find me for help!" Lucie thanked her warmly for the wonderful hospitality.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. fascinating - extremely interesting and captivating
2. hospitality - friendly and generous treatment of guests

Chapter 6: The Journey Home
Page 1
When it was time to leave, Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle walked with Lucie partway down the hillside, carrying her large basket of clean laundry to deliver to other animal customers throughout the countryside. They chatted pleasantly as they descended the winding path, with Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle sharing more delightful stories about her washing adventures.
Page 2
But as they walked further down the hill, something very strange began to happen to Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle. Her neat brown gown seemed to become rougher and more bristly. Her friendly face appeared to grow more pointed, and her small hands looked increasingly like tiny paws with sharp little claws.
Page 3
Lucie watched in amazement as Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle gradually transformed before her very eyes. The kind washerwoman was becoming smaller and more rounded, and her brown and yellow striped gown had turned into prickly spines! Mrs. Tiggy-Winkle had become a hedgehog - a perfectly ordinary hedgehog - and she scurried away quickly into the bracken.
Page 4
Lucie hurried home with her bundle of clean laundry, wondering if her magical encounter had been real or just a wonderful dream. But when she looked in her bundle, there were her three handkerchiefs and her beautiful pinafore, all perfectly clean and sweet-smelling. Her mother was delighted to see the lost items returned, and Lucie smiled, keeping her magical secret safe in her heart.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. bristly - rough and prickly; having stiff hairs
2. transformed - changed completely from one thing to another
