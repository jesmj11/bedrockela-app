Hansel and Gretel
Chapter Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions
3rd Grade Teacher's Guide

Chapter 1: Hard Times
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. woodcutter - a person who cuts down trees for wood
	•	2. scarce - hard to find; not enough of something
	•	3. desperate - feeling hopeless and willing to try anything
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where did Hansel and Gretel's family live?
	•	2. What was their father's job?
	•	3. Why was their stepmother unkind to the children?
	•	4. What made that year especially difficult for the family?
	•	5. How did the father feel about his children?

Chapter 2: The Terrible Plan
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. solution - an answer to a problem
	•	2. abandon - to leave someone or something behind
	•	3. starve - to become very sick or die from not having food
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What terrible plan did the stepmother suggest?
	•	2. How did the father react to this plan?
	•	3. What choice did the stepmother say the father had to make?
	•	4. How did Hansel and Gretel find out about the plan?
	•	5. How did Hansel try to comfort his sister?

Chapter 3: The Trail of Stones
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. pebbles - small, smooth stones
	•	2. secretly - done in hiding; without others knowing
	•	3. cleverly - done in a smart way
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did Hansel do late at night to prepare for the next day?
	•	2. How did Hansel mark their path through the forest?
	•	3. What excuse did Hansel give when his father asked why he kept stopping?
	•	4. Why didn't Hansel want his father to know about his plan?
	•	5. How did the white pebbles help them?

Chapter 4: Finding the Way Home
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. realized - understood or figured out
	•	2. glowed - gave off a soft light
	•	3. trail - a path or route to follow
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the parents tell the children to do at the fire?
	•	2. When did the children realize their parents weren't coming back?
	•	3. How did Hansel reassure Gretel when she was afraid?
	•	4. What made the pebbles visible in the dark?
	•	5. How long did it take them to follow the trail home?

Chapter 5: The Trail of Breadcrumbs
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. overjoyed - extremely happy
	•	2. pretended - acted like something was true when it wasn't
	•	3. crumbled - broke into small pieces
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the father react when the children returned?
	•	2. Why did the stepmother want to try the plan again?
	•	3. Why couldn't Hansel collect pebbles the second time?
	•	4. What did Hansel use instead of pebbles to mark their path?
	•	5. What excuse did Hansel give for stopping this time?

Chapter 6: Lost in the Forest
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. discovery - finding out something new
	•	2. creatures - animals or other living things
	•	3. huddled - crowded together for warmth or comfort
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How was this trip different from the first time?
	•	2. What terrible discovery did the children make when the moon rose?
	•	3. What had happened to their trail of breadcrumbs?
	•	4. How did Hansel try to stay hopeful despite being lost?
	•	5. What scary sounds did they hear during the night?

Chapter 7: The Gingerbread House
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. wandered - walked around without a specific destination
	•	2. clearing - an open space in a forest where there are no trees
	•	3. gingerbread - a sweet cake made with spices
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How long did Hansel and Gretel wander in the forest?
	•	2. What did they follow that led them to the house?
	•	3. Describe what the amazing house looked like.
	•	4. What parts of the house did the children eat first?
	•	5. What voice did they hear from inside the house?

Chapter 8: The Hungry Witch
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. suspicious - feeling that something might be wrong
	•	2. crooked - bent or twisted; not straight
	•	3. delicious - tasting very good
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Describe what the old woman looked like when she came out.
	•	2. How did the old woman treat the children at first?
	•	3. Why weren't the children suspicious of her kindness?
	•	4. What was the old woman really planning to do?
	•	5. Why had the witch built her house out of gingerbread and candy?

Chapter 9: Trapped
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. cage - a box or room with bars that keeps someone trapped
	•	2. cackled - laughed in a mean, scary way
	•	3. plump - nicely rounded and fat
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the witch do to Hansel the next morning?
	•	2. What did the witch make Gretel do?
	•	3. Why did the witch feed Hansel rich foods every day?
	•	4. How did clever Hansel trick the witch when she checked if he was fat?
	•	5. Why couldn't the witch tell that Hansel was tricking her?

Chapter 10: Gretel's Clever Plan
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. declared - announced something firmly
	•	2. innocently - in a way that seems harmless and not tricky
	•	3. demonstrate - to show how something is done
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Why did the witch decide to cook Hansel even though he wasn't fat yet?
	•	2. What did Gretel pretend not to know how to do?
	•	3. How did the witch fall into Gretel's trap?
	•	4. What did Gretel do to save her brother?
	•	5. How did Gretel free Hansel from his cage?

Chapter 11: The Great Escape
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. treasures - valuable things like gold and jewels
	•	2. victims - people who have been hurt by someone else
	•	3. emeralds - green precious stones
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the children find in the witch's bedroom?
	•	2. Why did they decide to take some of the treasures?
	•	3. What problem did they face when they reached the river?
	•	4. Who helped them cross the river safely?
	•	5. How did the children thank the duck for helping them?

Chapter 12: Home at Last
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. familiar - known; recognized from before
	•	2. comfortable - having everything you need; not worried about money
	•	3. convince - to make someone believe or agree to something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the children know they were getting close to home?
	•	2. How did their father react when he saw them?
	•	3. What had happened to their stepmother?
	•	4. How did the witch's treasure change their lives?
	•	5. What promise did the father make at the end of the story?

Discussion Questions for the Complete Story
	•	1. How did Hansel and Gretel work together to survive their adventures?
	•	2. What character traits helped the children overcome their challenges?
	•	3. How did both Hansel and Gretel show courage in different ways?
	•	4. Why do you think the father finally stood up to his wife at the end?
	•	5. What does this story teach us about being clever and resourceful?
	•	6. How did the children's kindness to the duck get repaid?
	•	7. What lessons can we learn about trusting strangers?
	•	8. How did working together make Hansel and Gretel stronger?

Writing Prompts
	•	1. Write about a time when you and a friend or sibling worked together to solve a problem.
	•	2. If you found a house made of your favorite food, what would it look like? Describe it in detail.
	•	3. Write a letter from Hansel to Gretel, thanking her for saving his life.
	•	4. Create a new adventure for Hansel and Gretel using the witch's treasure to help others.
	•	5. Write about what you think happened to the family in the years after they were reunited.
