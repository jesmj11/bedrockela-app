Chapter 1: An Unexpected Inheritance
Page 1
When the elderly miller who owned a prosperous grain mill in the countryside passed away peacefully after a long and productive life, he left behind three sons who had worked alongside him for many years, learning the various aspects of the milling trade and contributing their individual talents to the family business. According to the terms of his carefully prepared will, each son was to receive a specific inheritance that reflected both his father's wisdom and his understanding of each child's unique capabilities and future needs.
Page 2
The eldest son, who had demonstrated exceptional business acumen and leadership abilities throughout his years of service to the family enterprise, inherited the valuable mill itself along with all of its sophisticated grinding equipment, water wheels, and established customer relationships. The second son, who had shown remarkable skill in managing livestock and understanding animal husbandry, received the mill's sturdy donkey, which was essential for transporting grain and flour to markets throughout the region.
Page 3
However, when it came to the youngest son named Jack, who had always been considered the least practical and most dreamy of the three brothers, his inheritance seemed almost insulting in comparison to the substantial gifts received by his siblings. All that remained for Jack was his father's clever orange tabby cat, a remarkable feline who had lived in the mill for many years, demonstrating unusual intelligence and an almost human-like understanding of complex situations.
Page 4
Jack felt deeply disappointed and somewhat bitter about what he perceived as his father's unfair distribution of the family's assets, wondering how he was supposed to build a successful life with nothing more than a cat as his sole companion and resource. "My brothers have received practical tools for creating wealth and establishing themselves in business," he lamented to himself, "while I have been left with nothing but a pet that cannot even help me earn enough money to buy food or shelter."
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. acumen - the ability to make good judgments and quick decisions
2. lamented - expressed sorrow or regret about something

Chapter 2: The Cat's Remarkable Proposal
Page 1
Much to Jack's astonishment and initial disbelief, the orange tabby cat suddenly began to speak in clear, articulate sentences, demonstrating not only the remarkable ability to communicate in human language, but also revealing a sophisticated understanding of complex social and economic situations that far exceeded anything Jack had expected from his feline inheritance. The cat's voice was confident and persuasive, suggesting years of careful observation and strategic thinking about how to improve their mutual circumstances.
Page 2
"Master Jack," the cat said with dignified formality and unmistakable determination, "I understand your disappointment about your inheritance, but I believe you are greatly underestimating the potential value of our partnership. While your brothers have received tangible assets that will provide them with immediate opportunities, you have been given something far more valuable: a devoted ally with the intelligence, creativity, and resourcefulness necessary to transform your current disadvantages into extraordinary advantages."
Page 3
The remarkable feline continued his persuasive argument with growing enthusiasm and confidence. "If you will provide me with a sturdy pair of leather boots and a cloth sack suitable for carrying various items, I will demonstrate my ability to elevate your social status and economic position through a carefully orchestrated series of strategic actions that will ultimately result in your transformation from a poor miller's son into a wealthy and respected gentleman of significant influence and importance."
Page 4
Although Jack was initially skeptical about his cat's grandiose claims and ambitious promises, he realized that he had very few alternative options available to him given his limited resources and uncertain prospects for the future. "What do I have to lose?" he reasoned pragmatically. "My situation cannot possibly become worse than it already is, and if this remarkable cat truly possesses the abilities he claims to have, then perhaps there is hope for improving my circumstances after all."
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. articulate - expressing ideas clearly and effectively
2. orchestrated - carefully planned and organized
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions

	•	The ability to disappear

	•	A pair of leather boots and a cloth sack

Chapter 3: The Cat's Clever Hunt
Page 1
Once Jack had reluctantly provided his extraordinary cat with the requested leather boots and cloth hunting sack, the clever feline wasted no time in implementing the first phase of his carefully developed master plan. He strapped on the fine boots with obvious satisfaction and pride, adjusting them carefully to ensure maximum comfort and mobility, then slung the spacious sack over his shoulder with the confident air of a seasoned professional hunter preparing for an important expedition.
Page 2
The cat ventured into the nearby countryside where he had observed numerous rabbits living in the wild meadows and forest clearings, areas that he had studied extensively during his years of residence at the mill. Using his superior intelligence and natural feline hunting instincts, he devised an ingenious trap by partially filling his sack with fresh lettuce leaves and aromatic herbs that would prove irresistible to the local rabbit population.
Page 3
With remarkable patience and strategic positioning, the cat concealed himself near his baited trap and waited motionlessly for the perfect opportunity to execute his plan. When a plump rabbit approached the tempting sack and ventured inside to investigate the delicious-smelling contents, the cat immediately sprang into action with lightning-fast reflexes, securing his prize with the skill and precision of a master hunter who had never failed in any of his previous endeavors.
Page 4
Rather than bringing this valuable catch back to Jack as his young master might have expected, the cat had a far more ambitious and sophisticated plan in mind. He carried the rabbit directly to the magnificent royal palace where the king held court, presenting himself at the entrance with the confident bearing of someone who belonged in such elevated social circles and had important business to conduct with the highest authorities in the land.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. ingenious - very clever and original
2. endeavors - serious attempts or efforts

Chapter 4: An Audience with the King
Page 1
When the cat arrived at the royal palace wearing his impressive leather boots and carrying his precious cargo, he requested an immediate audience with the king, claiming to represent a nobleman of great importance who wished to present His Majesty with a special gift as a token of respect and admiration. The palace guards, impressed by the cat's unusual appearance and confident demeanor, as well as curious about this unprecedented situation, decided to grant his request and escort him into the royal presence.
Page 2
"Your Majesty," the cat announced with elaborate ceremony and perfect court etiquette, bowing deeply with theatrical flourish, "I have the distinct honor of representing my master, the Marquis of Carabas, who sends his most respectful greetings and wishes to present you with this fine rabbit as a humble expression of his loyalty and devotion to your royal person. The Marquis hopes that Your Majesty will find this small offering acceptable as a symbol of his desire to serve the crown."
Page 3
The king, who had never encountered such an extraordinary talking cat and was both amused and intrigued by this unprecedented visitor, graciously accepted the gift with royal dignity while expressing his appreciation for the thoughtful gesture from this mysterious nobleman called the Marquis of Carabas. "Please convey my sincere gratitude to your master," the king replied warmly, "and inform him that his generosity and consideration are noted with pleasure by the crown."
Page 4
Over the following weeks and months, the clever cat continued this same pattern of behavior with remarkable consistency and growing success, regularly hunting various types of game including partridges, pheasants, and other prized wild fowl, then presenting each catch to the king as another gift from the supposedly generous and devoted Marquis of Carabas. Each visit strengthened the king's positive impression of this mysterious nobleman and increased his curiosity about meeting this generous benefactor in person.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. etiquette - the rules of polite and proper behavior
2. benefactor - a person who gives help or money to others

	•	He was pleased and grew curious about meeting the Marquis
	•	He ignored the gifts completely

Chapter 5: The Grand Deception
Page 1
When the cat learned through his network of palace connections that the king planned to take a leisurely carriage ride along the river road with his beautiful daughter, he recognized this as the perfect opportunity to implement the most crucial and ambitious phase of his elaborate master plan. He immediately rushed home to Jack with urgent instructions and detailed preparations that would need to be executed with precise timing and absolute cooperation to ensure the success of their life-changing deception.
Page 2
"Master Jack," the cat announced with dramatic urgency, "today you must trust me completely and follow my instructions without question or hesitation. Go immediately to the river near the old stone bridge, remove all of your clothes, and wade into the water as if you were swimming. When the royal carriage approaches, I will handle everything else according to my carefully prepared plan, but your role in this performance is absolutely essential for our ultimate success."
Page 3
Although Jack was bewildered by these strange instructions and concerned about the potential consequences of such unusual behavior, he had come to trust his remarkable cat's judgment and strategic thinking after witnessing the success of his previous schemes. When the royal carriage appeared in the distance, exactly as the cat had predicted, Jack positioned himself in the river while his feline companion prepared to execute the most audacious performance of his entire career.
Page 4
As the king's ornate carriage approached the bridge, the cat began shouting with apparent panic and distress, "Help! Help! My master, the Marquis of Carabas, is drowning! Thieves have stolen all of his fine clothes while he was bathing in the river! Someone must rescue this noble gentleman before it is too late!" The cat's performance was so convincing and dramatic that the king immediately ordered his carriage to stop and commanded his guards to investigate this emergency situation.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. audacious - showing willingness to take bold risks
2. ornate - elaborately decorated
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions

	•	The king was moving to a new palace
2. What emergency did the cat pretend was happening?
	•	The Marquis was lost in the forest
	•	The Marquis was drowning and had his clothes stolen
	•	The Marquis had fallen from his horse

Chapter 6: The Transformation Complete
Page 1
The king immediately responded to the apparent emergency with characteristic royal generosity and compassion, ordering his servants to provide the supposedly noble Marquis of Carabas with the finest spare clothing available in the royal carriage, including an elegant silk shirt, a velvet jacket adorned with golden buttons, and polished leather shoes that transformed Jack's appearance from that of a poor miller's son into that of a sophisticated aristocrat worthy of royal attention and respect.
Page 2
When Jack emerged from the river wearing these magnificent royal garments, his natural good looks and inherent dignity were enhanced to such a degree that he appeared every inch the noble gentleman that the cat had claimed him to be. The king was immediately impressed by the young man's refined bearing and courteous manner, while the princess found herself captivated by his handsome appearance and the mysterious circumstances of their romantic riverside meeting.
Page 3
The cat's elaborate scheme had succeeded beyond his wildest expectations, as the king not only invited Jack to join them in the royal carriage for the remainder of their journey, but also expressed genuine interest in learning more about this intriguing nobleman and his extensive landholdings. The princess and Jack engaged in animated conversation throughout the carriage ride, discovering that they shared many common interests and values that formed the foundation for a rapidly developing mutual attraction.
Page 4
By the end of that remarkable day, Jack had been invited to dine at the royal palace, had received the king's blessing to court the princess, and had established himself as a respected member of the aristocracy through the brilliant machinations of his extraordinary feline companion. The clever cat's loyalty, intelligence, and strategic planning had transformed both of their lives completely, proving that sometimes the most unexpected partnerships can achieve the most spectacular results when guided by wisdom, creativity, and unwavering dedication to mutual success.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. aristocrat - a member of the highest social class
2. machinations - clever schemes or plots
