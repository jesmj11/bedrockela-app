Chapter 1: Christmas Morning
It was Christmas morning. The Boy sat on the nursery floor, surrounded by wrapping paper and ribbons. He had received many wonderful presents.
There were tin soldiers in bright red uniforms. There was a wooden horse with a real mane. A toy boat sat ready to sail in the bathtub. Books with colorful pictures lay open on the rug.
But tucked away in the bottom of his stocking was a small velveteen rabbit. The rabbit was brown and white, with real thread whiskers. His ears were pink satin, soft to touch.
The rabbit was quite handsome. His coat was spotted brown and white. His ears flopped down perfectly. Someone had sewn him with great care.
For a little while, the Boy played with the rabbit. He petted the soft ears and felt the velveteen fur. But soon, the Boy discovered the wind-up mouse that could run across the floor.
The velveteen rabbit was forgotten. He lay on the nursery floor, watching the excitement around him. All the other toys seemed so much more interesting than a simple stuffed rabbit.

Chapter 2: The Nursery
Later that day, Nana picked up the velveteen rabbit. She placed him on the nursery shelf next to the other toys. This would be his new home.
The nursery was a busy place. Model boats sailed on imaginary seas. Toy soldiers marched in perfect lines. A rocking horse galloped without moving.
The mechanical toys were very proud of themselves. They had gears and springs inside. They could move and make noise. 'We are the finest toys,' they said.
'Look at us!' said the toy mouse. 'I can run across the room!' He wound himself up and scurried in circles.
'And I can sail!' said the model boat. 'I have real sails and a working rudder!'
The velveteen rabbit felt very ordinary. He had no gears or springs. He couldn't move by himself. He could only lie there, soft and still.
Some of the toys ignored the rabbit completely. They thought he was too simple. After all, what could a plain velveteen rabbit do?

Chapter 3: The Skin Horse's Wisdom
Only the Skin Horse was kind to the velveteen rabbit. The Skin Horse was the oldest toy in the nursery. He had lived there longer than anyone.
The Skin Horse had brown fur, like the rabbit. But his fur was worn thin from years of loving. His seams showed where he had been hugged tight.
'Don't mind the others,' the Skin Horse told the rabbit gently. 'They think being expensive makes them important. But they don't understand what really matters.'
'What does matter?' asked the velveteen rabbit. He was eager to learn from the wise old horse.
'Being loved,' said the Skin Horse simply. 'When a child loves you for a long, long time, something wonderful happens.'
The rabbit's button eyes grew wide with curiosity. 'What wonderful thing happens?'
The Skin Horse smiled mysteriously. 'You become Real. Not just a toy anymore, but truly Real.'

Chapter 4: What Is Real?
'Real?' asked the velveteen rabbit. 'What does it mean to be Real?'
The Skin Horse thought carefully before answering. This was an important question that deserved a thoughtful reply.
'Real isn't about how you're made,' the Skin Horse explained. 'It doesn't matter if you have fancy parts or can move by yourself.'
'Real is something that happens to you,' he continued. 'When a child really loves you, then you become Real.'
'Does it hurt?' asked the rabbit. He was a little scared. Becoming Real sounded important and maybe dangerous.
'Sometimes,' said the Skin Horse honestly. 'When you're Real, your fur might get worn. Your eyes might come loose. But it doesn't matter. Once you're Real, you can't be ugly.'
The rabbit touched his soft brown fur. He wondered what it would feel like to be loved so much that he became Real.

Chapter 5: The Boy's Favorite
One evening, the Boy couldn't find his usual bedtime toy. Nana looked everywhere for it. She searched under the bed and in the toy box.
'Here,' she said finally, picking up the velveteen rabbit. 'This will have to do for tonight.'
She tucked the rabbit into bed next to the Boy. The Boy wrapped his arms around the soft, warm velveteen fur.
'He's perfect,' whispered the Boy sleepily. The rabbit felt a wonderful warmth spread through his stuffed body.
From that night on, the velveteen rabbit slept in the Boy's bed. He went everywhere with the Boy. To the garden, to tea parties, on walks around the house.
The other toys watched jealously from the nursery shelf. They couldn't understand why the Boy loved a simple velveteen rabbit best of all.
But the rabbit was beginning to understand. This must be what the Skin Horse meant about being loved. And maybe, just maybe, he was becoming Real.

Chapter 6: Adventures Begin
Spring came to the house and garden. The Boy took the velveteen rabbit on wonderful adventures outside. They explored every corner of the yard together.
The rabbit rode in the Boy's pocket during games of hide-and-seek. He sat quietly during tea parties under the big oak tree.
Sometimes the Boy would tell the rabbit stories. 'You're the bravest rabbit in the whole world,' he would say. 'You're my very best friend.'
The velveteen rabbit's fur was becoming worn from all the love and adventures. His brown spots were fading. His pink satin ears were getting shabby.
But the rabbit didn't mind. He remembered what the Skin Horse had said. When you're loved enough to become Real, you can't be ugly.
The Boy never seemed to notice that his rabbit was getting old and worn. To him, the rabbit was more beautiful than ever.
'I love you just the way you are,' the Boy would whisper at bedtime. The rabbit's heart would fill with happiness.

Chapter 7: Spring in the Garden
One beautiful spring day, the Boy and his rabbit were playing in the garden. Flowers were blooming everywhere. The sun was warm and bright.
The Boy made little holes in the dirt for the rabbit to hide in. 'You're a real rabbit,' he said. 'Real rabbits live in burrows.'
When the rabbit heard the word 'real,' his velveteen heart jumped with joy. The Boy thought he was real! Maybe he really was becoming Real.
They played until it was time for dinner. The Boy carried his rabbit back toward the house, chattering happily about their adventures.
'Tomorrow we'll play pirates,' the Boy said. 'You can be my first mate. We'll sail to distant islands and find buried treasure.'
The rabbit loved listening to the Boy's plans. Every day brought new games and new adventures. He felt more Real with each passing day.
That night, the Skin Horse smiled at the velveteen rabbit. 'You're looking more Real every day,' he whispered kindly.

Chapter 8: Meeting the Real Rabbits
One evening, the Boy left his rabbit outside in the garden. He had been called in suddenly for dinner and forgot to bring his favorite toy.
The velveteen rabbit lay quietly in the grass, watching the sunset. The garden was peaceful and still. Or so he thought.
Suddenly, something moved near the raspberry bushes. Two brown creatures hopped into view. They had long ears and fluffy tails.
'Look!' said one of the creatures. 'Another rabbit!' They hopped closer to investigate the velveteen rabbit.
The velveteen rabbit was amazed. These must be real rabbits! They looked just like him, but they could move and hop on their own.
'Why don't you come and play with us?' asked one of the real rabbits. 'We're going to have a lovely game of hop-scotch.'
The velveteen rabbit tried to move, but he couldn't. He had no muscles or joints that would let him hop. He could only lie still in the grass.

Chapter 9: The Sad Discovery
The real rabbits stared at the velveteen rabbit with confusion. 'Why aren't you moving?' they asked. 'Come on! Let's play!'
'I can't move,' the velveteen rabbit said sadly. 'I don't have muscles like you do.'
One of the real rabbits came closer and sniffed the velveteen rabbit. 'You smell like sawdust and cotton,' he said.
'You're not a real rabbit at all!' said the other rabbit. 'You're just a toy! Real rabbits can hop and run and dig burrows.'
The velveteen rabbit felt his heart break. All this time, he had thought he was becoming Real. But these creatures showed him the truth.
'I thought I was Real,' he whispered. 'The Boy loves me so much. Doesn't that make me Real?'
The real rabbits felt sorry for the sad velveteen rabbit. 'Love is wonderful,' they said gently. 'But it doesn't change what you're made of.'
They hopped away into the twilight, leaving the velveteen rabbit alone with his sadness.

Chapter 10: The Fever
Soon after that sad evening, the Boy became very sick with a fever. He lay in bed for many days, holding his velveteen rabbit close.
The rabbit never left the Boy's side. He provided comfort during the long, difficult nights. The Boy whispered to him when he felt scared.
'You're the only one who makes me feel better,' the Boy told his rabbit. 'I love you more than anything in the whole world.'
Slowly, the Boy began to get better. The doctor said he would be well soon. Everyone in the house was very happy.
But then the doctor said something that made the rabbit's heart sink. 'All of the Boy's toys must be burned. They might still carry the fever germs.'
Nana gathered up all the toys from the sick room. She put them in a big sack, including the beloved velveteen rabbit.
'I'm sorry, little rabbit,' she said sadly. 'You were his favorite, but we can't take any chances.'
The sack was carried outside and left by the garden shed, waiting for the bonfire the next morning.

Chapter 11: The Fairy's Gift
That night, the velveteen rabbit lay in the dark sack, thinking about all his happy times with the Boy. A single tear rolled down his fuzzy cheek.
Where the tear fell, something magical happened. A beautiful flower sprouted from the ground, glowing with soft light.
From the flower stepped the most beautiful fairy the rabbit had ever seen. She had gentle eyes and a kind smile.
'I am the Nursery Magic Fairy,' she said in a voice like silver bells. 'I take care of toys who are loved by children.'
'You have been very much loved,' the fairy continued. 'The Boy's love has made you Real. Not just to him, but truly Real.'
The rabbit couldn't believe what he was hearing. 'But the real rabbits said I wasn't real. I can't hop or run.'
'Love makes things Real in a way that nothing else can,' the fairy explained. 'And now I'm going to give you a special gift.'
She leaned down and kissed the velveteen rabbit's worn brown nose. Magic sparkled all around them like stardust.

Chapter 12: Real at Last
The fairy's kiss filled the rabbit with warm, tingling magic. His velveteen fur began to change and grow thick and soft.
His button eyes became bright and shiny. His floppy ears stood up tall. Strong muscles grew in his legs and back.
'Now you are Real to everyone, not just to the Boy who loved you,' said the fairy. 'You can run and hop with the other rabbits.'
The rabbit - now truly real - tried out his new legs. He could hop! He could run! He felt amazing and free and wonderful.
'Will I ever see the Boy again?' he asked the fairy. Even though he was happy, he missed his beloved friend.
'When he is older and can understand,' the fairy promised. 'But now you must go live with the wild rabbits. They're waiting for you.'
The real rabbit hopped away into the forest, his heart full of joy. He had learned the most important lesson of all: love makes everything Real.
And sometimes, on special spring mornings, a little boy playing in the garden might catch a glimpse of a brown rabbit watching him with kind, familiar eyes.

The End
