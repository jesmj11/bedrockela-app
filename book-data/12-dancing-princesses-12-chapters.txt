Chapter 1: The Mysterious Secret
Once upon a time, there was a king who had twelve beautiful daughters. Each princess was lovely in her own way, with flowing hair and bright, sparkling eyes.
The princesses all shared one large bedroom in the palace. Their beds were arranged in a row, each covered with silken sheets and soft pillows.
Every evening, the king would come to say goodnight to his daughters. He would kiss each one on the forehead and lock their bedroom door securely from the outside.
But every morning, when the servants came to wake the princesses, they discovered something very strange. All twelve pairs of the princesses' shoes were worn completely through.
The shoes looked as if they had been danced in all night long. The soles were full of holes, and the silk was tattered and torn from dancing.
'How is this possible?' the servants whispered to each other. 'The door was locked all night. Where could the princesses have gone dancing?'
No one could solve the mystery. How could twelve princesses dance all night when they were locked safely in their room?

Chapter 2: The King's Worry
The king was very troubled by this mystery. Every day, he had to buy new shoes for his daughters because their old ones were ruined from dancing.
But more than the expense, the king worried about his daughters' safety. If they were sneaking out at night, they could be in terrible danger.
'My dear daughters,' he said to them one morning, 'please tell me where you go at night. I only want to keep you safe.'
The princesses looked at each other with innocent expressions. 'Father, we don't go anywhere,' said the eldest princess. 'We sleep in our beds all night long.'
'Then how do your shoes get worn through every single night?' asked the king. 'This has been happening for months!'
The princesses just shrugged and smiled mysteriously. 'We don't know, Father,' they said. 'Perhaps it's magic.'
The king tried everything he could think of. He posted guards outside their door, but the guards always fell into a deep sleep and remembered nothing in the morning.

Chapter 3: The Royal Challenge
Finally, the king decided to make a royal proclamation throughout the kingdom. He would offer a great reward to anyone who could solve the mystery.
'Hear ye, hear ye!' called the royal messenger in every town and village. 'The king offers a magnificent prize to whoever can discover where his daughters go dancing each night!'
The proclamation explained the rules: The person would have three days and three nights to discover the secret. If they succeeded, they could marry one of the princesses and inherit the kingdom.
But there was also a warning: If they failed to solve the mystery after three nights, they would be banished from the kingdom forever.
Many young princes from neighboring kingdoms heard about the challenge. They were eager to marry a beautiful princess and become a king.
'How hard could it be?' they thought. 'We'll just stay awake and watch where the princesses go. Then we'll follow them and solve the mystery easily.'
One by one, brave young men came to the palace to try their luck at solving the puzzle of the dancing princesses.

Chapter 4: Failed Attempts
The first prince to try was young and handsome, with confidence in his abilities. 'I'll solve this mystery in one night,' he boasted.
The king put him in a small room next to the princesses' bedroom. From there, he could watch their door and see if they tried to leave.
At bedtime, the eldest princess brought the prince a cup of wine. 'You must be thirsty after your long journey,' she said sweetly.
The prince drank the wine gratefully, not knowing that it contained a powerful sleeping potion. Within minutes, he was fast asleep.
In the morning, the prince woke up to find all twelve pairs of shoes worn through with dancing. He had slept through the entire night and learned nothing.
The same thing happened the second night, and the third. The prince was sent away in shame, having failed completely.
Many other young men tried after him, but they all met the same fate. Each one drank the drugged wine and slept through the night, learning nothing about the princesses' secret.

Chapter 5: The Soldier's Arrival
After many failed attempts by princes and noblemen, an old soldier heard about the challenge. He had served in the king's army for many years but had been wounded in battle.
The soldier was not rich or handsome like the princes who had tried before him. But he was clever and observant from his years of military service.
'I have nothing to lose,' thought the soldier. 'If I fail, I'll be banished, but I'm already poor and homeless. If I succeed, I'll have a wonderful new life.'
As the soldier walked toward the palace, he met an old woman on the road. She looked tired and hungry, so he shared his small piece of bread with her.
'You are very kind,' said the old woman gratefully. 'Where are you going on this fine day?'
The soldier told her about the challenge and his plan to discover the princesses' secret. The old woman listened carefully, nodding her head.
'I think I can help you,' said the old woman with a mysterious smile. 'But you must listen to my advice very carefully.'

Chapter 6: The Old Woman's Gift
The old woman reached into her worn cloak and pulled out a beautiful silver cloak. It shimmered in the sunlight like moonbeams on water.
'This is a magic cloak,' she explained. 'When you wear it, you will become invisible to everyone around you.'
The soldier's eyes grew wide with wonder. 'Invisible? But how is that possible?'
'Magic is possible when you have a kind heart,' said the old woman. 'You shared your food with a stranger, so I will help you succeed.'
'But remember,' she continued, 'you must not drink anything the princesses offer you. They will try to give you wine that will make you sleep.'
'How do you know this?' asked the soldier.
The old woman smiled mysteriously. 'I know many things. Just promise me you'll be careful and follow my advice.'
The soldier promised, and the old woman disappeared as suddenly as she had appeared, leaving only the magic cloak behind.

Chapter 7: The Magic Cloak
When the soldier arrived at the palace, the king received him kindly but with little hope. So many had tried and failed before.
'I will do my best, Your Majesty,' said the soldier humbly. 'I only ask for a fair chance to solve this mystery.'
That evening, the soldier was shown to the small room next to the princesses' bedroom. He could hear them laughing and talking as they prepared for bed.
Just as the old woman had predicted, the eldest princess soon appeared with a cup of wine. 'You must be tired,' she said sweetly. 'This will help you sleep well.'
The soldier pretended to drink the wine but secretly poured it out when the princess wasn't looking. Then he lay down on his bed and pretended to snore loudly.
Soon, he heard the princesses whispering excitedly. 'He's asleep,' said one. 'Just like all the others!'
Quietly, the soldier put on the magic cloak. Immediately, he became completely invisible, just as the old woman had promised.

Chapter 8: Following the Secret
The invisible soldier watched in amazement as the eldest princess walked to her bed and pressed a hidden button. The bed moved aside, revealing a secret staircase!
One by one, the twelve princesses put on their most beautiful gowns and dancing shoes. Then they descended the hidden staircase, giggling with excitement.
The soldier quickly followed them down the stairs. The staircase was long and winding, leading deep underground beneath the palace.
As they walked, the youngest princess kept looking over her shoulder nervously. 'I feel like someone is following us,' she whispered to her sisters.
'Don't be silly,' said the eldest princess. 'The soldier is fast asleep, just like all the others. You're just imagining things.'
But the youngest princess was right to be nervous. The invisible soldier was indeed following close behind them, being very careful not to make any noise.
The staircase led to a beautiful underground passage lit by glowing crystals. The walls sparkled like diamonds, and sweet music drifted through the air.

Chapter 9: The Underground Kingdom
The passage opened into the most magnificent underground kingdom the soldier had ever seen. Everything glittered with magic and beauty.
First, they walked through a forest where all the trees had leaves of pure silver. The leaves chimed like bells when the wind touched them.
Next, they entered a forest where all the trees had leaves of shining gold. The golden leaves caught the magical light and made everything glow.
Finally, they came to a forest where the trees had leaves made of sparkling diamonds. The light reflected off the diamond leaves like thousands of tiny stars.
The soldier quietly broke a small branch from each forest to keep as proof of what he had seen. Each time, the youngest princess heard the snap and looked around.
'Did you hear that?' she asked her sisters. 'Something is breaking branches behind us!'
'It's just the wind,' said the other princesses. 'Stop worrying and enjoy our wonderful adventure!'

Chapter 10: The Dancing Palace
Beyond the diamond forest lay a beautiful lake with crystal-clear water that sparkled like liquid starlight.
Twelve handsome princes waited by the lake in twelve golden boats. Each prince was under a magic spell that compelled him to take the princesses dancing every night.
The soldier watched as each princess stepped into a boat with a prince. There was no room for him, so he quietly climbed onto the back of the youngest princess's boat.
'This boat feels heavier than usual,' said the prince as he rowed across the lake. 'I can barely make it move tonight.'
'Perhaps you're just tired,' said the youngest princess, still feeling nervous about something she couldn't explain.
On the far shore of the lake stood a magnificent palace made entirely of crystal. Music floated from its windows, and lights twinkled in every room.
Inside the palace, the soldier watched in wonder as the twelve princesses danced with the twelve princes. They danced waltzes and polkas, spinning and laughing with joy until dawn approached.

Chapter 11: Gathering Evidence
As the first light of dawn appeared, the princesses knew it was time to return home. They said goodbye to the princes and hurried back to their boats.
The soldier again climbed onto the youngest princess's boat for the return journey across the magical lake.
Before leaving the crystal palace, the soldier took a golden cup from one of the tables as proof of where he had been.
The princesses traveled back through the diamond forest, the golden forest, and the silver forest. They climbed the long staircase to their bedroom.
Once in their room, they quickly changed out of their dancing gowns and into their nightgowns. They hid their worn-out shoes and pretended to be asleep.
The soldier removed his magic cloak and returned to his room. He hid the three branches and the golden cup carefully under his bed.
For two more nights, the soldier followed the princesses and watched them dance. Each time, he gathered more evidence of their secret activities.

Chapter 12: The Truth Revealed
On the morning after the third night, the king summoned the soldier to the throne room. All the princesses were there, looking confident that their secret was still safe.
'Well, good soldier,' said the king sadly, 'I'm afraid you have failed like all the others. You must tell me where my daughters go dancing, or be banished from my kingdom.'
The soldier smiled and bowed respectfully. 'Your Majesty, I have indeed discovered where the princesses go every night.'
The princesses gasped in surprise. How could he possibly know their secret?
'They go to an underground kingdom,' the soldier explained, 'where they dance with twelve enchanted princes in a crystal palace.'
Then he produced the silver branch, the golden branch, the diamond branch, and the golden cup as proof of his words.
The princesses could no longer deny the truth. They confessed everything to their father and begged for his forgiveness.
The king was amazed by the soldier's cleverness and bravery. 'You have earned the right to marry one of my daughters and inherit my kingdom,' he said.
The soldier chose the eldest princess, who had learned to respect his wisdom. They were married with great celebration, and the soldier broke the enchantment that bound the twelve princes.
From that day forward, the princesses were free to dance whenever they wished, but they promised their father to always tell him where they were going. The kingdom lived in happiness and honesty ever after.

The End
