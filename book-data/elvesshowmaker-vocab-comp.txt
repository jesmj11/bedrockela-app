The Elves and the Shoemaker
Chapter Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions
3rd Grade Teacher's Guide

Chapter 1: Hard Times
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. skilled - very good at doing something
	•	2. trade - a job that requires special training
	•	3. savings - money that has been saved for later
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What was the shoemaker's job?
	•	2. How did the shoemaker and his wife treat their customers?
	•	3. What kind of shoes could the shoemaker make?
	•	4. Why weren't people buying shoes anymore?
	•	5. What were the shoemaker and his wife worried about?

Chapter 2: The Last Piece of Leather
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. remaining - what is left over
	•	2. merchant - a person who sells goods
	•	3. examined - looked at carefully
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How much money did the shoemaker have left?
	•	2. What did he decide to buy with his last coins?
	•	3. What kind of leather did he choose?
	•	4. How did the shoemaker spend his day?
	•	5. Why didn't he finish the shoes that evening?

Chapter 3: A Christmas Miracle
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. neatly - in an organized, tidy way
	•	2. miracle - something wonderful that seems impossible
	•	3. flawless - perfect; without any mistakes
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the shoemaker prepare his work area before going to bed?
	•	2. What did he pray for that night?
	•	3. What amazing thing did the shoemaker find in the morning?
	•	4. How did his wife react when she saw the shoes?
	•	5. What made the shoes so special?

Chapter 4: Perfect Shoes
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. workmanship - the skill shown in making something
	•	2. wealthy - having a lot of money
	•	3. craftsmanship - skill in making things by hand
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the shoemaker notice about the stitching on the shoes?
	•	2. Who came into the shop that morning?
	•	3. How did the gentleman react when he saw the shoes?
	•	4. Why did the shoes fit the gentleman perfectly?
	•	5. How much money did the gentleman pay for the shoes?

Chapter 5: More Leather, More Hope
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. fortune - luck, especially good luck
	•	2. prosperity - success and wealth
	•	3. delicate - needing careful handling; fine
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What were the shoemaker and his wife able to do with their money?
	•	2. How much leather could they now afford to buy?
	•	3. What types of shoes did the shoemaker plan to make?
	•	4. How did the shoemaker feel about their changing luck?
	•	5. What did the couple hope would happen again?

Chapter 6: The Mystery Continues
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. exquisite - extremely beautiful
	•	2. construction - the way something is built
	•	3. puzzle - think hard about something confusing
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the shoemaker find on his workbench the next morning?
	•	2. How were the lady's shoes different from the gentleman's shoes?
	•	3. What happened before the couple could think about the mystery?
	•	4. How had word about their shoes spread?
	•	5. What did customers say about the quality of the shoes?

Chapter 7: A Growing Business
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. transformed - completely changed
	•	2. neighboring - nearby
	•	3. exchanged - shared with each other
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How much leather could the shoemaker afford to buy now?
	•	2. What happened every night in the shop?
	•	3. Who began coming to buy shoes from the shop?
	•	4. What did one customer say about the shoes?
	•	5. How had their situation changed in just a few weeks?

Chapter 8: The Wife's Curiosity
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. curiosity - wanting to know about something
	•	2. gratitude - feeling thankful
	•	3. mysterious - hard to explain or understand
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What was the wife thinking about by the fire?
	•	2. What did she think they should do?
	•	3. How did her husband respond to her idea?
	•	4. What plan did the wife suggest for discovering their helper?
	•	5. How did they prepare for their secret watch?

Chapter 9: Hiding and Watching
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. crept - moved very quietly
	•	2. cupboard - a piece of furniture for storing things
	•	3. threading - putting thread through a needle
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Where did the couple hide to watch?
	•	2. What time did they start hearing sounds?
	•	3. What strange thing happened to the shop door?
	•	4. What did they see happening to the leather pieces?
	•	5. How did the shoemaker and his wife feel watching this magic?

Chapter 10: The Secret Helpers
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. nimble - quick and skillful with hands or feet
	•	2. tattered - torn and worn out
	•	3. barefoot - not wearing shoes or socks
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who were the mysterious helpers?
	•	2. What did the shoemaker and his wife notice about the elves' clothes?
	•	3. How did the elves feel about their work?
	•	4. How long did it take the elves to complete all the shoes?
	•	5. How did the elves disappear when they were finished?

Chapter 11: A Gift of Thanks
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. creatures - living beings
	•	2. ragged - torn and worn out
	•	3. materials - things used to make something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the couple feel about the elves after seeing them?
	•	2. What made their hearts ache when they saw the elves?
	•	3. What idea did the wife have for Christmas?
	•	4. What did the shoemaker decide to make for the elves?
	•	5. What special materials did they buy for their gifts?

Chapter 12: Christmas Kindness
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. outfits - complete sets of clothing
	•	2. miniature - very small versions of something
	•	3. cobbling - making or repairing shoes
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the wife make for the elves?
	•	2. What did the shoemaker create for the elves?
	•	3. What did they leave instead of leather pieces on Christmas Eve?
	•	4. How did the elves react when they found their gifts?
	•	5. What important lesson did the shoemaker and his wife learn?

Discussion Questions for the Complete Story
	•	1. What character traits did the shoemaker and his wife show throughout the story?
	•	2. Why do you think the elves chose to help this particular shoemaker?
	•	3. How did the couple show gratitude to their mysterious helpers?
	•	4. What does this story teach us about helping others?
	•	5. Why was it important that the couple gave gifts to the elves?
	•	6. How did both the shoemaker's family and the elves benefit from kindness?
	•	7. What does the story teach us about working hard and being honest?
	•	8. How did the experience change the way the shoemaker and his wife treated others?

Writing Prompts
	•	1. Write about a time when someone helped you when you really needed it. How did it make you feel?
	•	2. If you could help someone secretly like the elves did, who would you help and how?
	•	3. Describe what you think happened to the elves after they received their gifts.
	•	4. Write a thank-you letter from the elves to the shoemaker and his wife.
	•	5. Create a story about another person who receives mysterious help when they need it most.
