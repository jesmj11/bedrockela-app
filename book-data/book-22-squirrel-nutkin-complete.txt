Chapter 1: Journey to Owl Island
Page 1
In a peaceful woodland valley beside a pristine lake that reflected the surrounding forest like a perfect mirror, there lived a large community of industrious squirrels who spent their days gathering acorns, hazelnuts, and other precious treasures to store away for the long winter months ahead. Among these hardworking creatures were many cousins and relatives who cooperated harmoniously in their seasonal preparations, understanding that their survival depended upon careful planning and respectful behavior toward all the other woodland inhabitants.
Page 2
However, there was one particular squirrel named Nutkin who stood out from all the others not because of his diligent work habits or considerate nature, but because of his mischievous personality and his unfortunate tendency to show off at inappropriate times. While his relatives conducted themselves with dignity and proper woodland etiquette, Nutkin delighted in making silly jokes, performing ridiculous stunts, and generally behaving in ways that drew attention to himself rather than contributing meaningfully to their collective efforts.
Page 3
Every autumn, according to ancient woodland tradition that had been observed for countless generations, the squirrel families would make a respectful pilgrimage across the tranquil lake to a mysterious island where the great brown owl named Old Brown had made his magnificent home in the hollow of an enormous oak tree. This journey was not undertaken lightly, for the squirrels needed to ask Old Brown's permission to gather nuts from his island, and everyone understood that proper protocol and courteous behavior were absolutely essential for success.
Page 4
On this particular morning, as the squirrel families prepared their makeshift rafts constructed from pieces of bark and twigs, everyone understood the importance of showing appropriate respect to Old Brown, who had the power to grant or deny their crucial request for nut-gathering privileges. The wisest elders reminded the younger squirrels about the proper etiquette expected during their audience with this formidable ruler of Owl Island, emphasizing that politeness and humility would determine the success of their entire winter preparation efforts.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. pristine - perfectly clean and fresh; in original condition
2. pilgrimage - a journey to a sacred or important place

Chapter 2: Meeting the Mighty Old Brown
Page 1
When the squirrel families reached Owl Island after their careful journey across the lake, they approached Old Brown's magnificent oak tree home with the reverent silence and respectful demeanor that the occasion demanded. The great brown owl sat majestically in the doorway of his hollow, his enormous golden eyes surveying the visiting squirrels with an expression of dignified authority that commanded immediate respect from all creatures who understood the natural hierarchy of the woodland community.
Page 2
All of Nutkin's relatives behaved with exemplary courtesy and proper protocol, offering Old Brown beautiful gifts as tokens of their respect and appreciation for his generous permission to gather nuts on his island territory. They brought carefully selected offerings including fresh fish caught from the pristine lake waters, smooth pebbles polished by years of gentle wave action, and delicate wildflowers arranged with artistic care to please the aesthetic sensibilities of their distinguished host.
Page 3
Old Brown acknowledged these thoughtful gifts with gracious dignity, nodding his magnificent head in acceptance while maintaining the serious composure befitting a woodland ruler of his status and responsibility. The ceremony proceeded smoothly and harmoniously as each squirrel family presented their offerings with humble gratitude, understanding that this ancient ritual ensured peaceful coexistence and mutual benefit for all the creatures sharing their forest ecosystem.
Page 4
However, Nutkin had no patience for such formal ceremonies and traditional protocols, viewing them as tedious obstacles to his personal amusement rather than important expressions of respect and community cooperation. While his relatives conducted themselves with appropriate solemnity, Nutkin began dancing about irreverently, making faces, telling silly riddles, and generally behaving in ways that demonstrated complete disregard for the significance of the occasion and the dignity of their esteemed host, Old Brown.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. reverent - showing deep respect and admiration
2. irreverently - showing a lack of respect for things that should be respected

Chapter 3: Nutkin's Ridiculous Riddles
Page 1
As the formal ceremony continued and Old Brown graciously granted the squirrel families permission to gather nuts throughout his island territory, Nutkin's behavior became increasingly inappropriate and disruptive to the solemn proceedings. Instead of joining his relatives in expressions of gratitude and respectful acknowledgment of Old Brown's generosity, Nutkin began reciting a series of nonsensical riddles designed more to showcase his own cleverness than to contribute meaningfully to the important diplomatic negotiations taking place.
Page 2
"Old Mr. Brown!" Nutkin called out impudently, completely ignoring the established protocols for addressing such a distinguished woodland authority. "I have a riddle for you! Riddle me, riddle me, rot-tot-tote! A little wee man in a red red coat! A staff in his hand and a stone in his throat; if you tell me this riddle, I'll give you a groat!" His voice rang out with inappropriate cheerfulness that contrasted sharply with the dignified atmosphere his elders had carefully cultivated.
Page 3
Old Brown received this impertinent display with stoic silence, his golden eyes remaining fixed upon Nutkin with an expression that grew increasingly stern and disapproving. The great owl understood that this young squirrel was testing the boundaries of acceptable behavior, challenging his authority through deliberate disrespect disguised as playful entertainment. However, Old Brown chose to maintain his dignified composure rather than respond to such obvious provocation with the swift punishment that Nutkin's behavior clearly deserved.
Page 4
Nutkin's relatives watched this inappropriate performance with growing embarrassment and concern, recognizing that their young cousin was placing their entire community at risk through his thoughtless disregard for proper woodland etiquette. They attempted to signal him discretely to moderate his behavior, but Nutkin was too intoxicated by his own perceived cleverness to notice their worried glances and subtle warnings about the dangerous path he was pursuing with such reckless abandon and complete lack of wisdom.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. impudently - in a rude way that shows no respect
2. impertinent - rude and not showing proper respect
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions

	•	He quietly sat in the corner
2. How did Old Brown respond to Nutkin's impertinent riddle?
	•	He laughed and joined in the fun
	•	He maintained stoic silence with increasingly stern disapproval
	•	He immediately chased Nutkin away

Chapter 4: Escalating Mischief
Page 1
Day after day, as the squirrel families industriously gathered nuts throughout Old Brown's island territory with grateful appreciation for his generous permission, Nutkin's disrespectful behavior escalated to new heights of inappropriate audacity. Each morning brought fresh displays of impudence as he invented increasingly elaborate riddles, performed more outrageous stunts, and generally conducted himself with complete disregard for the consequences that his actions might eventually bring upon himself and his worried relatives.
Page 2
On the second day of their visit, Nutkin danced mockingly around Old Brown's oak tree while reciting another ridiculous riddle: "Arthur O'Bower has broken his band, he comes roaring up the land! The King of Scots with all his power, cannot turn Arthur of the Bower!" His voice carried across the island with boisterous confidence, apparently oblivious to Old Brown's growing irritation and the increasingly ominous silence that followed each of his presumptuous performances.
Page 3
By the third day, Nutkin had become so intoxicated with his own supposed wit and cleverness that he began engaging in even more provocative behavior, including making rude faces directly at Old Brown, performing acrobatic somersaults that deliberately drew attention away from the serious work his relatives were conducting, and generally treating the entire situation as his personal entertainment venue rather than recognizing it as a sacred trust that required respectful behavior and humble gratitude.
Page 4
Old Brown's patience, though considerable due to his wisdom and experience with woodland creatures of all temperaments, was beginning to reach its natural limits as Nutkin's behavior became increasingly intolerable and disruptive to the peaceful atmosphere that should have characterized this important seasonal gathering. The great owl's golden eyes began to flash with unmistakable warning signals that any sensible creature would have recognized as clear indicators that serious consequences were rapidly approaching for anyone foolish enough to continue testing his authority and tolerance.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. audacity - willingness to take bold risks; rude boldness
2. presumptuous - too bold; overstepping proper boundaries
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

	•	He became more respectful and helpful

	•	He stopped talking completely
2. What warning signs did Old Brown begin to show?
	•	He started singing and dancing
	•	His golden eyes began to flash with unmistakable warning signals

Chapter 5: The Final Insult
Page 1
On what would prove to be the final day of their visit to Owl Island, Nutkin's reckless behavior reached an absolutely unacceptable climax that would forever change the course of his life and serve as a permanent reminder of the serious consequences that inevitably follow when someone persistently ignores all warnings and boundaries. His relatives had completed their grateful nut-gathering activities and were preparing to depart with respectful farewells to their generous host, but Nutkin had one more outrageous performance planned for his unwilling audience.
Page 2
With supreme arrogance and complete disregard for every principle of proper woodland etiquette that his elders had attempted to teach him, Nutkin approached Old Brown's oak tree and began the most insulting riddle of his entire repertoire: "Humpty Dumpty lies in the beck, with a white counterpane round his neck, forty doctors and forty wrights, cannot put Humpty Dumpty to rights!" But even this inappropriate verse was not sufficient to satisfy his craving for attention and dramatic effect.
Page 3
In an act of unprecedented disrespect that crossed every line of acceptable behavior, Nutkin picked up a large nut and threw it directly at Old Brown's magnificent head, striking the great owl with deliberate malicious intent that represented the ultimate violation of woodland law and social order. This final act of defiance was not merely childish mischief or playful teasing, but a serious assault upon the dignity and authority of one of the forest's most respected leaders.
Page 4
The effect of this outrageous action was immediate and terrifying as Old Brown's patience finally reached its absolute breaking point after days of mounting provocation and deliberate insult. The great owl's eyes blazed with righteous fury as he rose from his hollow with majestic wrath, his powerful wings spreading wide to display the full extent of his formidable size and strength. Every creature on the island froze with terror as they witnessed the unleashing of Old Brown's long-suppressed anger at this intolerable breach of respect and decency.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. unprecedented - never done or known before; without previous example
2. formidable - inspiring fear or respect through being impressively powerful

	•	He told one more riddle

	•	He laughed and forgave him

Chapter 6: Swift Justice and Lasting Consequences
Page 1
With lightning-fast reflexes that demonstrated why Old Brown commanded such respect and authority throughout the woodland community, the great owl swooped down upon Nutkin with talons extended and wings spread wide in a display of natural justice that had been postponed far too long. Nutkin's shocked realization that his behavior had finally triggered serious consequences came far too late to prevent the swift retribution that his persistent disrespect and arrogance had inevitably earned through his own foolish choices and stubborn refusal to heed obvious warnings.
Page 2
In the terrifying struggle that followed, Nutkin managed to escape Old Brown's powerful grasp only by leaving behind his beautiful tail, which remained forever in the great owl's possession as a permanent trophy and reminder of what happens to creatures who persistently challenge authority through inappropriate behavior and disrespectful conduct. The physical pain of losing his magnificent tail was severe, but the deeper lesson about the consequences of arrogance and the importance of showing proper respect would prove to be far more significant and lasting.
Page 3
Nutkin managed to escape back across the lake to safety, but he was forever changed by this traumatic experience that had taught him profound lessons about respect, authority, and the serious consequences that follow when someone refuses to recognize appropriate boundaries and social expectations. His relatives, who had watched this entire drama unfold with horror and embarrassment, understood that Nutkin's punishment, while severe, was the natural result of his own choices and his persistent refusal to accept guidance from those who cared about his welfare.
Page 4
From that day forward, Nutkin never again spoke in riddles or engaged in the kind of disrespectful behavior that had cost him so dearly, for he had learned through painful personal experience that showing off and challenging authority might seem amusing at first, but ultimately leads to serious consequences that cannot be undone. His missing tail served as a permanent visible reminder to himself and to all other woodland creatures about the importance of humility, respect, and understanding that there are times when wisdom means knowing when to remain quiet and show proper appreciation for the generosity and patience of others.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. retribution - punishment for doing something wrong
2. traumatic - emotionally disturbing and lasting
