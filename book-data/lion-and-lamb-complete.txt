Chapter 1: The King of Beasts
Page 1
In the heart of a great wilderness where tall grasses swayed in the afternoon breeze and acacia trees provided scattered shade, there ruled a magnificent lion whose golden mane caught the sunlight like a crown. All the animals of the savanna knew of his strength and power, from the smallest field mouse to the largest elephant. This lion was proud of his position as king of all the beasts, and he ruled his domain with fierce authority that none dared to question.
Page 2
The lion spent his days patrolling his territory, making sure that every creature understood his dominance and respected his commands. He would roar loudly each morning to announce his presence, and the sound would echo across the plains, reminding all who heard it that he was the undisputed ruler of this wild kingdom. His powerful muscles rippled beneath his tawny coat, and his sharp claws and teeth showed clearly that he was built for hunting and fighting.
Page 3
Other animals would scatter when they heard the lion approaching, hiding behind rocks or in tall grass until the danger passed. The zebras would stop their grazing and bound away on swift legs. The antelope would freeze with fear before leaping to safety. Even the proud elephants would move aside respectfully when the lion prowled through their gathering places, understanding that it was wiser to avoid conflict with such a formidable predator.
Page 4
The lion took great pride in the fear he inspired and the respect he commanded from all the other animals. He believed that his strength made him superior to every other creature, and that his position as the most powerful hunter gave him the right to rule over all the gentler animals who could not defend themselves against his mighty roar and sharp claws. This belief filled him with arrogance and made him feel that he was the most important animal in all the world.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. dominance - power and control over others
2. formidable - inspiring fear through being powerful
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the lion show his authority?
	•	By hiding from other animals
	•	By roaring loudly each morning and patrolling his territory
	•	By running away from danger
2. How did other animals react to the lion?
	•	They challenged him to fights
	•	They scattered and hid, showing fear and respect
	•	They ignored him completely

Chapter 2: The Gentle Lamb
Page 1
Far from the lion's territory, in a peaceful meadow filled with wildflowers and soft green grass, lived a small white lamb who spent her days in quiet contentment. Unlike the fierce lion, the lamb possessed no sharp claws or powerful muscles. She had no mighty roar to frighten others, and her gentle bleating was barely loud enough to call to her friends. But what the lamb lacked in physical strength, she made up for in kindness and wisdom.
Page 2
The lamb spent her time helping other animals in the meadow solve their problems through patience and understanding. When two rabbits argued over the best clover patch, she would suggest they take turns sharing it. When a lost baby bird cried for its mother, the lamb would comfort it with gentle words until the parent returned. Her soft wool provided warmth for smaller creatures on cold nights, and her calm presence brought peace to any situation.
Page 3
All the animals in the meadow loved the lamb for her generous heart and wise advice. The field mice would bring their troubles to her, knowing she would listen carefully and offer helpful solutions. The butterflies would rest on her back while she grazed, trusting in her gentleness. Even the bees, who were usually too busy for conversation, would pause to thank her when she moved carefully around their favorite flowers.
Page 4
The lamb never boasted about her good deeds or demanded recognition for her kindness. She simply lived each day trying to make life better for those around her, believing that true strength came not from the ability to frighten others, but from the power to comfort, heal, and bring peace. Her influence spread throughout the meadow like ripples in a calm pond, creating harmony wherever she went.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. contentment - a state of peaceful happiness
2. harmony - peaceful agreement and cooperation
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the lamb help other animals?
	•	By fighting their enemies
	•	Through patience, understanding, and wise advice
	•	By giving them food
2. What did the lamb believe about true strength?
	•	That it comes from frightening others
	•	That it comes from the power to comfort, heal, and bring peace
	•	That only large animals can be strong

Chapter 3: An Unexpected Meeting
Page 1
One day, as fate would have it, the lamb wandered farther from her meadow than she had ever gone before, following a beautiful butterfly that seemed to dance just beyond her reach. She traveled through forests and across streams, so enchanted by the colorful wings that she didn't notice how far she had walked. When the butterfly finally disappeared among some tall trees, the lamb found herself in completely unfamiliar territory.
Page 2
As the lamb looked around nervously, trying to figure out which way would lead her home, she heard a sound that made her freeze with terror. The mighty roar of the lion echoed across the savanna, so close that it seemed to shake the very ground beneath her small hooves. She had heard stories about the fearsome king of beasts, but she had never imagined she would actually encounter him face to face.
Page 3
The lion appeared through the tall grass like a golden storm, his massive form moving with deadly grace. His eyes blazed with fierce pride, and his mane rippled in the wind like flames. When he saw the small, defenseless lamb standing alone in his domain, he felt a surge of his usual arrogance. Here was the perfect opportunity to demonstrate his power over one of the weakest creatures in all the animal kingdom.
Page 4
"What are you doing in my territory, little lamb?" the lion growled, his voice deep and menacing. "Don't you know that I am the king of all beasts? I could crush you with one paw or tear you apart with my teeth. You should be trembling with fear at the very sight of me!" He paced around the lamb in a circle, expecting her to cower and beg for mercy as so many other animals had done before.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. enchanted - filled with delight as if under a magic spell
2. menacing - threatening or suggesting danger
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the lamb end up in the lion's territory?
	•	She was hunting for food
	•	She followed a butterfly and got lost
	•	She was looking for the lion
2. What did the lion expect from the lamb?
	•	That she would challenge him to a fight
	•	That she would cower and beg for mercy
	•	That she would ignore him completely

Chapter 4: The Lamb's Response
Page 1
To the lion's great surprise, the lamb did not tremble or run away in fear. Instead, she looked up at him with calm, gentle eyes and spoke in a voice as soft as morning mist. "I am sorry for entering your territory without permission, great lion. I became lost while following a butterfly and did not mean to disturb you. I know you are very powerful and strong, and I respect your position as king of the beasts."
Page 2
The lion was confused by this unexpected response. He was accustomed to animals either fleeing in terror or challenging him with defiant roars of their own, but the lamb's quiet courtesy was something entirely new in his experience. She showed no fear, but also no disrespect. Her words acknowledged his power while maintaining her own dignity, creating a situation the lion had never encountered before.
Page 3
"But tell me," the lamb continued gently, "what good does it do you to have everyone afraid of you? When you roar, all the animals run away, leaving you alone. When you approach, even the mighty elephants move aside, but they do not stay to be your friends. Does ruling through fear bring you happiness, or does it make you feel lonely?"
Page 4
These words struck the lion like a lightning bolt, because they revealed a truth he had never wanted to admit. Despite all his power and the fear he inspired, he was indeed very lonely. No animal had ever chosen to spend time with him simply for the pleasure of his company. He ruled through fear, but he had no real friends, no companions to share his thoughts with, no one who truly cared about his well-being.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. courtesy - polite and respectful behavior
2. defiant - boldly refusing to obey or respect
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the lamb respond to the lion's threats?
	•	With terror and begging
	•	With calm courtesy and gentle questions
	•	By running away quickly
2. What truth did the lamb's question reveal about the lion?
	•	That he was the strongest animal
	•	That despite his power, he was very lonely
	•	That he needed to roar louder

Chapter 5: A Change of Heart
Page 1
The lion sat down heavily, his proud head drooping as he considered the lamb's wise words. For the first time in his life, he began to think about what his reign of fear had truly accomplished. Yes, he was respected and obeyed, but was he truly happy? The honest answer was no. His power had isolated him from all the other animals, leaving him as alone as if he were the only creature in the entire savanna.
Page 2
"You speak the truth, little lamb," the lion admitted, his fierce growl softening to a sad rumble. "I have spent so much time making sure everyone fears me that I never learned how to make anyone like me. I thought being the strongest made me the best, but what is the point of being king if your kingdom is empty of friendship and joy?"
Page 3
The lamb moved closer to the lion, showing no fear of his sharp claws or powerful jaws. "Being strong is not wrong," she said gently. "But there are different kinds of strength. You have the strength to protect and defend. I have seen how other animals scatter when predators threaten them, but they might feel safer if they knew a strong protector was nearby, not just another danger they had to avoid."
Page 4
As the two unlikely friends continued their conversation, the lion began to understand that true leadership meant more than simply being the most feared animal in the land. The lamb showed him that gentleness could be just as powerful as ferocity, that wisdom could be more valuable than sharp teeth, and that earning respect through kindness was far more rewarding than demanding it through intimidation and threats.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. isolated - separated from others; alone
2. intimidation - frightening someone to make them do what you want
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. What did the lion realize about his reign of fear?
	•	That it made him the happiest animal
	•	That it had isolated him and left him without friendship
	•	That he needed to be even more frightening
2. What did the lamb teach the lion about strength?
	•	That only big animals can be strong
	•	That there are different kinds of strength, including the strength to protect
	•	That strength is not important

Chapter 6: A New Kind of Kingdom
Page 1
From that day forward, the lion began to change the way he ruled his kingdom. Instead of roaring to frighten the other animals, he would call out friendly greetings. Instead of prowling through their gathering places to show his dominance, he would visit to ask if they needed his help with any dangers or problems. The other animals were surprised and cautious at first, but gradually they began to trust this new, kinder version of their king.
Page 2
The lamb stayed with the lion for several days, teaching him about the gentle arts of friendship and cooperation. She showed him how to listen carefully when other animals spoke about their troubles, how to offer advice without demanding immediate obedience, and how to use his strength to protect the weak rather than to frighten them. Under her patient guidance, the lion learned that true power came from serving others, not from ruling over them.
Page 3
Soon, the lion's territory became a place where all animals felt safe and welcome. When hyenas threatened the grazing herds, the lion would drive them away, but not with cruel violence – instead, he would roar just loud enough to remind the intruders that peaceful animals were under his protection. When disputes arose between different groups, he would listen to all sides and help them find fair solutions that made everyone happy.
Page 4
The lamb eventually returned to her peaceful meadow, but she and the lion remained the best of friends, visiting each other regularly and sharing the wisdom they had learned together. Their friendship became a legend throughout the animal kingdom, proving that the strongest and gentlest creatures could work together to create a world where both power and kindness had their proper place, and where true leadership meant protecting and serving those who depended on you.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. cooperation - working together toward a common goal
2. disputes - disagreements or arguments
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the lion change his approach to ruling?
	•	He became even more frightening
	•	He began greeting animals friendly and offering help instead of intimidation
	•	He stopped being the king
2. What did their friendship prove to the animal kingdom?
	•	That big animals are always better
	•	That the strongest and gentlest can work together for a better world
	•	That animals should live separately
