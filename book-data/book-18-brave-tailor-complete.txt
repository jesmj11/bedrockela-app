Chapter 1: Seven at One Stroke
Page 1
Once upon a time, there lived a little tailor who was skilled at making beautiful clothes, but he was very poor. He worked hard every day in his small shop, sewing jackets and mending torn garments for the people in his village. Though he was tiny in size, he had a brave heart and a clever mind.
Page 2
One particularly hot summer morning, the little tailor was having his breakfast of bread and jam when he noticed that his simple meal had attracted an enormous swarm of buzzing flies. The pesky insects covered his food completely, making it impossible for him to eat his well-deserved morning meal.
Page 3
"Enough of this nonsense!" declared the tailor boldly. He grabbed a cloth from his workbench and swung it with all his might at the annoying flies. WHAP! When the dust settled, he discovered that his single powerful swing had killed exactly seven flies at once. He could hardly believe his remarkable success!
Page 4
Feeling tremendously proud of his achievement, the little tailor decided to make a special belt to commemorate this extraordinary feat. He carefully embroidered the words "Seven at One Stroke" in golden thread across the leather. He wore his new belt with great pride, never imagining the adventures it would soon bring him.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. swarm - a large group of insects flying together
2. commemorate - to honor the memory of something special
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions

	•	Flies were covering his food
	•	He had no money for food
2. What did the tailor embroider on his belt?
	•	"Brave Little Tailor"
	•	"Seven at One Stroke"
	•	"Fly Killer"

Chapter 2: The Tailor Becomes Famous
Page 1
The little tailor decided to venture forth into the wide world wearing his magnificent new belt. As he walked through the marketplace, people noticed the bold golden letters proclaiming "Seven at One Stroke" and began whispering excitedly among themselves about this mysterious stranger who had accomplished such an incredible feat.
Page 2
Word of the tailor's amazing deed spread rapidly throughout the countryside like wildfire. People assumed he had defeated seven mighty warriors in a single battle, not seven tiny flies at his breakfast table! Soon messengers arrived with news that even the king himself had heard about the brave little tailor's legendary prowess.
Page 3
The king's royal messenger approached the tailor with great ceremony and deep respect. "His Majesty requests your immediate presence at the palace," announced the messenger formally. "The king has heard of your remarkable strength and courage, and he has an important mission that requires a hero of your extraordinary capabilities."
Page 4
The little tailor was astonished by this unexpected turn of events, but he was far too curious and adventurous to refuse such an intriguing royal summons. He packed his few belongings and followed the messenger eagerly, wondering what dangerous quest the king might have in mind for someone with his supposed legendary fighting skills.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. prowess - great skill or ability, especially in fighting or battle
2. intriguing - very interesting and mysterious
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. What did people think the tailor had defeated?
	•	Seven flies
	•	Seven mighty warriors
	•	Seven wild animals
2. Who wanted to meet the tailor?
	•	The village mayor
	•	The king
	•	Another tailor

Chapter 3: A Dangerous Mission
Page 1
At the magnificent royal palace, the king received the little tailor with great ceremony and fanfare. "Welcome, brave warrior!" declared the king majestically. "My kingdom faces a terrible crisis, and I believe you are exactly the hero we desperately need to solve this dangerous problem that threatens all of our peaceful subjects."
Page 2
The king explained that two enormous giants had been terrorizing the countryside for many months. These fearsome monsters were destroying villages, stealing livestock, and frightening all the hardworking people who lived in the surrounding areas. Many brave knights and soldiers had tried to defeat the giants, but all had failed miserably in their attempts.
Page 3
"If you can defeat these terrible giants," promised the king hopefully, "I will give you half my kingdom and my daughter's hand in marriage as your reward!" The little tailor's eyes grew wide with amazement at such a generous offer, though he was secretly terrified by the enormity of the challenge that lay ahead of him.
Page 4
Despite his fear, the clever little tailor realized that this was the opportunity of a lifetime. He accepted the king's dangerous mission with outward confidence, though his heart was beating rapidly with nervousness. He knew he would need to use his wits rather than his strength to have any hope of succeeding against such formidable opponents.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. terrorizing - frightening and threatening people
2. formidable - very powerful and difficult to defeat
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. What problem was threatening the kingdom?
	•	A terrible drought
	•	Two enormous giants
	•	A wicked witch
2. What did the king promise as a reward?
	•	A bag of gold
	•	Half his kingdom and his daughter's hand in marriage
	•	A new castle

Chapter 4: Outwitting the Giants
Page 1
The little tailor ventured bravely into the dark, mysterious forest where the giants were known to live. After walking for several hours through the thick undergrowth, he finally discovered the two massive creatures sleeping peacefully beneath an enormous oak tree. They were even larger and more frightening than he had imagined possible!
Page 2
The clever tailor quickly devised a brilliant plan to defeat the giants without engaging them in direct combat. He quietly gathered a pile of heavy stones and climbed silently up into the branches of the great oak tree, positioning himself directly above the sleeping monsters. His heart pounded with excitement and terror as he prepared to execute his scheme.
Page 3
From his hidden perch high in the tree, the tailor carefully aimed and dropped stones onto each giant alternately. When the first giant felt the impact, he woke up angrily and immediately accused his companion of hitting him during sleep. "Why did you strike me?" he roared furiously at the other giant.
Page 4
"I never touched you!" protested the second giant indignantly, just as another stone struck him on the head. Soon both giants were convinced that the other was attacking them deliberately. They began fighting each other with tremendous fury, throwing massive punches and wrestling violently until they had knocked each other completely unconscious! The tailor's ingenious plan had worked perfectly.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. devised - created or invented a plan
2. ingenious - very clever and creative
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

	•	In a cave
	•	Sleeping under an enormous oak tree
	•	By a river
2. How did the tailor defeat the giants?
	•	He fought them with a sword
	•	He made them fight each other by dropping stones
	•	He used magic

Chapter 5: Return to the Palace
Page 1
When the unconscious giants finally awakened hours later, they were both so battered and bruised from their fierce battle that they could barely stand upright. Groaning with pain and completely exhausted from their violent fight, they decided to leave the forest immediately and never return to terrorize the peaceful kingdom again.
Page 2
Meanwhile, the triumphant little tailor hurried back to the royal palace to report his incredible success to the amazed king. "Your Majesty," he announced proudly, "the giant problem has been solved completely! The terrible monsters have been defeated and driven from your kingdom forever. Your subjects can now live in peace and safety once more."
Page 3
The king could hardly believe that such a small man had accomplished what his mightiest knights and strongest soldiers had failed to achieve. He was both grateful and impressed by the tailor's remarkable feat, though he was secretly worried about keeping his promise to give away half his kingdom to someone so seemingly ordinary.
Page 4
However, being a man of honor and integrity, the king kept his royal word exactly as he had promised. He arranged a magnificent wedding celebration for the brave little tailor and his daughter, and officially granted him half of the kingdom to rule wisely and justly. The clever tailor had achieved far more than he ever dreamed possible!
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. battered - badly damaged or hurt from fighting
2. integrity - being honest and keeping your promises
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. What happened to the giants after their fight?
	•	They became friends
	•	They left the forest and never returned
	•	They continued terrorizing the kingdom
2. How did the king feel about the tailor's success?
	•	Angry and disappointed
	•	Grateful and impressed, though worried
	•	Completely uninterested

Chapter 6: The Wise Ruler
Page 1
As the new ruler of half the kingdom, the former little tailor proved to be an exceptionally wise and compassionate leader. He used the same clever thinking that had helped him defeat the giants to solve problems and settle disputes fairly among his subjects. His people quickly learned to respect and admire their surprisingly capable new ruler.
Page 2
The tailor never forgot his humble beginnings in the small sewing shop, and he always treated the poor and working people of his kingdom with special kindness and understanding. He established fair laws, encouraged trade and craftsmanship, and made sure that everyone in his realm had enough food to eat and a safe place to live.
Page 3
Years later, when visitors would ask about his famous belt that still proclaimed "Seven at One Stroke," the wise ruler would smile mysteriously and tell them that sometimes the greatest victories come not from physical strength, but from using intelligence, creativity, and courage to solve seemingly impossible problems in unexpected ways.
Page 4
And so the brave little tailor lived happily ever after, ruling his kingdom with wisdom and fairness, beloved by all his subjects who had come to understand that true heroism comes in many different forms. His story became a legend that inspired countless others to believe that with cleverness and courage, even the smallest person can achieve the most extraordinary things.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. compassionate - kind and caring about others' problems
2. extraordinary - amazing and very unusual; beyond ordinary
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions

	•	Lazy and careless
2. What lesson does the story teach about heroism?
	•	Only big, strong people can be heroes
	•	True heroism comes from cleverness and courage, not just strength
	•	Heroes must always tell the truth
