Chapter 1: A Lucky Discovery
Page 1
In a bustling village where the market square was always filled with the sounds of merchants calling out their wares and customers bargaining for the best prices, there lived a clever brown dog who had learned to survive by his wits and quick reflexes. This resourceful canine had no master to feed him regular meals, so he spent his days searching for scraps of food wherever he could find them.
Page 2
The dog had become quite skilled at finding opportunities that other animals might miss. He knew exactly which bakery threw out day-old bread behind their shop, which butcher was generous with bone scraps, and what time the fish market closed so he could search for any dropped morsels. His keen nose and sharp eyes made him an expert at discovering hidden treasures that could satisfy his constant hunger.
Page 3
One particularly fortunate morning, as the dog was making his usual rounds through the market area, his sensitive nose detected the most wonderful smell he had encountered in weeks. Following the delicious aroma, he discovered that the butcher had accidentally dropped a large, juicy bone right outside his shop. The bone was still fresh and covered with bits of tender meat that made the dog's mouth water with anticipation.
Page 4
The dog couldn't believe his extraordinary luck! This wasn't just any ordinary scrap or small leftover - this was a magnificent bone that would provide him with the best meal he had enjoyed in months. He quickly snatched it up in his jaws, feeling the satisfying weight of his prize and savoring the rich, meaty flavor that promised hours of happy gnawing and a completely satisfied stomach.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. resourceful - able to find clever ways to solve problems
2. morsels - small pieces of food
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions

	•	By using his wits and searching for food scraps
	•	By stealing from other dogs
2. What made this particular discovery so special?
	•	It was a small, ordinary bone
	•	It was a large, juicy bone with tender meat - his best find in months
	•	It was made of gold

Chapter 2: Seeking a Safe Place
Page 1
Now that the dog had secured such a valuable prize, he faced a new challenge: where could he safely enjoy his feast without worrying about other animals trying to steal his treasure? The busy market square was full of competing dogs, hungry cats, and bold ravens who would not hesitate to snatch away such a delicious meal if they had the chance.
Page 2
The dog looked around carefully, holding his precious bone firmly in his mouth while considering his options. He needed somewhere quiet and secluded, away from the crowds and the constant threat of thieves who might try to take what he had worked so hard to find. His experience as a street dog had taught him to always be cautious when he possessed something valuable.
Page 3
After thinking for a moment, the dog remembered a peaceful spot he had discovered during his wanderings - a quiet grove of trees beside a gentle stream, far from the noise and bustle of the village. It would be the perfect place to enjoy his meal in solitude, without any interruptions or threats from competitors who might want to share his good fortune.
Page 4
With his destination decided, the dog began his journey toward the stream, walking carefully to avoid attracting attention from other hungry animals who might follow him to his secret dining spot. The bone felt heavier with each step, but the dog's excitement about his upcoming feast made the weight seem insignificant compared to the pleasure he anticipated from this magnificent meal.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. secluded - private and away from other people
2. solitude - the state of being alone
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. Why was the dog worried about staying in the market square?
	•	It was too noisy
	•	Other animals might try to steal his bone
	•	The market was closed
2. Where did the dog decide to go with his bone?
	•	Back to his home
	•	A quiet grove of trees beside a gentle stream
	•	To another market

Chapter 3: The Bridge Crossing
Page 1
To reach his peaceful dining spot beside the stream, the dog had to cross a narrow wooden bridge that spanned the flowing water. The old bridge was sturdy enough, but it was quite narrow, with wooden planks that creaked softly under his weight as he walked carefully across, still holding his precious bone securely between his teeth.
Page 2
Halfway across the bridge, the dog paused to rest for a moment and enjoy the peaceful sound of water flowing gently beneath him. The stream was crystal clear, reflecting the blue sky and white clouds like a perfect mirror. Birds sang in the trees on both sides of the water, and a gentle breeze carried the fresh scent of wildflowers and green grass.
Page 3
As the dog stood on the bridge, enjoying the tranquil scene and anticipating his delicious meal, he happened to glance down at the water below. The surface was so calm and clear that it acted like a perfect mirror, showing him his own reflection with startling clarity. He could see himself standing there with the bone in his mouth, looking exactly as he would appear to someone watching from below.
Page 4
But what the dog saw in the water's surface made his eyes widen with surprise and excitement. There, looking back at him from the stream, was another dog who appeared to be holding a bone that looked just as large and juicy as his own! The reflection was so perfect and realistic that the dog didn't realize he was looking at his own image reflected in the water.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. tranquil - peaceful and calm
2. reflection - an image seen in a mirror or water
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. What was special about the stream under the bridge?
	•	It was very deep and dangerous
	•	It was crystal clear and reflected like a perfect mirror
	•	It was full of fish
2. What did the dog think he saw in the water?
	•	A fish swimming
	•	Another dog with a bone just as large as his own
	•	A treasure chest

Chapter 4: Greed Takes Hold
Page 1
The dog stared at the reflection in amazement, his mind racing with exciting possibilities. If there was another dog down there with an equally delicious bone, then perhaps he could somehow get that second bone too! Instead of being satisfied with his current good fortune, the greedy thought of having twice as much food filled his imagination with visions of an incredible feast.
Page 2
"Why should I be content with just one bone," the dog thought to himself, "when there's clearly another dog right there with a bone that looks just as good as mine? If I'm clever enough, maybe I can convince him to share, or perhaps I can even take his bone away from him. After all, I found mine through my own hard work and intelligence."
Page 3
The more the dog stared at the reflection, the more convinced he became that he deserved to have both bones. He had spent so much time and effort searching for food every day, surely he was entitled to this extra reward! The bone in the water looked just as fresh and meaty as his own, and the idea of having two delicious meals instead of one seemed too good to pass up.
Page 4
Greed began to cloud the dog's judgment as he plotted how he might obtain the second bone. He completely forgot about finding a peaceful place to enjoy his current treasure, and instead became obsessed with the possibility of doubling his good fortune. The reflection seemed to taunt him, holding that tempting second bone just out of reach, challenging him to find a way to claim what he now believed should rightfully be his.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. entitled - believing you deserve something
2. obsessed - thinking about something constantly
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. What happened to the dog's attitude when he saw the reflection?
	•	He became grateful for what he had
	•	He became greedy and wanted the second bone too
	•	He became scared and ran away
2. What did greed do to the dog's thinking?
	•	It made him wiser
	•	It clouded his judgment and made him forget his peaceful plan
	•	It had no effect

Chapter 5: A Costly Mistake
Page 1
Unable to resist the temptation of getting the second bone, the dog decided to take action. He would bark at the other dog in the water and demand that he drop his bone, just as he had seen stronger dogs do in the market when they wanted to intimidate weaker animals into giving up their food. Surely this strategy would work just as well here.
Page 2
The dog opened his mouth wide to let out his most fierce and threatening bark, hoping to frighten the reflection into dropping its bone. But the moment he opened his jaws to bark, disaster struck! His own precious bone fell from his mouth, tumbling down through the air and landing in the stream below with a splash that shattered the perfect reflection into rippling circles.
Page 3
The dog watched in horror as his beautiful bone sank beneath the surface of the water, disappearing forever into the depths of the stream. In that terrible moment, he realized the awful truth - there had never been another dog at all! What he had seen was merely his own reflection, and in his greedy attempt to get a second bone that didn't really exist, he had lost the wonderful prize he already possessed.
Page 4
The dog stared down at the water in disbelief, watching the ripples slowly fade until the surface became calm and mirror-like once again. Now the reflection showed only a foolish dog with no bone at all, standing alone on the bridge with nothing to show for his morning's good fortune. His greed had cost him everything, leaving him hungrier and more disappointed than he had been before he found the bone.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. intimidate - to frighten someone to make them do what you want
2. disbelief - inability to believe that something is true
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. What happened when the dog opened his mouth to bark?
	•	The other dog ran away
	•	His own bone fell from his mouth into the stream
	•	He got the second bone
2. What did the dog realize after he lost his bone?
	•	That there were many other dogs around
	•	That there had never been another dog - only his own reflection
	•	That he could swim down to get it

Chapter 6: A Hard Lesson Learned
Page 1
The dog stood on the bridge for a long time, staring down at the water and thinking about his terrible mistake. He had started the day as one of the luckiest dogs in the village, with the finest bone he had found in months. But his greed and desire for more had transformed his good fortune into complete loss, leaving him with nothing but regret and an empty stomach.
Page 2
As the dog finally turned away from the stream and began his sad journey back to the village, he reflected on the lesson his experience had taught him. If he had been content with his original good fortune and simply proceeded to his quiet spot by the stream, he would now be enjoying a delicious meal instead of wandering hungry and disappointed through the countryside.
Page 3
The dog realized that his greed had blinded him to the value of what he already possessed. Instead of appreciating his lucky find and feeling grateful for such an unexpected blessing, he had immediately focused on getting more, even when that "more" was nothing but an illusion. His desire for something extra had cost him the very thing he had been so happy to discover.
Page 4
From that day forward, the dog never forgot the expensive lesson he learned on the bridge. Whenever he was fortunate enough to find food or shelter, he focused on being grateful for what he had rather than immediately wanting more. He discovered that contentment with his blessings brought him more happiness than constantly seeking additional rewards ever could, and he became known as the wisest dog in the village.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. illusion - something that appears real but is not
2. contentment - satisfaction and happiness with what you have
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions
1. What lesson did the dog learn from his experience?
	•	That he should always bark at other dogs
	•	That greed can cause you to lose what you already have
	•	That reflections are dangerous
2. How did the dog change after this experience?
	•	He became meaner to other animals
	•	He learned to be grateful and content with what he had
	•	He never looked at water again
