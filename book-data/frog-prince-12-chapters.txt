Chapter 1: The Golden Ball
Once upon a time, there lived a young princess who was known throughout the kingdom for her beauty. Her hair was like spun gold, and her eyes sparkled like jewels.
The princess lived in a magnificent palace with her father, the king. She had everything a girl could want - beautiful dresses, precious jewels, and servants to attend to her every need.
But the princess's most treasured possession was a golden ball. It was perfectly round and so shiny that it seemed to glow with its own light.
Every day, the princess would take her golden ball to the forest near the palace. There, beside a deep, dark well, she would throw the ball high into the air and catch it again.
This was her favorite game, and she could play it for hours. The golden ball would sparkle as it flew through the sunbeams, and the princess would laugh with delight.
'Higher, higher!' she would say to herself, tossing the ball toward the treetops. She never grew tired of watching it spin and gleam in the afternoon sun.
The well beside her was very old and very deep. It was said to be so deep that no one had ever seen the bottom. The princess never paid much attention to it.

Chapter 2: Into the Deep Well
One particularly warm summer day, the princess was playing with her golden ball as usual. She threw it higher than ever before, watching it glitter against the blue sky.
But this time, when the ball came down, her hands were wet with perspiration from the heat. The golden ball slipped right through her fingers!
The princess watched in horror as her precious ball bounced once on the ground and then rolled directly toward the old well.
'No, no, no!' cried the princess, running after it. But she was too late. The golden ball rolled right over the edge and disappeared into the dark depths below.
The princess fell to her knees beside the well and peered down into the blackness. She could not see her ball anywhere, no matter how hard she strained her eyes.
'My beautiful golden ball!' she wailed. 'It's gone forever! I'll never see it again!' Tears began to stream down her face as she realized her treasure was lost.
The princess sat beside the well and cried as if her heart would break. She had never lost anything precious before, and she didn't know how to handle such sadness.

Chapter 3: A Strange Voice
As the princess sat crying beside the well, she suddenly heard a voice calling to her. 'Princess, princess, why are you crying so sadly?'
The princess looked around in surprise. She saw no one in the forest clearing. 'Who's there?' she called out. 'Where are you?'
'I'm down here, in the well,' came the reply. The princess looked down and saw the strangest sight - a large, green frog sitting on a lily pad that floated on the water far below.
'You can talk?' gasped the princess. She had never heard of a talking frog before, though she had seen many ordinary frogs in the palace gardens.
'Indeed I can,' said the frog politely. 'I heard you crying and came to see what was wrong. Perhaps I can help you with your troubles.'
The princess wrinkled her nose with disgust. The frog was slimy and ugly, with bulging eyes and wet, green skin. She could barely stand to look at it.
'I doubt you can help me,' she said rudely. 'My precious golden ball fell into your well, and now it's lost forever. What could an ugly creature like you possibly do about it?'

Chapter 4: The Frog's Bargain
The frog was not offended by the princess's rude words. Instead, he spoke kindly and calmly. 'I can swim to the bottom of this well and bring back your golden ball.'
The princess's eyes grew wide with hope. 'You can? You would do that for me? Oh, please, dear frog! I would give you anything!'
'Anything?' asked the frog thoughtfully. 'I don't need your gold or jewels or fine clothes. But there is something I would like very much.'
'Name it!' cried the princess eagerly. 'Whatever you want, it's yours! Just please get my ball back!'
The frog cleared his throat. 'I would like to be your friend and companion. I want to eat from your golden plate and drink from your golden cup at dinner.'
'And,' the frog continued, 'I would like to sleep on your soft pillow in your beautiful bedroom. If you promise me these things, I will get your ball.'
The princess shuddered at the thought of the slimy frog touching her things. But she wanted her golden ball back so desperately that she was willing to agree to anything.

Chapter 5: A Promise Made
'Very well,' said the princess quickly. 'I promise you can eat from my plate, drink from my cup, and sleep on my pillow. Just please get my ball!'
The frog nodded seriously. 'You must give me your word as a princess that you will keep this promise. Do you swear it?'
'Yes, yes, I swear it!' said the princess impatiently. 'I promise everything you asked for. Now please hurry!'
But in her heart, the princess was thinking, 'This silly frog can never leave his well. How could he possibly come to the palace? I'll get my ball back, and then I'll never have to see him again.'
The frog seemed satisfied with her promise. He dove deep into the well with a splash, disappearing beneath the dark water.
The princess waited anxiously, watching the surface of the water for any sign of the frog. Minutes passed like hours as she wondered if he would really be able to find her ball.
Finally, the frog's head popped up above the water. In his mouth, glinting like a small sun, was her precious golden ball!

Chapter 6: Back at the Palace
'My golden ball!' cried the princess joyfully. The frog swam to the edge of the well and tossed the ball up to her with his mouth.
The princess caught the ball and hugged it to her chest. She was so happy to have it back that she forgot all about the frog who had helped her.
'Wait!' called the frog as the princess turned to leave. 'Don't forget your promise! Take me with you to the palace!'
'Don't be ridiculous,' laughed the princess cruelly. 'I can't take a slimy frog to the palace. Stay in your well where you belong!'
Without another word, the princess ran back through the forest toward her father's palace. She clutched her golden ball tightly and didn't look back once.
The poor frog sat sadly on the edge of the well, watching her disappear through the trees. 'She promised,' he said to himself quietly. 'A princess should keep her word.'
Back at the palace, the princess quickly forgot about the frog. She was so happy to have her ball back that she spent the rest of the day playing with it in the royal gardens.

Chapter 7: An Unexpected Visitor
That evening, the princess sat down to dinner with the king in the grand dining hall. They dined on the finest foods served on golden plates with golden cups.
The princess was telling her father about her day when suddenly they heard a strange sound at the door: 'Splish, splash, splish, splash.'
Then came a gentle knocking and a voice called out: 'Princess, dear princess, please open the door for me. Remember the promise you made by the old well's shore.'
The princess's face turned pale as she recognized the voice. 'Oh no,' she whispered to herself. 'It can't be the frog! How did he get out of the well?'
'Who is at the door?' asked the king curiously. 'And what promise are they talking about?'
The princess felt trapped. She couldn't lie to her father, but she was too ashamed to tell him the whole truth about her broken promise.
'It's... it's just a frog, Father,' she said reluctantly. 'He helped me get my golden ball back from the well today.'

Chapter 8: The King's Command
'A frog?' said the king in surprise. 'How did a frog help you, and what promise did you make to him?'
Reluctantly, the princess told her father the whole story - how her ball had fallen into the well, how the frog had rescued it, and what she had promised in return.
'But Father,' she added quickly, 'I never thought he would actually come to the palace! How could I have dinner with a disgusting, slimy frog?'
The king's face grew stern. 'Daughter, a promise is a promise, especially one made by a princess. If this frog helped you, then you must keep your word.'
'Go and open the door this instant,' commanded the king. 'We do not break our promises in this royal family, no matter who we make them to.'
The princess had no choice but to obey her father. With heavy steps, she walked to the door and opened it slowly.
There sat the frog, looking up at her with his big, gentle eyes. 'Thank you, Princess,' he said politely. 'I knew you would remember your promise.'

Chapter 9: Dinner with a Frog
'Come in, little frog,' said the king kindly. 'Any creature who helps my daughter is welcome in our palace.'
The frog hopped slowly across the marble floor, leaving little wet spots with each jump. The princess watched with disgust, but she didn't dare disobey her father.
'I cannot climb up to the table by myself,' said the frog politely. 'Would you please lift me up so I can sit beside you, Princess?'
The princess shuddered but picked up the wet, cold frog and placed him on the chair next to hers. She tried not to touch him any more than necessary.
'Now I would like to eat from your golden plate, as you promised,' said the frog. He hopped onto the table and began to eat daintily from the princess's food.
The princess lost her appetite completely. She couldn't bear to eat from the same plate that the frog was using. The king watched everything with interest.
When the frog was finished eating, he asked for a drink from the princess's golden cup. Again, she had to share, though it made her feel quite sick.

Chapter 10: The Frog's Demands
After dinner, the frog sat contentedly on the table, his belly full of the finest royal food. 'That was delicious,' he said. 'Thank you, Princess.'
'Now I am very tired,' continued the frog. 'It has been a long day, and I would like to sleep on your soft pillow, as you promised.'
'Absolutely not!' cried the princess. 'I shared my food and drink with you, but I will not have a wet, slimy frog in my beautiful bedroom!'
The king frowned at his daughter. 'You made a complete promise, and you must keep all of it. Take the frog to your room at once.'
'But Father—' the princess began to protest.
'No arguments,' said the king firmly. 'A promise is sacred. This frog trusted you and helped you when you needed it. You cannot break your word now.'
With tears of frustration in her eyes, the princess picked up the frog and carried him to her bedroom. She placed him on the floor as far from her bed as possible.

Chapter 11: A Promise Broken
'I kept my promise,' said the princess crossly. 'You're in my bedroom. You can sleep on the floor.'
'But you promised I could sleep on your soft pillow,' said the frog sadly. 'I'm very small and cold, and the floor is hard and uncomfortable.'
The princess was tired and angry. She had been forced to share her dinner with a frog, and now he wanted to sleep in her beautiful bed. It was too much to bear.
'I've had enough of you!' she shouted. 'I never wanted to make that stupid promise! You're disgusting and slimy, and I wish I'd never met you!'
In her anger, the princess picked up the poor frog and threw him as hard as she could against the bedroom wall.
But the moment the frog touched the wall, something magical happened. There was a flash of golden light, and suddenly the frog was gone.
In his place stood the most handsome young prince the princess had ever seen. He had kind eyes, a warm smile, and was dressed in fine royal clothing.

Chapter 12: The Magic of Truth
'Don't be afraid,' said the prince gently, seeing the princess's shocked expression. 'I am the frog who helped you today.'
'But how?' gasped the princess. 'You were a frog! What kind of magic is this?'
'A wicked witch cast a spell on me long ago,' explained the prince. 'I could only return to my true form when someone saw past my ugly appearance to my true character.'
The princess felt ashamed of how rudely she had treated him. 'I was horrible to you,' she whispered. 'I broke my promise and threw you against the wall.'
'But in the end, you kept me close to you despite my appearance,' said the prince kindly. 'Even in anger, you showed me your true feelings. That broke the spell.'
'I learned something important today,' said the princess. 'I shouldn't judge others by how they look, and I should always keep my promises, even when it's difficult.'
The prince smiled. 'And I learned that true friendship must be earned through kindness, not demanded through promises.'
From that day forward, the princess and the prince became the best of friends. She learned to value honesty and kindness above beauty, and they both lived happily ever after.

The End
