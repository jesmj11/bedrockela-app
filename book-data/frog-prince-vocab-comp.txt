The Frog Prince
Chapter Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions
3rd Grade Teacher's Guide

Chapter 1: The Golden Ball
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. magnificent - very beautiful and impressive
	•	2. treasured - valued greatly; kept as something very special
	•	3. possession - something that belongs to you
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What was the princess known for throughout the kingdom?
	•	2. What was the princess's most treasured possession?
	•	3. Where did the princess like to play with her golden ball?
	•	4. What was her favorite game?
	•	5. Why didn't the princess pay attention to the well?

Chapter 2: Into the Deep Well
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. perspiration - sweat from being hot or working hard
	•	2. depths - the deepest parts of something
	•	3. strained - tried very hard
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What was different about this particular summer day?
	•	2. Why did the ball slip through the princess's fingers?
	•	3. What happened to the golden ball?
	•	4. How did the princess react when she lost her ball?
	•	5. Why had the princess never experienced this kind of sadness before?

Chapter 3: A Strange Voice
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. clearing - an open space in a forest
	•	2. bulging - sticking out; swelling
	•	3. disgust - a feeling of strong dislike
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What surprising thing happened while the princess was crying?
	•	2. Where was the voice coming from?
	•	3. What did the princess see when she looked down the well?
	•	4. How did the princess react to seeing the frog?
	•	5. What rude things did the princess say to the frog?

Chapter 4: The Frog's Bargain
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. offended - hurt or upset by someone's words or actions
	•	2. bargain - an agreement between two people
	•	3. companion - a friend who spends time with you
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the frog react to the princess's rude words?
	•	2. What did the frog offer to do for the princess?
	•	3. What did the frog say he didn't need as payment?
	•	4. What three things did the frog ask for in return?
	•	5. How did the princess feel about the frog's requests?

Chapter 5: A Promise Made
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. impatiently - in a hurry; not wanting to wait
	•	2. anxiously - with worry and nervous feelings
	•	3. glinting - shining with small flashes of light
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the princess promise the frog?
	•	2. What was the princess really thinking when she made the promise?
	•	3. Why didn't the princess think she would have to keep her promise?
	•	4. How long did the princess have to wait for the frog to return?
	•	5. What did the frog bring back with him?

Chapter 6: Back at the Palace
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. joyfully - with great happiness and excitement
	•	2. ridiculous - very silly or unreasonable
	•	3. cruelly - in a mean and hurtful way
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the princess react when she got her ball back?
	•	2. What did she forget about in her happiness?
	•	3. What did the frog ask the princess to do?
	•	4. How did the princess respond to the frog's request?
	•	5. How did the frog feel after the princess left?

Chapter 7: An Unexpected Visitor
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. dining - eating a meal
	•	2. recognized - knew from before
	•	3. ashamed - feeling bad about something you did wrong
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What were the princess and king doing when they heard the strange sounds?
	•	2. What sounds did they hear at the door?
	•	3. What did the voice at the door say?
	•	4. How did the princess react when she recognized the voice?
	•	5. Why was the princess ashamed to tell her father the whole truth?

Chapter 8: The King's Command
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. reluctantly - not wanting to do something
	•	2. stern - serious and firm
	•	3. commanded - ordered someone to do something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the king want to know about the frog?
	•	2. What excuse did the princess give for not wanting to keep her promise?
	•	3. What important lesson did the king teach his daughter?
	•	4. What did the king command the princess to do?
	•	5. How did the frog greet the princess when she opened the door?

Chapter 9: Dinner with a Frog
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. marble - a hard, smooth stone used in buildings
	•	2. daintily - in a careful, delicate way
	•	3. appetite - the desire to eat food
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the king welcome the frog to the palace?
	•	2. What did the frog ask the princess to help him do?
	•	3. How did the frog eat from the princess's plate?
	•	4. Why did the princess lose her appetite?
	•	5. What did the frog drink from after eating?

Chapter 10: The Frog's Demands
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. contentedly - in a happy, satisfied way
	•	2. protest - to argue against something
	•	3. sacred - very important and must be respected
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the frog feel after dinner?
	•	2. What final request did the frog make?
	•	3. How did the princess react to this request?
	•	4. What did the king say about promises?
	•	5. Where did the princess place the frog in her bedroom?

Chapter 11: A Promise Broken
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. crossly - in an angry, annoyed way
	•	2. uncomfortable - not pleasant; causing discomfort
	•	3. disgusting - very unpleasant; causing strong dislike
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did the princess try to avoid fully keeping her promise?
	•	2. Why did the frog say the floor was uncomfortable?
	•	3. What finally made the princess lose her temper completely?
	•	4. What did the princess do to the frog in her anger?
	•	5. What magical thing happened when the frog hit the wall?

Chapter 12: The Magic of Truth
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. appearance - how someone or something looks
	•	2. character - the kind of person someone really is inside
	•	3. earned - gotten through hard work or good behavior
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who was the handsome prince really?
	•	2. What had happened to the prince long ago?
	•	3. How could the spell be broken?
	•	4. What important lesson did the princess learn?
	•	5. What lesson did the prince learn about friendship?

Discussion Questions for the Complete Story
	•	1. Why was it wrong for the princess to break her promise to the frog?
	•	2. How did the king show good leadership when he made his daughter keep her word?
	•	3. What does this story teach us about judging people by their appearance?
	•	4. How did the princess change throughout the story?
	•	5. Why do you think the frog was so patient and kind despite the princess's rudeness?
	•	6. What does this story teach us about the importance of keeping promises?
	•	7. How might the story have been different if the princess had been kind from the beginning?
	•	8. What does it mean to see someone's true character rather than just their appearance?

Writing Prompts
	•	1. Write about a time when you made a promise that was hard to keep. What did you do?
	•	2. Describe someone you know who is kind and helpful, even when others aren't nice to them.
	•	3. Write a letter from the frog to the princess, explaining how he felt when she broke her promise.
	•	4. Create a new story about someone who learns not to judge others by their appearance.
	•	5. Write about what you think the princess and prince did together after they became friends.
