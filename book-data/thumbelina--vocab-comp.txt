Thumbelina
Chapter Vocabulary & Comprehension Questions
3rd Grade Teacher's Guide

Chapter 1: The Magic Seed
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. barley - a type of grain plant
	•	2. witch - a wise woman with magical powers
	•	3. bud - a flower before it opens
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What did the woman want more than anything?
	•	2. Who did the woman visit for help?
	•	3. What did the witch give to the woman?
	•	4. How did the woman care for the seed?
	•	5. What happened after a few days?

Chapter 2: Born from a Flower
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. petals - the colorful parts of a flower
	•	2. precious - very valuable and special
	•	3. oars - long poles used to row a boat
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What happened when the woman kissed the flower bud?
	•	2. How big was the little girl inside the flower?
	•	3. What name did the woman give the tiny girl?
	•	4. What did the woman use to make Thumbelina's bed?
	•	5. How did Thumbelina spend her days and nights?

Chapter 3: The Toad's Plan
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. croaked - the sound a toad or frog makes
	•	2. bulging - sticking out; swelling
	•	3. warty - covered with small bumps
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who came through the window while Thumbelina slept?
	•	2. What was the toad's plan for Thumbelina?
	•	3. Where did the toad take Thumbelina?
	•	4. Describe the toad's son.
	•	5. Why did the mother toad put Thumbelina on a lily pad?

Chapter 4: Escape on the Lily Pad
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. surrounded - all around; encircled
	•	2. nibbled - took small bites
	•	3. companion - a friend who travels with you
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did Thumbelina feel when she woke up on the lily pad?
	•	2. Who heard Thumbelina crying and decided to help?
	•	3. How did the fish help Thumbelina escape?
	•	4. What did Thumbelina use as a sail?
	•	5. Who became Thumbelina's traveling companion?

Chapter 5: The Beetle's Rejection
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. beetle - a type of insect with hard wing covers
	•	2. antennae - long thin parts on an insect's head that help it feel and smell
	•	3. rejected - turned away; not accepted
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Who grabbed Thumbelina from her lily pad?
	•	2. What did the beetle first think about Thumbelina?
	•	3. Why didn't the other beetles like Thumbelina?
	•	4. How did the beetles' comments affect the first beetle?
	•	5. How did Thumbelina feel after the beetle left her on the daisy?

Chapter 6: Alone in the Field
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. wove - made by weaving threads or grass together
	•	2. dewdrops - small drops of water that form in the morning
	•	3. brittle - dry and easily broken
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How did Thumbelina make a home for herself in the forest?
	•	2. What did she eat and drink during the summer?
	•	3. What happened when autumn came?
	•	4. How did winter affect Thumbelina?
	•	5. What did Thumbelina find at the edge of the field?

Chapter 7: Winter with the Field Mouse
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. organized - neat and orderly
	•	2. grateful - thankful
	•	3. velvet - a soft, smooth type of fabric
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What kind of creature was the field mouse?
	•	2. What did Thumbelina promise to do if she could stay?
	•	3. How did Thumbelina help the field mouse?
	•	4. Who was the field mouse's neighbor?
	•	5. What happened when the mole heard Thumbelina's voice?

Chapter 8: The Mole's Proposal
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. tunnels - underground passages
	•	2. treasure - valuable things like gold and jewels
	•	3. permission - being allowed to do something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What was the mole's underground home like?
	•	2. What did they find lying in one of the tunnels?
	•	3. How did the mole and field mouse feel about the swallow?
	•	4. What did the mole ask the field mouse that evening?
	•	5. How did Thumbelina feel about marrying the mole?

Chapter 9: The Swallow's Story
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. injured - hurt or wounded
	•	2. secretly - done in hiding; not telling others
	•	3. chirped - made soft bird sounds
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. Why couldn't Thumbelina sleep that night?
	•	2. What amazing discovery did Thumbelina make about the swallow?
	•	3. How did Thumbelina help the swallow?
	•	4. Why couldn't the swallow fly south with the other birds?
	•	5. Why did Thumbelina have to keep the swallow secret?

Chapter 10: A Kind Deed
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. glossy - shiny and smooth
	•	2. eagerly - with excitement and interest
	•	3. promise - a commitment to do something
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. How had the swallow changed by spring?
	•	2. What did the swallow invite Thumbelina to do?
	•	3. Why didn't Thumbelina want to leave with the swallow?
	•	4. What did the swallow say about Thumbelina's heart?
	•	5. How did Thumbelina feel after the swallow left?

Chapter 11: Flying South
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. preparing - getting ready for something
	•	2. familiar - known; recognized
	•	3. feathers - the soft covering on a bird's body
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What were the field mouse and mole doing during the summer?
	•	2. What did Thumbelina ask to do the day before her wedding?
	•	3. Who appeared when Thumbelina was outside crying?
	•	4. What did the swallow tell Thumbelina about the field mouse?
	•	5. What choice did Thumbelina make at the end of the chapter?

Chapter 12: The Flower Kingdom
Vocabulary Words
	•	1. palaces - large, beautiful homes for kings and queens
	•	2. gossamer - very thin and light, like spider webs
	•	3. kingdom - a place ruled by a king or queen
Comprehension Questions
	•	1. What was special about the warm country where they flew?
	•	2. Who did Thumbelina meet in the white flower?
	•	3. What made the flower people similar to Thumbelina?
	•	4. What did the flower prince ask Thumbelina?
	•	5. How did the story end for Thumbelina?

Discussion Questions for the Complete Story
	•	1. How did Thumbelina change from the beginning to the end of the story?
	•	2. Which characters tried to help Thumbelina? Which ones tried to use her?
	•	3. Why didn't Thumbelina want to marry the toad's son or the mole?
	•	4. What does this story teach us about being different from others?
	•	5. How did kindness play an important role in this story?
	•	6. Why was it important that Thumbelina saved the swallow?
	•	7. What do you think the flower kingdom represents in the story?
	•	8. How did Thumbelina show courage throughout her adventures?

Writing Prompts
	•	1. If you were tiny like Thumbelina, what adventures would you have? Write about your journey.
	•	2. Write a letter from Thumbelina to the field mouse, explaining why she had to leave.
	•	3. Describe what you think happened to Thumbelina after she became queen of the flower kingdom.
	•	4. Write about a time when you felt different from everyone else. How did you handle it?
	•	5. Create a new character who could be Thumbelina's friend. Describe their adventures together.
