Chapter 1: A Young Shepherd's Responsibility
Page 1
In a peaceful mountain village surrounded by rolling green hills and meadows filled with wildflowers, there lived a young shepherd boy named Marcus who had recently been entrusted with one of the most important responsibilities in his community. Every morning before dawn, he would carefully guide his father's valuable flock of sheep to the rich pastures high on the mountainside, where they could graze safely throughout the long day.
Page 2
Marcus took his duties seriously at first, understanding that his family's livelihood depended entirely upon the health and safety of these precious animals. The villagers had placed their complete trust in him, knowing that sheep were not only valuable for their wool and meat, but also represented the accumulated wealth and security of multiple families who had worked together to build this thriving pastoral community over many generations.
Page 3
The mountainous region where Marcus worked was known to be occasionally visited by wolves, fierce predators that would attack defenseless sheep if given the opportunity. For this reason, the village had established a time-honored system of protection: if any shepherd spotted a wolf approaching the flocks, he was to cry out loudly for help, and all the able-bodied men would immediately drop their work and rush to assist with whatever weapons they could carry.
Page 4
This emergency response system had served the village well for countless years, successfully protecting their livestock from numerous genuine threats and maintaining the economic stability that allowed their small mountain community to prosper despite the natural dangers that surrounded them. The system worked only because every villager understood the absolute importance of responding immediately to any shepherd's call for assistance, regardless of what other activities they might be engaged in at the time.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. entrusted - given responsibility for something important
2. pastoral - relating to the countryside and farming, especially sheep farming

Chapter 2: Boredom and Mischief
Page 1
After several weeks of faithfully performing his shepherding duties, Marcus began to find his daily routine increasingly monotonous and tedious. Day after day, he would sit alone on the hillside watching the sheep graze peacefully, with nothing but the sound of the wind and the occasional bleating of his flock to break the seemingly endless silence that surrounded him in his isolated mountain pasture.
Page 2
The young shepherd's mind began to wander as he contemplated how much more exciting life seemed to be down in the village, where people gathered in the marketplace to share news and stories, where children played games together, and where there was always some interesting activity or conversation to occupy one's attention. His solitary existence on the mountainside felt increasingly lonely and unstimulating.
Page 3
As his boredom intensified, Marcus found himself daydreaming about ways to create some excitement in his mundane daily routine. He watched the busy villagers going about their various activities far below and wondered what it would be like to have them all drop everything and come running up the mountainside, their faces filled with concern and urgency as they rushed to provide assistance.
Page 4
A mischievous idea began to form in Marcus's restless mind as he considered the emergency response system that the village had established for protecting the sheep. "What would happen," he wondered with growing curiosity and a complete lack of understanding about the serious consequences of his contemplated actions, "if I were to test this system by calling for help when there wasn't actually any real danger present?"
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. monotonous - dull and repetitive; lacking in variety
2. contemplated - thought about carefully; considered
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions

	•	Proud and important
2. What mischievous idea formed in Marcus's mind?
	•	To take the sheep to a different pasture
	•	To test the emergency system by calling for help when no danger was present
	•	To play games with the sheep

Chapter 3: The First False Alarm
Page 1
Unable to resist the temptation of his impulsive plan, Marcus decided to implement his mischievous scheme on a particularly quiet afternoon when his boredom had reached an almost unbearable level. He climbed to the highest point of his hillside pasture, took a deep breath, and began shouting as loudly as his voice would allow: "Wolf! Wolf! Help me! A wolf is attacking the sheep!"
Page 2
The effect of his deceptive cries was immediate and dramatic. Down in the village, every person who heard his urgent pleas stopped their work instantly and responded with the kind of selfless dedication that had made their community strong and resilient for generations. Men grabbed pitchforks, axes, and clubs, while others collected ropes and torches, all preparing to face a dangerous predator that threatened their collective livelihood.
Page 3
Within minutes, a breathless group of concerned villagers came running up the steep mountain path, their hearts pounding from both the physical exertion of the difficult climb and their genuine worry about Marcus's safety and the welfare of the precious sheep that represented so much of their community's economic foundation. They arrived ready to risk their own lives to protect both the boy and the animals from a vicious wolf attack.
Page 4
When the villagers discovered that there was no wolf anywhere to be found, and that all the sheep were grazing peacefully and completely unharmed, Marcus burst into delighted laughter at what he considered a tremendously successful practical joke. "Fooled you!" he called out cheerfully, completely oblivious to the serious nature of his deception and the profound disrespect he had shown to people who had dropped everything to help him in what they believed was a genuine emergency.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. impulsive - done suddenly without thinking carefully about consequences
2. oblivious - completely unaware of what is happening around you

2. How did Marcus react when the villagers found no wolf?
	•	He felt ashamed and apologized
	•	He burst into laughter, thinking it was a successful joke
	•	He pretended he had made an honest mistake

Chapter 4: A Second Deception
Page 1
The villagers were understandably annoyed and disappointed by Marcus's thoughtless prank, but they chose to respond with patience and understanding, recognizing that he was still young and might not fully comprehend the seriousness of his actions. They gave him a stern but gentle warning about the importance of honesty and the dangers of crying false alarms, hoping that this single incident would serve as a valuable learning experience for the immature shepherd.
Page 2
However, instead of feeling remorseful about his deceptive behavior, Marcus found himself energized and excited by the dramatic response his false alarm had generated. The memory of seeing the entire village mobilize so quickly and efficiently filled him with a sense of power and importance that he had never experienced before during his solitary hours on the mountainside with only sheep for company.
Page 3
Several days later, when his boredom returned and the temporary excitement had faded from memory, Marcus decided to repeat his successful experiment with an even louder and more convincing performance. "Wolf! Wolf!" he screamed with theatrical desperation, "A huge wolf is attacking the sheep! Please help me immediately! I cannot fight him alone!" His cries echoed across the valley with remarkable authenticity and urgency.
Page 4
Once again, the dedicated villagers responded to his call with the same swift efficiency and selfless concern they had demonstrated before, though some noticed that his previous false alarm had planted seeds of doubt in their minds. Despite their growing skepticism, they still felt obligated to respond to what might be a genuine emergency, understanding that the consequences of ignoring a real wolf attack would be catastrophic for their entire community.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. remorseful - feeling sorry and guilty about something you have done
2. skepticism - doubt about whether something is true or real
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

	•	They were annoyed but responded with patience and a gentle warning
	•	They thought it was funny and laughed
2. How did Marcus feel about the response to his first false alarm?
	•	Ashamed and sorry
	•	Energized and excited by the sense of power
	•	Indifferent and bored

Chapter 5: Trust Eroded
Page 1
When the villagers discovered that Marcus had deceived them for a second time, their patience and understanding began to transform into frustration and disappointment. They realized that his behavior was not simply the innocent mistake of an inexperienced young person, but rather a deliberate pattern of dishonesty that showed complete disregard for the trust they had placed in him and the sacrifice they made by interrupting their important daily work to respond to his calls.
Page 2
"This young shepherd is making fools of us all," muttered some of the villagers as they trudged wearily back down the mountain path, their energy depleted and their valuable time wasted on what had proven to be another elaborate hoax. "How can we trust anything he says in the future? What if there really is an emergency someday, but we assume it's just another one of his thoughtless pranks?"
Page 3
The foundation of trust that had taken years to establish between Marcus and his community was beginning to crack and crumble under the weight of his repeated deceptions. The villagers began to question not only his reliability as a shepherd, but also his character as a member of their close-knit community where mutual dependence and honest communication were essential for everyone's survival and prosperity.
Page 4
Marcus, still failing to understand the serious consequences of his actions, continued to view his successful deceptions as amusing entertainment rather than recognizing them as harmful betrayals of his neighbors' goodwill. He remained completely unaware that his reputation for truthfulness and dependability was being systematically destroyed with each false alarm, and that the villagers were beginning to lose faith in his ability to fulfill his crucial responsibilities as their shepherd.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. systematic - done in a planned, organized way
2. crucial - extremely important; essential
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions

	•	Their patience transformed into frustration and disappointment
	•	They thought it was getting funnier
2. What was happening to the trust between Marcus and the community?
	•	It was growing stronger
	•	It was beginning to crack and crumble
	•	It remained exactly the same

Chapter 6: The Real Wolf Arrives
Page 1
Several weeks passed quietly on the mountainside, with Marcus continuing his daily routine of shepherding while the villagers went about their business with growing reluctance to respond to any future alarms from the boy who had proven himself unreliable. Then, on one particularly peaceful afternoon when the sheep were grazing contentedly and Marcus was daydreaming in the warm sunshine, a large gray shadow emerged silently from the dense forest that bordered the high pasture.
Page 2
A genuine wolf, larger and more menacing than any Marcus had ever imagined in his worst nightmares, stepped deliberately into the open meadow with predatory intent clearly visible in his yellow eyes and bared fangs. This was not a figment of imagination or a character in one of his entertaining deceptions, but a real and immediate danger that threatened not only the valuable sheep under his protection, but potentially his own life as well.
Page 3
Terror gripped Marcus as he watched the fierce predator begin stalking toward the unsuspecting flock with calculated precision and deadly purpose. "Wolf! Wolf!" he screamed with genuine panic and desperate urgency, his voice cracking with authentic fear as he realized the catastrophic situation that was unfolding before his eyes. "Please help me! This time it's real! A wolf is actually attacking the sheep!"
Page 4
But down in the village, the people who heard his frantic cries simply shook their heads with weary resignation and continued with their daily activities. "There goes Marcus again with another one of his silly pranks," they said to one another dismissively. "We're not going to be fooled a third time by that dishonest boy." They had no way of knowing that this time, his desperate pleas for help were completely genuine, and that both he and the sheep were in mortal danger from a very real predator.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. menacing - appearing dangerous and threatening
2. resignation - accepting something unpleasant without protest
