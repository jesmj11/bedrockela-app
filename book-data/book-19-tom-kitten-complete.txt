Chapter 1: Getting Dressed for Company
Page 1
Once upon a time there were three little kittens named Mittens, Tom Kitten, and Moppet. They lived with their mother, Mrs. Tabitha Twitchit, in a lovely house with a beautiful garden. Mrs. Tabitha was always very particular about keeping her home neat and tidy.
Page 2
One morning, Mrs. Tabitha Twitchit was expecting important visitors for tea. She wanted everything to be absolutely perfect for their arrival. The house was spotless, the tea was prepared, and delicious cakes were cooling on the windowsill. But there was still one important task remaining.
Page 3
"Children!" called Mrs. Tabitha sternly. "Come here immediately! You must be washed and dressed properly before our guests arrive. I will not have you looking untidy when the ladies come to visit us for afternoon tea." The three little kittens groaned softly but obeyed their mother.
Page 4
Mrs. Tabitha fetched warm water and soap, and began the lengthy process of washing three squirming kittens. She scrubbed their faces carefully, cleaned behind their ears thoroughly, and combed their fur until it was smooth and fluffy. Tom Kitten, in particular, did not enjoy this process and kept trying to escape.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. particular - very careful and exact about details; wanting everything to be just right
2. lengthy - taking a long time to complete

Chapter 2: Elegant Clothes for Little Kittens
Page 1
After the bath, Mrs. Tabitha brought out the kittens' finest clothing. For Mittens and Moppet, who were well-behaved young lady kittens, she had lovely blue dresses with crisp white pinafores and matching ribbons. The dresses fit perfectly and looked absolutely charming on the two sisters.
Page 2
But for Tom Kitten, Mrs. Tabitha had chosen a very special outfit indeed. She had elegant blue trousers with brass buttons, a sophisticated white shirt with tiny pearl buttons, and a smart little jacket to complete the ensemble. Tom had never worn such fancy clothes before in his entire life.
Page 3
However, there was one significant problem with Tom's outfit. Tom Kitten had been eating quite well recently and had grown considerably rounder than when his clothes were first made. The buttons strained dangerously across his plump little tummy, and the sleeves were uncomfortably tight around his arms.
Page 4
Mrs. Tabitha tugged and pulled at the garments, trying desperately to make them fit properly. "Goodness me, Tom!" she exclaimed in frustration. "You have certainly grown since these were made! But they will simply have to do for today's important occasion. Please try to sit very still and be extremely careful."
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. sophisticated - elegant and fashionable; very fancy
2. considerably - by a large amount; much more

Chapter 3: An Uncomfortable Situation
Page 1
Once the three kittens were properly dressed, Mrs. Tabitha lined them up for inspection. Mittens and Moppet looked absolutely adorable in their matching outfits, standing gracefully with their paws folded politely. But Tom Kitten was already experiencing serious difficulties with his restrictive clothing and could barely move comfortably.
Page 2
"Now listen carefully, my dears," instructed Mrs. Tabitha in her most serious voice. "You must walk slowly and carefully. Do not run, do not jump, and absolutely do not play rough games. Most importantly, you must keep your clothes clean and tidy until after our guests have departed." The kittens nodded solemnly.
Page 3
Mrs. Tabitha sent the three elegantly dressed kittens outside to the garden to wait for the visitors' arrival. "Stay on the path," she called after them. "And remember to behave like proper little ladies and gentlemen!" Then she hurried back inside to make final preparations for the important tea party.
Page 4
At first, the three kittens tried very hard to follow their mother's strict instructions. They walked sedately along the garden path, admiring the colorful flowers and avoiding anything that might soil their beautiful clothes. But Tom Kitten found it increasingly difficult to move in his tight-fitting outfit, and he was growing quite uncomfortable indeed.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. restrictive - limiting movement; too tight to move freely
2. sedately - in a calm, slow, and dignified manner
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions

	•	Go outside
2. How was Tom feeling in his tight clothes?

	•	Perfectly fine

Chapter 4: The Clothes Begin to Come Apart
Page 1
As the kittens continued walking through the garden, Tom's discomfort grew worse and worse. The tight clothes were making it almost impossible for him to move naturally. When he tried to climb over a small garden wall to follow his sisters, disaster struck. His jacket split right down the back with a loud ripping sound!
Page 2
Mittens and Moppet gasped in horror as they watched their brother's beautiful jacket tear apart. "Oh no, Tom!" whispered Moppet anxiously. "Mother will be absolutely furious when she sees what has happened to your lovely clothes!" But unfortunately for poor Tom, this was only the beginning of his wardrobe catastrophe.
Page 3
Next, several brass buttons popped off Tom's trousers and went rolling across the garden path. Then the seams of his shirt began to strain and pull apart. With every movement Tom made, his clothes became more and more damaged. The elegant outfit was rapidly transforming into a collection of torn fabric and scattered buttons.
Page 4
Poor Tom Kitten stood in the middle of the garden path looking absolutely miserable. His beautiful clothes hung in tatters around him, and he felt terribly embarrassed. "What am I going to do?" he asked his sisters desperately. "Mother will be so disappointed when she sees me looking like this before the important visitors arrive!"
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. catastrophe - a sudden disaster; something that goes very wrong
2. tatters - torn pieces; badly ripped fabric
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

Chapter 5: An Unexpected Solution
Page 1
Just as Tom was wondering how he could possibly face his mother in such a disheveled state, three unusual visitors appeared in the garden. They were the Puddle-Duck family - Jemima, Rebeccah, and their cousin who lived nearby. The ducks waddled importantly along the path, examining the scattered buttons with great curiosity.
Page 2
"My goodness!" exclaimed Jemima Puddle-Duck when she noticed Tom's predicament. "What lovely clothes these are! Though they do seem to be rather ill-fitting for a kitten." She picked up one of the brass buttons and admired it carefully. "These would look absolutely splendid on us ducks, don't you think, sisters?"
Page 3
The three ducks quickly came to an agreement with poor Tom Kitten. They would take his torn clothes and wear them proudly around the farmyard, while Tom could return home in his natural fur coat. "This arrangement will suit everyone perfectly!" declared Rebeccah as they gathered up the jacket, trousers, and remaining buttons.
Page 4
Within minutes, the three Puddle-Ducks had dressed themselves in Tom's elegant outfit. The clothes fit them much better than they had fit Tom, and they looked quite distinguished waddling about the garden. They thanked Tom politely for his generous contribution to their wardrobe and waddled away proudly, leaving Tom feeling greatly relieved.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. disheveled - messy and untidy; not neat in appearance
2. distinguished - having an elegant and important appearance

Chapter 6: Facing the Consequences
Page 1
When Tom Kitten returned home in his natural fur, Mrs. Tabitha was initially shocked and then quite cross indeed. "Where are your beautiful clothes, Tom?" she demanded sternly. Tom explained about the tight fit and the helpful ducks, but his mother was not particularly sympathetic to his explanation about the unfortunate incident.
Page 2
As punishment for losing his elegant outfit, Mrs. Tabitha decided that Tom would not be allowed to join the tea party with the important visitors. Instead, he would spend the afternoon upstairs in his bedroom, thinking about the importance of taking proper care of one's belongings and following mother's instructions carefully.
Page 3
Meanwhile, Mittens and Moppet, who had managed to keep their dresses clean and tidy throughout the entire adventure, were allowed to join their mother for the elegant tea party. They served delicate cakes and poured tea for the distinguished lady visitors, practicing their very best manners and polite conversation skills.
Page 4
From his bedroom window, Tom Kitten could see the three Puddle-Ducks still wearing his torn clothes, parading around the pond looking very pleased with themselves. Though he was sad to miss the tea party, Tom had to admit he felt much more comfortable in his own soft fur than in those terribly tight, restrictive garments that had caused him so much trouble.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. sympathetic - understanding and caring about someone's problems
2. parading - walking around proudly to show off
