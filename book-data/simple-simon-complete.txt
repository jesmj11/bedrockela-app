Chapter 1: Simple Simon's Character
Page 1
In a cheerful village where everyone knew everyone else's business and neighbors looked out for one another with genuine care and concern, there lived a young man named Simple Simon whose approach to life was characterized by an extraordinary combination of innocent optimism and remarkable naivety that often led him into amusing predicaments but also endeared him to all the townspeople who appreciated his kind heart and genuine desire to be helpful despite his frequent misunderstandings of how the world actually worked.
Page 2
Simon's defining characteristic was his tendency to approach every situation with complete sincerity and trust, believing that all people were fundamentally good and that every interaction would result in positive outcomes if he simply maintained his friendly attitude and willingness to please others. This admirable but sometimes impractical worldview often placed him at a disadvantage when dealing with individuals who might take advantage of his trusting nature or who lacked his generous spirit and honest intentions.
Page 3
Despite his frequent mistakes and occasional embarrassing misadventures that resulted from his inability to recognize social cues or understand complex situations, Simon possessed an infectious enthusiasm for life that brought joy and laughter to those around him, even when his well-intentioned efforts produced unexpected or amusing results. His neighbors had learned to appreciate his unique perspective on the world and often found that his unconventional approaches to problems sometimes revealed creative solutions that more conventional thinkers might overlook.
Page 4
The villagers understood that Simon's simplicity was not a limitation but rather a different way of experiencing the world that emphasized kindness, honesty, and genuine human connection over the complicated social maneuvering and calculated self-interest that sometimes characterized more sophisticated individuals. His presence in their community served as a gentle reminder that life's greatest pleasures often come from basic human virtues rather than clever schemes or complicated strategies for personal advancement.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. naivety - lack of experience or wisdom; being too trusting
2. predicaments - difficult or unpleasant situations
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions

	•	His tendency to approach situations with complete sincerity and trust
	•	His ability to solve complex problems
2. How did the villagers view Simon's simplicity?
	•	As a serious problem that needed fixing
	•	As a different way of experiencing the world that emphasized kindness and honesty
	•	As something to be ignored completely

Chapter 2: The Encounter with the Pieman
Page 1
One bright morning as Simon wandered through the village market with his characteristic cheerful curiosity and genuine interest in all the fascinating activities taking place around him, he encountered a traveling pieman who had set up his colorful cart in the town square and was calling out enthusiastic advertisements for his delicious wares to attract customers with promises of the most wonderful pastries and savory treats that any reasonable person could possibly imagine wanting to purchase and enjoy.
Page 2
The appetizing aromas wafting from the pieman's cart filled Simon with genuine desire to sample these tempting delicacies, but unfortunately he discovered that his pockets contained no money whatsoever to make such a purchase possible, creating a disappointing obstacle to his spontaneous culinary desires. However, rather than simply accepting this limitation and walking away like most people might do when faced with insufficient funds, Simon decided to approach the situation with his characteristic optimism and direct honesty.
Page 3
"Good pieman," Simon said with his typical straightforward manner and genuine friendliness, "I would very much like to buy one of your pies, but I must confess that I have no money to pay for such a purchase. However, I am perfectly willing to work for you or provide some service that might earn me the right to enjoy one of your delicious creations, if you would be so kind as to consider such an arrangement."
Page 4
The pieman, who had dealt with countless customers throughout his years of traveling from village to village and had developed considerable experience in recognizing various types of potential buyers, looked at Simon with a mixture of amusement and skepticism that suggested he was not entirely convinced that this earnest young man possessed either the resources or the practical skills necessary to provide adequate compensation for valuable merchandise, but he was curious enough about Simon's unusual approach to consider what might develop from this unexpected interaction.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. wafting - moving gently through the air
2. skepticism - doubt about the truth of something

	•	The pieman's cart was closed

	•	He offered to work or provide service in exchange for a pie

Chapter 3: The Pieman's Challenge
Page 1
After considering Simon's unusual request with the kind of careful deliberation that experienced merchants typically employ when evaluating potentially risky but interesting business propositions, the pieman decided that this encounter might provide him with both entertainment and practical assistance, though he suspected that the entertainment value would likely exceed the practical benefits given Simon's obviously limited understanding of commercial transactions and marketplace dynamics that governed such exchanges.
Page 2
"Very well, young Simon," the pieman replied with a sly grin that suggested he was planning something that would test both Simon's determination and his ability to understand the complexities of adult negotiations, "I will indeed give you a pie, but first you must demonstrate that you can meet my simple requirement, which should be easily accomplished by anyone who possesses even basic intelligence and practical problem-solving abilities."
Page 3
The challenge that the pieman presented was both deceptively simple in its basic concept and surprisingly complex in its practical execution, requiring Simon to demonstrate not only his willingness to work but also his capacity to think creatively and adapt to unexpected obstacles that might arise during the completion of seemingly straightforward tasks. The pieman's experience with various customers had taught him that such challenges often revealed important information about people's character and capabilities.
Page 4
"You must bring me a bucket of water from the village well," the pieman explained with elaborate formality that suggested this was a test of considerable importance, "but you must carry it here without using your hands to hold the bucket handle, and you must not spill a single drop along the way. If you can accomplish this feat successfully, then you shall have the finest pie in my entire cart as your well-deserved reward."
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. deliberation - long and careful consideration
2. deceptively - in a way that gives a false impression
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions

	•	To give him a pie immediately
2. What was the pieman's challenge?
	•	To count all the pies in the cart
	•	To bring water from the well without using hands and without spilling
	•	To sing a song for all the customers

Chapter 4: Simon's Creative Solution
Page 1
Upon hearing the pieman's seemingly impossible challenge, Simon experienced neither discouragement nor frustration but rather the kind of excited curiosity that characterized his approach to all of life's interesting puzzles and unexpected opportunities for creative problem-solving. His unique perspective on the world often allowed him to discover unconventional solutions that more conventional thinkers might overlook because they were too focused on traditional methods and established procedures that seemed obvious but were not necessarily the only viable approaches.
Page 2
After considering the specific requirements of the challenge with the kind of careful attention to detail that he applied to all important tasks, Simon began to understand that the key to success lay not in finding ways to circumvent the pieman's restrictions, but rather in discovering creative interpretations of the rules that would satisfy the literal requirements while achieving the desired outcome through innovative methods that demonstrated both ingenuity and respect for the spirit of the agreement.
Page 3
Simon's solution involved utilizing parts of his body other than his hands to manipulate the bucket, combined with careful planning of his route and movement techniques that would minimize the risk of spilling while maintaining complete adherence to the pieman's specific prohibition against using hands to hold the bucket handle. His approach demonstrated that apparent limitations could often be transformed into opportunities for creativity and innovation when viewed from the proper perspective.
Page 4
With considerable determination and remarkable ingenuity, Simon successfully transported the bucket of water from the village well back to the pieman's cart without violating any of the specified conditions, using a combination of his arms, shoulders, and careful balancing techniques that allowed him to maintain complete control over the bucket's position and movement while keeping his hands entirely free from contact with the handle, thus satisfying the literal requirements of the challenge through creative interpretation and skillful execution.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. circumvent - find a way around an obstacle
2. ingenuity - cleverness and inventiveness
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions

	•	By giving up immediately
2. How did Simon solve the challenge?
	•	By breaking the rules secretly
	•	By using his arms and shoulders with careful balancing techniques
	•	By asking someone else to help him

Chapter 5: Recognition and Reward
Page 1
The pieman watched Simon's innovative approach to the water-carrying challenge with growing amazement and genuine respect, gradually recognizing that what he had initially perceived as simple naivety was actually a different kind of intelligence that emphasized creative problem-solving and literal interpretation of instructions over conventional approaches that relied on standard methods and predictable strategies. Simon's success demonstrated that alternative perspectives could lead to effective solutions that more traditional thinkers might never consider.
Page 2
When Simon arrived back at the cart with the bucket of water completely intact and not a single drop spilled despite the complex maneuvering required by his unconventional transportation method, the pieman realized that he had seriously underestimated both Simon's determination and his capacity for innovative thinking. The successful completion of what seemed like an impossible task revealed that Simon possessed valuable qualities that were not immediately apparent to casual observers.
Page 3
"Well done, Simon!" the pieman exclaimed with genuine admiration and newfound respect for the young man whose simple manner had initially led him to expect failure rather than success. "You have not only met the specific requirements of my challenge, but you have also demonstrated the kind of creative thinking and persistent effort that I truly appreciate. As promised, you have earned the finest pie in my entire collection."
Page 4
The pieman selected his most delicious and beautifully crafted pie and presented it to Simon with ceremonial flourish that acknowledged both the successful completion of the challenge and the valuable lesson that the encounter had provided about the importance of not judging people's capabilities based solely on first impressions or conventional expectations. Simon's achievement had transformed a potentially exploitative situation into a genuine exchange of value and mutual respect between two very different individuals.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. maneuvering - moving skillfully or carefully
2. exploitative - taking advantage of others unfairly
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions

	•	He became more suspicious of Simon
2. What did Simon's achievement transform the situation into?
	•	A complete disaster
	•	A genuine exchange of value and mutual respect
	•	An argument between the two men

Chapter 6: The Value of Different Kinds of Intelligence
Page 1
As Simon enjoyed his well-earned pie with the kind of genuine satisfaction that comes from achieving success through honest effort and creative problem-solving, he reflected on the interesting encounter with the pieman and the valuable lessons it had provided about the importance of persistence, innovation, and maintaining a positive attitude even when faced with seemingly impossible challenges that might initially appear to be beyond one's capabilities or understanding.
Page 2
The pieman, meanwhile, had gained important insights about the dangers of making hasty judgments about people's abilities based on superficial observations or conventional expectations that might not accurately reflect their true potential for creative thinking and successful problem-solving. Simon's unexpected success had challenged his assumptions and reminded him that intelligence manifests itself in many different forms, some of which might not be immediately obvious to casual observers.
Page 3
The encounter between Simon and the pieman became a popular story throughout the village, serving as a gentle reminder to all residents about the importance of appreciating different types of intelligence and recognizing that apparent simplicity might actually represent a valuable alternative approach to understanding and interacting with the world that emphasizes honesty, creativity, and genuine human connection over complicated strategies and sophisticated manipulation techniques that sometimes characterize more conventional social interactions.
Page 4
Simon continued his journey through life with the same cheerful optimism and innovative problem-solving approach that had served him well in his encounter with the pieman, understanding that his unique perspective on the world was not a limitation to be overcome but rather a valuable asset to be cherished and shared with others who might benefit from learning alternative approaches to life's challenges and opportunities for growth, connection, and mutual understanding.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. superficial - existing or occurring at the surface; not thorough or deep
2. manifests - shows or displays clearly
