Chapter 1: A Father's Growing Concern
Page 1
In a prosperous farming community nestled in a fertile valley, there lived an elderly farmer named Thomas who had devoted his entire life to building a successful agricultural operation that would provide security and opportunity for his beloved family. Through decades of hard work, careful planning, and wise decision-making, he had accumulated substantial wealth, extensive landholdings, and a reputation for integrity that made him one of the most respected members of his community.
Page 2
Thomas was blessed with five strong sons, each possessing valuable skills and abilities that could contribute significantly to the family's continued prosperity and success. However, as the years passed and his sons grew into adulthood, he began to notice a troubling pattern of behavior that filled him with increasing worry and disappointment about the future stability of everything he had worked so tirelessly to create for their benefit.
Page 3
Rather than working together harmoniously as a unified family team, his sons constantly engaged in petty arguments, jealous competition, and bitter disputes over trivial matters that should have been easily resolved through communication and compromise. They seemed incapable of cooperating on even the simplest tasks, preferring instead to pursue their individual interests without regard for how their selfish behavior affected the overall welfare of their family enterprise.
Page 4
The wise old farmer recognized that this divisive attitude among his sons represented a serious threat to the future of everything he had built, since their inability to work together would inevitably lead to the destruction of the family business and the loss of their collective inheritance. He understood that without unity and cooperation, even the strongest foundation could crumble, and he knew he needed to find a way to teach his sons this crucial lesson before it was too late.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. accumulated - gathered or collected over time
2. divisive - causing disagreement and conflict between people

Chapter 2: The Sons' Constant Conflicts
Page 1
The five brothers - William, James, Samuel, Benjamin, and Michael - had developed such an intense pattern of rivalry and competition that they could barely accomplish any meaningful work together without descending into heated arguments and destructive conflicts. Each brother believed himself to be superior to the others in intelligence, strength, or skill, and none was willing to compromise or accept guidance from his siblings, creating a toxic atmosphere of mistrust and hostility.
Page 2
William, the eldest, insisted that his age and experience gave him the natural right to make all important decisions for the family, while James argued that his superior education and knowledge of modern farming techniques made him the most qualified leader. Samuel claimed that his exceptional physical strength made him indispensable, Benjamin boasted about his talent for negotiating profitable business deals, and Michael maintained that his innovative ideas were essential for future success.
Page 3
These constant power struggles and ego conflicts manifested themselves in numerous ways that actively undermined the efficiency and profitability of their family's agricultural operations. When working in the fields, they would compete to see who could work fastest, often damaging crops or equipment in their haste to outperform each other. During harvest time, they argued about the best methods to use, wasting precious time while their crops waited to be gathered.
Page 4
Their inability to present a united front also made them vulnerable to exploitation by outside business partners who quickly learned to manipulate the brothers' competitive nature for their own advantage. Merchants would play one brother against another to secure better deals, knowing that the lack of communication and cooperation among the siblings made it impossible for them to negotiate effectively as a unified family unit.
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. manifested - showed or displayed clearly
2. exploitation - taking unfair advantage of someone

Chapter 3: A Father's Intervention
Page 1
As Thomas observed his sons' destructive behavior continuing to escalate despite his repeated attempts to reason with them through conventional conversation and gentle persuasion, he realized that more dramatic action was necessary to capture their attention and communicate the vital importance of unity and cooperation. He knew that simply lecturing them about the value of working together would be ineffective, since they had already demonstrated their unwillingness to listen to his verbal advice and warnings.
Page 2
After much careful thought and consideration, the wise farmer decided to devise a practical demonstration that would illustrate his message in a way that his sons could not ignore or dismiss. He spent several days quietly preparing his lesson, gathering materials and planning exactly how he would present his important message about the power of unity versus the weakness of division. He understood that sometimes the most profound truths are best communicated through actions rather than words.
Page 3
One evening, when all five of his sons had gathered in the family's main living area after completing their individual daily tasks, Thomas announced that he wanted to speak with them about a matter of great importance to their family's future. His serious tone and solemn expression immediately captured their attention, since they rarely saw their father display such gravity and formality during their usual casual family interactions.
Page 4
"My sons," Thomas began with quiet dignity and unmistakable authority in his voice, "I have watched with growing concern as your conflicts and competition have threatened to destroy everything our family has built together over many generations. Tonight, I want to show you something that I hope will help you understand why your current path leads only to weakness, vulnerability, and ultimate failure, while a different approach could ensure our continued prosperity and strength."
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. conventional - traditional or usual way of doing something
2. formality - serious, official behavior

Chapter 4: The Demonstration Begins
Page 1
From beneath his chair, Thomas produced a carefully prepared bundle of five sturdy wooden sticks that he had selected and bound together with strong rope earlier that day. Each individual stick was straight, solid, and approximately the same length and thickness as the others, representing in his mind the five sons who sat before him with expressions of curiosity mixed with skepticism about their father's unusual behavior and mysterious demonstration.
Page 2
"William," Thomas said, addressing his eldest son with calm authority, "you have always prided yourself on being the strongest member of our family. I want you to take this bundle of sticks and break it in half using only your hands and your considerable physical strength." He extended the tightly bound bundle toward his son, who accepted the challenge with confidence, certain that his father had chosen an easy task to demonstrate some simple point.
Page 3
William grasped the bundle firmly in his strong hands and applied significant pressure, expecting the wood to snap easily under his grip. However, despite his powerful muscles and determined effort, the tightly bound sticks remained completely intact, refusing to break or even bend under the considerable force he was applying. His face grew red with exertion as he twisted and strained, but the bundle maintained its structural integrity without showing any signs of weakness or damage.
Page 4
After several minutes of unsuccessful attempts, William finally admitted defeat and passed the stubborn bundle to James, who was equally confident in his ability to accomplish what his older brother had failed to do. One by one, each of the five sons tried their strength against the mysterious bundle, with Samuel using his impressive physical power, Benjamin applying his problem-solving skills to find the best grip and leverage, and Michael attempting various innovative techniques, but none of them succeeded in breaking the resilient collection of bound sticks.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. integrity - the state of being whole and undamaged
2. resilient - able to withstand difficult conditions

Chapter 5: The Lesson Revealed
Page 1
After watching all five of his sons fail to break the seemingly indestructible bundle despite their combined efforts and individual strengths, Thomas smiled knowingly and carefully untied the rope that held the sticks together. As the binding fell away, the five wooden sticks separated from each other and lay individually in his weathered hands, no longer united in their collective strength but instead existing as isolated, vulnerable pieces of wood.
Page 2
"Now, William," Thomas said with quiet confidence, "take just one of these individual sticks and try to break it." His eldest son accepted the single wooden stick with obvious skepticism, wondering what point his father was attempting to make with this seemingly pointless exercise. However, when William applied pressure to the isolated stick, it snapped in half immediately with a sharp crack that echoed through the room, demonstrating dramatically how easily individual strength could be overcome.
Page 3
Thomas handed the remaining individual sticks to his other sons, and one by one, James, Samuel, Benjamin, and Michael each broke their stick effortlessly, the sound of splintering wood filling the room as each piece succumbed instantly to even minimal pressure. The contrast between the unbreakable bundle and the fragile individual sticks was so stark and obvious that even the most stubborn of the brothers could not fail to understand the powerful symbolism of their father's carefully orchestrated demonstration.
Page 4
"Do you understand what I am trying to teach you, my sons?" Thomas asked gently but seriously, his eyes moving from one face to another as he observed their dawning comprehension of the lesson he had so dramatically illustrated. "When you stand together as a unified family, bound by cooperation and mutual support, you are strong and unbreakable like that bundle. But when you fight among yourselves and pursue individual interests at the expense of family unity, you become weak and vulnerable like these broken sticks."
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. succumbed - gave way to pressure; was defeated
2. orchestrated - carefully planned and organized

	•	They broke effortlessly with minimal pressure

Chapter 6: Unity Restored
Page 1
The profound impact of their father's simple yet powerful demonstration was immediately apparent in the changed expressions and body language of all five sons, who sat in thoughtful silence as they fully absorbed the wisdom of the lesson they had just witnessed. The brilliant symbolism of the bundled sticks had communicated more effectively in a few minutes than years of lectures and arguments could have accomplished, penetrating their stubborn pride and competitive spirits with undeniable truth about the consequences of division.
Page 2
William was the first to speak, his voice filled with genuine remorse and newfound understanding as he addressed both his father and his brothers. "Father, I now realize how foolish we have been, allowing our pride and individual ambitions to weaken our family when we could have been stronger together. I want to apologize to all of my brothers for my arrogance and competitiveness, and I pledge to work with them cooperatively from this day forward rather than against them."
Page 3
One by one, the other brothers followed William's example, each offering sincere apologies for their past behavior and making commitments to prioritize family unity over personal advancement. James acknowledged that his education meant nothing if it didn't serve the family's collective interests, Samuel pledged to use his strength to support rather than dominate his siblings, Benjamin promised to negotiate for the entire family's benefit, and Michael committed to sharing his innovative ideas rather than using them for personal gain.
Page 4
From that transformative evening forward, the five brothers worked together with unprecedented harmony and effectiveness, combining their individual talents and strengths to create a unified force that was far more powerful and successful than any of them could have been alone. Their father's agricultural business flourished as never before, and their reputation for integrity and reliability made them the most respected and prosperous family in their entire region. The simple lesson of the bundled sticks had saved not only their family legacy, but had also taught them the timeless truth that unity creates strength while division leads inevitably to weakness and failure.
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. penetrating - getting through to; having a deep effect
2. unprecedented - never done or known before; extraordinary
