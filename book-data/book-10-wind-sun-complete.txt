Chapter 1: A Boastful Argument
Page 1
High above the earth, where clouds drifted lazily across the endless blue sky, two of nature's most powerful forces were engaged in a heated argument about which of them possessed the greater strength and influence over the world below. The North Wind, fierce and proud, believed that his ability to howl and rage and move mountains made him the strongest force in all of creation.
Page 2
"I am clearly the most powerful!" declared the Wind with a voice like thunder rolling across the heavens. "When I blow with all my might, I can uproot the strongest trees, tear the roofs from houses, and send ships racing across the ocean waves. Nothing can stand against my fury when I choose to unleash my full strength upon the world!"
Page 3
The Sun, warm and bright but equally confident in his own abilities, smiled down from his position high in the sky and responded with gentle but firm conviction. "My dear Wind, while your strength is certainly impressive, I believe my power runs much deeper than yours. I give life to every growing thing, I warm the earth and seas, and I bring light to chase away the darkness. Without me, the world would be cold, dark, and lifeless."
Page 4
This debate had been going on for quite some time, with neither the Wind nor the Sun willing to admit that the other might possibly be stronger. Each felt absolutely certain that his own powers were superior, and both were growing frustrated with their inability to convince the other of what seemed like an obvious truth. Clearly, they needed some way to settle this dispute once and for all.
Chapter 1 Vocabulary
1. conviction - a strong belief or opinion
2. superior - better or greater than others
Chapter 1 Comprehension Questions

	•	Which of them was stronger and more powerful
	•	Who could make more noise
2. How did the Wind show his power?
	•	By hiding behind clouds
	•	By uprooting trees, tearing roofs, and moving ships
	•	By making gentle breezes

Chapter 2: A Challenge Proposed
Page 1
As their argument continued to rage without resolution, the Sun suddenly had an idea that would allow them to test their powers in a practical way rather than simply boasting about their abilities. "Instead of arguing endlessly," he suggested thoughtfully, "why don't we have a contest that will clearly demonstrate which of us is truly more powerful?"
Page 2
The Wind, eager to prove his superiority once and for all, immediately agreed to this proposal. "Excellent idea!" he roared with enthusiasm. "What kind of contest did you have in mind? Perhaps we could see who can create the biggest storm, or who can move the heaviest objects, or who can make the loudest noise? I'm confident that any test of raw power will prove that I am the stronger!"
Page 3
The Sun pointed down toward the earth below, where a lone traveler was walking along a dusty country road. The man was wearing a heavy cloak wrapped tightly around his shoulders to protect him from the cool morning air. "Do you see that traveler down there?" asked the Sun. "Let's see which of us can make him remove his cloak. The one who succeeds will be declared the winner of our contest."
Page 4
This seemed like a perfect test to the Wind, who was already imagining how easily he would accomplish this task. "That's far too simple!" he laughed confidently. "I'll have that cloak off his shoulders in no time at all. My powerful gusts will tear it right off his back before he even knows what's happening. You might as well admit defeat right now and save us both some time!"
Chapter 2 Vocabulary
1. resolution - the solving of a problem or ending of a conflict
2. superiority - the state of being better or greater than others
Chapter 2 Comprehension Questions
1. What did the Sun suggest to settle their argument?
	•	To continue arguing forever
	•	To have a contest that would demonstrate their power
	•	To ask other forces of nature to judge
2. What was the specific challenge they agreed upon?
	•	To see who could create the biggest storm
	•	To see who could make a traveler remove his cloak
	•	To see who could move the heaviest object

Chapter 3: The Wind's Attempt
Page 1
The Sun graciously allowed the Wind to try first, stepping back and dimming his light slightly so that his competitor would have the best possible chance to succeed. The Wind gathered all his strength and prepared to unleash the full fury of his power upon the unsuspecting traveler walking peacefully along the road below, completely unaware that he was about to become the focus of a cosmic competition.
Page 2
With a tremendous roar that echoed across the countryside, the Wind began to blow with all his might. He sent fierce gusts racing down from the sky, howling and shrieking as they struck the traveler with tremendous force. The man's hair whipped wildly around his face, dust swirled up from the road in blinding clouds, and nearby trees bent almost to the ground under the assault of the powerful gale.
Page 3
But instead of removing his cloak as the Wind had confidently expected, the traveler did exactly the opposite! The fierce, cold gusts made him feel chilled to the bone, so he pulled his cloak even more tightly around his body, wrapping it securely and holding it closed with both hands to prevent the bitter wind from reaching his skin and making him even colder.
Page 4
The harder the Wind blew, the more tightly the man clutched his protective cloak around himself. No matter how the Wind raged and howled, no matter how he sent leaves and debris flying through the air, the traveler only hunched his shoulders and gripped his garment more firmly, determined to stay warm against the increasingly violent and uncomfortable weather that seemed to be attacking him from all directions.
Chapter 3 Vocabulary
1. cosmic - relating to the universe or outer space; very large
2. assault - a violent attack
Chapter 3 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the traveler respond to the Wind's powerful gusts?
	•	He immediately removed his cloak
	•	He pulled his cloak more tightly around himself
	•	He ran away in fear
2. What happened as the Wind blew harder and harder?
	•	The man became warmer
	•	The man clutched his cloak even more tightly
	•	The man started dancing

Chapter 4: Growing Frustration
Page 1
The Wind became increasingly frustrated as his efforts produced exactly the opposite effect from what he had intended. He had been so confident that his raw power would easily accomplish the task, but now he found himself in the embarrassing position of actually making the traveler more determined to keep his cloak on rather than persuading him to remove it.
Page 2
"This is impossible!" the Wind roared in anger and confusion. "I'm using all my strength, creating the most powerful storm I can manage, and yet that stubborn traveler refuses to cooperate! Perhaps if I blow even harder and create an even more violent tempest, I can force him to give up his cloak!"
Page 3
So the Wind redoubled his efforts, summoning every bit of his considerable power and unleashing it upon the poor traveler with even greater force than before. The storm became so violent that small trees were uprooted, stones were lifted from the ground, and the very air seemed to scream with the intensity of the gale. But still, the man only wrapped his cloak more securely around his body.
Page 4
After what felt like hours of furious effort, the Wind finally exhausted himself and could blow no more. He was tired, frustrated, and completely defeated by a simple human being who had done nothing more than hold onto his clothing. The traveler, though battered by the storm, was still firmly wrapped in his cloak and showed no signs of removing it voluntarily.
Chapter 4 Vocabulary
1. tempest - a violent storm with strong winds
2. redoubled - greatly increased; made much stronger
Chapter 4 Comprehension Questions
1. How did the Wind feel about his lack of success?
	•	Happy and satisfied
	•	Increasingly frustrated and angry
	•	Completely calm
2. What happened when the Wind used even more power?
	•	The traveler finally removed his cloak
	•	The man wrapped his cloak even more securely
	•	The storm stopped completely

Chapter 5: The Sun's Gentle Approach
Page 1
Now it was the Sun's turn to try his approach to the challenge. While the Wind rested and recovered from his exhausting efforts, the Sun began to shine down upon the traveler with gentle warmth. Instead of using force and violence like his competitor, the Sun chose a completely different strategy that relied on kindness and comfort rather than power and intimidation.
Page 2
The Sun's warm rays gradually chased away the chill that the Wind's fierce storm had created, replacing the cold, harsh weather with pleasant, comfortable heat that felt wonderful after the traveler's ordeal. The man began to relax his tight grip on his cloak as the air around him grew warmer and more pleasant, making his heavy garment feel less necessary for protection against the elements.
Page 3
As the Sun continued to beam down with steady, gentle warmth, the traveler began to feel quite comfortable indeed. The peaceful heat made him feel drowsy and content, a perfect contrast to the violent, frightening experience he had just endured during the Wind's attack. For the first time since the storm began, the man was able to continue his journey in peace and comfort.
Page 4
The Sun gradually increased his warmth, but not in the aggressive, overwhelming way that the Wind had used his power. Instead, the heat grew slowly and steadily, like a gentle embrace that made the traveler feel more and more comfortable with each passing moment. Soon, the heavy cloak that had been so essential during the cold, windy storm began to feel unnecessarily warm and bulky.
Chapter 5 Vocabulary
1. strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a goal
2. ordeal - a painful or difficult experience
Chapter 5 Comprehension Questions
1. How was the Sun's approach different from the Wind's?
	•	The Sun used even more force
	•	The Sun used gentle warmth instead of force and violence
	•	The Sun gave up immediately
2. How did the traveler respond to the Sun's warmth?
	•	He became angry and upset
	•	He began to relax and feel comfortable
	•	He started running away

Chapter 6: The Power of Gentle Persuasion
Page 1
As the Sun's gentle heat continued to warm the air around him, the traveler began to feel quite hot in his heavy cloak. What had been absolutely necessary protection during the Wind's cold storm was now becoming an uncomfortable burden that made him feel overheated and stuffy. Without any external pressure or force, he naturally began to loosen his grip on the garment.
Page 2
Within just a few minutes of the Sun's pleasant warmth, the traveler made a decision that the Wind's most violent efforts had been unable to achieve. Feeling hot and uncomfortable, he voluntarily removed his cloak and folded it over his arm, grateful to be free of the heavy fabric that was no longer needed in the now-pleasant weather.
Page 3
The Wind watched in amazement and dismay as the Sun accomplished with gentle kindness what all his powerful fury had failed to achieve. The contest was over, and the Sun had clearly won through patience, wisdom, and understanding of human nature rather than through raw force and aggressive intimidation.
Page 4
"You have taught me an important lesson," the Wind admitted humbly to his competitor. "I thought that strength meant using the most force possible, but you have shown me that true power sometimes lies in gentleness and persuasion rather than violence and intimidation. People respond better to kindness than to force, and gentle encouragement can accomplish what harsh demands cannot."
Chapter 6 Vocabulary
1. voluntarily - done by choice, without being forced
2. persuasion - the act of convincing someone through reasoning
Chapter 6 Comprehension Questions

	•	He put on more clothes
	•	He voluntarily removed his cloak
	•	He complained about the weather
2. What lesson did the Wind learn from this contest?
	•	That he should blow even harder
	•	That gentleness and persuasion can be more powerful than force
	•	That contests are unfair
